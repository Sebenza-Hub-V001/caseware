# ISA 300 — Planning an Audit of Financial Statements

## Objective

The objective of the auditor is to plan the audit so that it will be performed in an effective manner. (Para. 4)

## Scope

ISA 300 deals with the auditor's responsibility to plan an audit of financial statements. It covers establishing the overall audit strategy and developing the audit plan. The standard is written in the context of recurring audits, with additional considerations for initial audit engagements separately identified. Planning benefits the audit by helping the auditor devote appropriate attention to important areas, identify and resolve potential problems on a timely basis, and properly organize and manage the engagement.

## Key Requirements

### Involvement of Key Engagement Team Members (Para. 5)
- The engagement partner and other key members of the engagement team shall be involved in planning the audit, including planning and participating in the discussion among engagement team members.

### Preliminary Engagement Activities (Para. 6)
The auditor shall undertake the following at the beginning of the current audit engagement:
- Performing procedures required by ISA 220 (Revised) regarding acceptance and continuance of the client relationship and the specific audit engagement
- Evaluating compliance with relevant ethical requirements, including independence
- Establishing an understanding of the terms of the engagement as required by ISA 210

### Overall Audit Strategy (Para. 7-8)
The auditor shall establish an overall audit strategy that sets the scope, timing and direction of the audit, and that guides the development of the audit plan. In establishing the strategy, the auditor shall:
- Identify the characteristics of the engagement that define its scope
- Ascertain the reporting objectives of the engagement to plan the timing and nature of communications required
- Consider factors significant in directing the engagement team's efforts
- Consider results of preliminary engagement activities
- Ascertain the nature, timing and extent of resources necessary to perform the engagement

### Audit Plan (Para. 9)
The auditor shall develop an audit plan that includes a description of:
- The nature, timing and extent of the planned direction and supervision of engagement team members and the review of their work
- The nature, timing and extent of planned risk assessment procedures (per [[standards/isa-315]])
- The nature, timing and extent of planned further audit procedures at the assertion level (per [[standards/isa-330]])
- Other planned audit procedures required to comply with ISAs

### Updating the Plan (Para. 10-11)
- The auditor shall update and change the overall audit strategy and audit plan as necessary during the course of the audit.
- The engagement partner shall review the overall audit strategy and audit plan.

### Documentation (Para. 12)
The auditor shall include in the audit documentation:
- The overall audit strategy
- The audit plan
- Any significant changes made during the engagement to the overall audit strategy or audit plan, and the reasons for such changes

### Initial Audit Engagements (Para. 13)
The auditor shall undertake the following activities prior to starting an initial audit:
- Performing procedures required by ISA 220 (Revised) regarding acceptance of client relationships
- Communicating with the predecessor auditor, where there has been a change of auditors, in compliance with relevant ethical requirements

## Key Definitions

ISA 300 does not contain a formal definitions section. Key concepts are defined contextually:
- **Overall audit strategy** — Sets the scope, timing and direction of the audit; guides development of the audit plan
- **Audit plan** — More detailed than the strategy; includes descriptions of the nature, timing and extent of planned audit procedures
