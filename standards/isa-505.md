# ISA 505 — External Confirmations

## Objective

The objective of the auditor, when using external confirmation procedures, is to design and perform such procedures to obtain relevant and reliable audit evidence.

## Scope

This ISA deals with the auditor's use of external confirmation procedures to obtain audit evidence in accordance with ISA 330 and ISA 500. It does not address inquiries regarding litigation and claims (dealt with in ISA 501).

## Key Requirements

### External Confirmation Procedures
- The auditor **shall** maintain control over external confirmation requests, including determining information to be confirmed, selecting the confirming party, designing requests with proper addressing and return information, and sending requests with follow-up (para. 7)

### Management's Refusal
- If management refuses to allow confirmation requests, the auditor **shall** inquire as to reasons, evaluate implications on risk assessment (including fraud risk), and perform alternative procedures (para. 8)
- If the refusal is unreasonable or alternative procedures cannot produce sufficient evidence, the auditor **shall** communicate with those charged with governance and determine implications for the audit opinion (para. 9)

### Results of Confirmation Procedures
- The auditor **shall** obtain further evidence if factors give rise to doubts about reliability of responses (para. 10)
- If a response is unreliable, the auditor **shall** evaluate implications on risk assessment and other audit procedures (para. 11)
- For non-responses, the auditor **shall** perform alternative audit procedures (para. 12)
- The auditor **shall** investigate exceptions to determine whether they indicate misstatements (para. 14)

### Negative Confirmations
- Negative confirmations **shall not** be used as the sole substantive procedure unless: risk is assessed as low with effective controls, population is large/homogeneous/small balances, very low exception rate expected, and no factors suggesting disregard of responses (para. 15)

### Evaluating Evidence
- The auditor **shall** evaluate whether results of confirmation procedures provide relevant and reliable audit evidence, or whether further evidence is needed (para. 16)

## Key Definitions

- **External confirmation** — Audit evidence obtained as a direct written response to the auditor from a third party (confirming party), in paper form, electronic or other medium
- **Positive confirmation request** — Request that the confirming party respond indicating agreement or disagreement, or providing requested information
- **Negative confirmation request** — Request that the confirming party respond only if they disagree with information provided
- **Non-response** — Failure of confirming party to respond or fully respond to a positive confirmation request, or a request returned undelivered
- **Exception** — Response indicating a difference between information requested/in entity records and information provided by the confirming party
