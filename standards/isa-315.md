# ISA 315 (Revised 2019) — Identifying and Assessing the Risks of Material Misstatement

## Objective

The objective of the auditor is to identify and assess the risks of material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error, at the financial statement and assertion levels, thereby providing a basis for designing and implementing responses to the assessed risks of material misstatement. (Para. 11)

## Scope

ISA 315 deals with the auditor's responsibility to identify and assess the risks of material misstatement in the financial statements. It covers the auditor's risk assessment procedures, obtaining an understanding of the entity and its environment (including the applicable financial reporting framework and the entity's system of internal control), and the identification and assessment of risks at both the financial statement level and the assertion level. The standard distinguishes between inherent risk and control risk, and introduces the concept of the "spectrum of inherent risk." The risk identification and assessment process is iterative and dynamic.

## Key Requirements

### Risk Assessment Procedures and Related Activities (Para. 13-18)
- The auditor shall design and perform risk assessment procedures to obtain audit evidence that provides an appropriate basis for the identification and assessment of risks of material misstatement and the design of further audit procedures in accordance with [[standards/isa-330]].
- Risk assessment procedures shall not be biased towards corroborative or contradictory evidence.
- Risk assessment procedures shall include:
  - Inquiries of management and other appropriate individuals within the entity
  - Analytical procedures
  - Observation and inspection
- The auditor shall consider information from acceptance/continuance procedures and prior engagements.
- The engagement partner and other key engagement team members shall discuss the susceptibility of the entity's financial statements to material misstatement.

### Understanding the Entity and Its Environment (Para. 19-20)
The auditor shall obtain an understanding of:
- The entity's organizational structure, ownership and governance, and its business model (including IT integration)
- Industry, regulatory and other external factors
- Measures used to assess the entity's financial performance
- The applicable financial reporting framework, accounting policies and reasons for any changes
- How inherent risk factors affect susceptibility of assertions to misstatement

### Understanding the System of Internal Control (Para. 21-27)
The auditor shall understand five interrelated components:

1. **Control Environment** (Para. 21) — Culture of honesty and ethical behavior; whether the control environment provides an appropriate foundation for other components
2. **Entity's Risk Assessment Process** (Para. 22-23) — How the entity identifies, assesses and addresses business risks relevant to financial reporting
3. **Entity's Process to Monitor the System of Internal Control** (Para. 24) — Ongoing and separate evaluations, internal audit function
4. **Information System and Communication** (Para. 25) — How information flows through the entity's information system; how significant matters supporting financial statement preparation are communicated
5. **Control Activities** (Para. 26) — Identifying controls that address risks at the assertion level, including controls over significant risks, journal entries, and IT-related controls; evaluating design and determining implementation

### Identifying and Assessing Risks (Para. 28-37)
- The auditor shall identify risks of material misstatement at the financial statement level and/or the assertion level.
- The auditor shall determine relevant assertions and related significant classes of transactions, account balances and disclosures.
- **Inherent risk assessment** (Para. 31): Assess the likelihood and magnitude of misstatement, taking into account how inherent risk factors affect susceptibility of assertions.
- **Significant risks** (Para. 32): Determine whether any assessed risks are significant risks (close to the upper end of the spectrum of inherent risk).
- **Control risk assessment** (Para. 34): If the auditor plans to test controls, assess control risk. If not, the risk of material misstatement equals the inherent risk assessment.
- **Revision** (Para. 37): The auditor shall revise the identification or assessment if new information is inconsistent with the original audit evidence.

### Documentation (Para. 38)
The auditor shall document:
- The engagement team discussion and significant decisions reached
- Key elements of the auditor's understanding of the entity, its environment and internal controls
- Evaluation of identified controls' design and implementation
- Identified and assessed risks at both financial statement and assertion levels, including significant risks and the rationale for significant judgments

## Key Definitions

- **Assertions** — Representations (explicit or otherwise) with respect to recognition, measurement, presentation and disclosure of information in the financial statements
- **Business risk** — A risk resulting from significant conditions, events, circumstances, actions or inactions that could adversely affect an entity's ability to achieve its objectives
- **Controls** — Policies or procedures established to achieve control objectives
- **General IT controls** — Controls over IT processes that support the continued proper operation of the IT environment
- **Information processing controls** — Controls relating to the processing of information that directly address risks to the integrity of information
- **Inherent risk factors** — Characteristics of events or conditions that affect susceptibility to misstatement (complexity, subjectivity, change, uncertainty, or susceptibility to management bias/fraud)
- **IT environment** — IT applications, supporting IT infrastructure, and IT processes and personnel used to support business operations
- **Relevant assertions** — Assertions that have an identified risk of material misstatement (determined before consideration of controls)
- **Risk assessment procedures** — Audit procedures designed to identify and assess the risks of material misstatement
- **Significant class of transactions, account balance or disclosure** — One for which there is one or more relevant assertions
- **Significant risk** — An identified risk of material misstatement for which inherent risk is close to the upper end of the spectrum, or that is to be treated as significant per other ISAs
- **System of internal control** — System designed and maintained by governance, management and other personnel, consisting of five components: control environment, entity's risk assessment process, entity's process to monitor the system of internal control, information system and communication, and control activities
