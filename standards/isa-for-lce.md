# ISA for LCE — International Standard on Auditing for Audits of Financial Statements of Less Complex Entities

## Objective

The overall objectives of the auditor when conducting an audit using the ISA for LCE are to:

1. Obtain reasonable assurance about whether the financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error, to enable the auditor to express an opinion on whether the financial statements are prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with an applicable financial reporting framework; and
2. Report on the financial statements, and communicate as required by this standard, in accordance with the auditor's findings.

## Scope

The ISA for LCE is a standalone standard designed for audits of financial statements of less complex entities (LCEs). It is effective for audits of financial statements for periods beginning on or after December 15, 2025.

The standard is **not** permitted for use when:
- Law or regulation prohibits it or specifies other auditing standards
- The entity is a **listed entity**
- The entity's main function is to take deposits from the public, provide insurance to the public, or falls into a class prohibited by local standard-setters
- The audit is a **group audit** where any group entity meets the prohibited criteria or component auditors are involved (except for limited physical presence needs)

The standard does not address complex matters, and is not designed for entities with:
- Complex business activities, operations, or transactions
- Complex ownership, governance arrangements, or policies/procedures
- Segment information reporting or key audit matters
- Use of internal auditors' work or service organization reports (Type 1/Type 2)

## Key Requirements

The ISA for LCE is structured into 10 Parts following the flow of an audit:

- **Part A** — Authority for determining appropriate use of the ISA for LCE (specific prohibitions, qualitative characteristics, quantitative thresholds)
- **Part 1** — Fundamental concepts, general principles, and overarching requirements (ethical requirements, quality management, professional judgment and skepticism, fraud, laws and regulations, related parties, communications, documentation)
- **Part 2** — Audit evidence and documentation (sufficient appropriate audit evidence, information used as audit evidence, general documentation requirements)
- **Part 3** — Engagement quality management (engagement partner responsibilities, direction, supervision, and review)
- **Part 4** — Acceptance or continuance of the audit engagement and initial audits
- **Part 5** — Planning the audit and identifying/assessing risks of material misstatement
- **Part 6** — Responding to assessed risks of material misstatement
- **Part 7** — Concluding (evaluating misstatements, subsequent events, going concern, written representations)
- **Part 8** — Forming the auditor's opinion and reporting
- **Part 9** — Special considerations (particular circumstances such as opening balances, comparative information, other information)
- **Part 10** — Special considerations for group financial statements

Key principles:
- The auditor shall comply with all relevant requirements unless, in **exceptional circumstances**, the auditor departs from a requirement and performs alternative procedures to achieve the aim of that requirement
- Professional judgment and professional skepticism are required throughout
- The auditor shall not represent compliance with the ISA for LCE unless all relevant requirements have been met
- If an objective in a Part cannot be achieved, the auditor must either modify terms to use full ISAs, modify the opinion, or withdraw from the engagement

## Key Definitions

- **Less Complex Entity (LCE)** — An entity that exhibits qualitative characteristics described in Part A, does not fall into prohibited categories, and falls within quantitative thresholds set by the jurisdiction
- **Essential Explanatory Material (EEM)** — Material presented in italics within blue boxes that provides further context for requirements (unique to ISA for LCE format)
- **Consolidation process** — Used broadly to refer to the process used to prepare group financial statements, not limited to the financial reporting framework definition of "consolidation"
- **Component auditor** — An auditor who performs audit work related to a component for purposes of the group audit

## Application to Probe Working Papers

These standards are not currently referenced by any Probe working papers, but the ISA for LCE is highly relevant for firms using CaseWare Probe to audit less complex entities. The simplified structure (10 Parts vs. 39+ individual ISAs) could influence how Probe templates are designed for LCE audits. Any future LCE-specific working papers would need to align with this standard rather than individual ISAs.
