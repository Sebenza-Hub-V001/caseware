# ISAE 3400 — The Examination of Prospective Financial Information

## Objective

In an engagement to examine prospective financial information, the auditor should obtain sufficient appropriate evidence as to whether:

1. Management's **best-estimate assumptions** on which the prospective financial information is based are not unreasonable and, in the case of hypothetical assumptions, such assumptions are consistent with the purpose of the information;
2. The prospective financial information is **properly prepared** on the basis of the assumptions;
3. The prospective financial information is **properly presented** and all material assumptions are adequately disclosed, including a clear indication as to whether they are best-estimate assumptions or hypothetical assumptions; and
4. The prospective financial information is prepared on a **consistent basis** with historical financial statements, using appropriate accounting principles.

## Scope

This ISAE establishes standards and provides guidance on engagements to examine and report on prospective financial information, including examination procedures for best-estimate and hypothetical assumptions.

Key scope points:
- Covers examinations of **forecasts** (based on best-estimate assumptions of expected events) and **projections** (based on hypothetical "what-if" assumptions)
- Does **not** apply to prospective financial information expressed in general or narrative terms (e.g., MD&A in annual reports)
- Prospective financial information may be prepared for internal management purposes or for third-party distribution (prospectus, annual report, lender documents)
- Previously designated as ISA 810

Note: This is an older standard and uses "should" language rather than the "shall" language of more recent standards.

## Key Requirements

1. **Acceptance of engagement** — Consider the intended use, whether the information will be for general or limited distribution, the nature of assumptions (best-estimate vs. hypothetical), elements to be included, and the period covered
2. **Knowledge of the business** — Obtain sufficient knowledge of the business to evaluate whether all significant assumptions required for preparation have been identified
3. **Period covered** — Consider the period covered by the prospective financial information; the further into the future, the less reliable the assumptions
4. **Examination procedures** — Include:
   - Evaluating the source and reliability of evidence supporting management's best-estimate assumptions
   - Determining whether hypothetical assumptions are consistent with the purpose of the information
   - Assessing whether the prospective financial information is properly prepared from the assumptions
   - Focusing on areas most sensitive to variation (material, uncertain items)
   - Obtaining written representations from management regarding intended use and completeness of significant assumptions
5. **Presentation and disclosure** — Evaluate presentation, including whether assumptions are adequately disclosed
6. **Reporting** — Report should include:
   - Statement of negative assurance as to whether assumptions provide a reasonable basis
   - Opinion on whether the information is properly prepared on the basis of assumptions and presented in accordance with the applicable framework
   - Appropriate caveats regarding achievability of results

## Key Definitions

- **Prospective financial information** — Financial information based on assumptions about events that may occur in the future and possible actions by an entity
- **Forecast** — Prospective financial information prepared on the basis of assumptions as to future events management **expects** to take place (best-estimate assumptions)
- **Projection** — Prospective financial information prepared on the basis of **hypothetical assumptions** about future events not necessarily expected to occur, or a mixture of best-estimate and hypothetical assumptions
