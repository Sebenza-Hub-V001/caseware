# ISA 800 — Special Considerations: Audits of Financial Statements Prepared in Accordance with Special Purpose Frameworks

## Objective

The objective of the auditor, when applying ISAs in an audit of financial statements prepared in accordance with a special purpose framework, is to address appropriately the special considerations that are relevant to:

- The acceptance of the engagement;
- The planning and performance of that engagement; and
- Forming an opinion and reporting on the financial statements.

## Scope

ISA 800 (Revised) deals with special considerations in the application of the ISAs in the 100-700 series to an audit of financial statements prepared in accordance with a special purpose framework. It is written in the context of a complete set of special purpose financial statements. [[standards/isa-805]] deals with audits of single financial statements or specific elements. This ISA does not override other ISA requirements nor purport to deal with all special considerations that may be relevant.

## Key Requirements

### Accepting the Engagement (para 8)

- **Understand the framework's acceptability:** The auditor shall obtain an understanding of:
  - The purpose for which the financial statements are prepared
  - The intended users
  - The steps taken by management to determine that the applicable financial reporting framework is acceptable in the circumstances

### Planning and Performing the Audit (paras 9-10)

- **Special considerations:** The auditor shall determine whether application of the ISAs requires special consideration in the circumstances of the engagement.
- **Contract-based frameworks:** When financial statements are prepared in accordance with a contract, the auditor shall obtain an understanding of any significant interpretations of the contract that management made. An interpretation is significant when another reasonable interpretation would have produced a material difference.

### Forming an Opinion and Reporting (paras 11-14)

- **Apply ISA 700 (Revised):** The auditor shall apply the requirements of [[standards/isa-700]] when forming an opinion and reporting.
- **Evaluate framework description:** In the case of contract-based financial statements, evaluate whether the financial statements adequately describe any significant interpretations of the contract on which they are based.
- **Report content for special purpose financial statements:**
  - Describe the purpose for which the financial statements are prepared and, if necessary, the intended users (or refer to a note containing that information)
  - If management has a choice of frameworks, the management responsibility description shall reference responsibility for determining that the applicable framework is acceptable
- **Emphasis of Matter paragraph (mandatory):** The auditor's report on special purpose financial statements shall include an Emphasis of Matter paragraph alerting users that the financial statements are prepared in accordance with a special purpose framework and that, as a result, the financial statements may not be suitable for another purpose.

## Key Definitions

- **Special purpose financial statements:** Financial statements prepared in accordance with a special purpose framework.
- **Special purpose framework:** A financial reporting framework designed to meet the financial information needs of specific users. May be a fair presentation framework or a compliance framework. Examples include:
  - A tax basis of accounting for financial statements accompanying a tax return
  - The cash receipts and disbursements basis of accounting for cash flow information prepared for creditors
  - Financial reporting provisions established by a regulator
  - Financial reporting provisions of a contract (e.g., bond indenture, loan agreement, project grant)

## Application to Probe Working Papers

No specific Probe working papers are currently mapped to ISA 800. The standard would be relevant when auditing entities that prepare financial statements under a special purpose framework rather than a general purpose framework (e.g., IFRS or local GAAP).
