# ISA 580 — Written Representations

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor are:
- To obtain written representations from management (and, where appropriate, those charged with governance) that they believe they have fulfilled their responsibility for the preparation of the financial statements and for the completeness of the information provided to the auditor
- To support other audit evidence relevant to the financial statements or specific assertions by means of written representations if determined necessary or required by other ISAs
- To respond appropriately to written representations provided, or if requested representations are not provided

## Scope

This ISA deals with the auditor's responsibility to obtain written representations from management and, where appropriate, those charged with governance in an audit of financial statements. Written representations are necessary audit evidence but do not provide sufficient appropriate audit evidence on their own.

## Key Requirements

### Management from Whom Representations Are Requested
- The auditor **shall** request written representations from management with appropriate responsibilities and knowledge of the matters concerned (para. 9)

### Written Representations about Management's Responsibilities
- The auditor **shall** request a representation that management has fulfilled its responsibility for preparing the financial statements in accordance with the applicable framework (para. 10)
- The auditor **shall** request a representation that management has provided all relevant information and access and that all transactions have been recorded (para. 11)
- Management's responsibilities **shall** be described as set out in the terms of the audit engagement (para. 12)

### Other Written Representations
- The auditor **shall** request additional written representations to support other audit evidence when determined necessary (para. 13)

### Date and Period
- Written representations **shall** be dated as near as practicable to, but not after, the date of the auditor's report, covering all financial statements and periods referred to in the auditor's report (para. 14)

### Form
- Written representations **shall** be in the form of a representation letter addressed to the auditor (para. 15)

### Doubt as to Reliability / Representations Not Provided
- If the auditor has concerns about management's integrity, the auditor **shall** determine the effect on the reliability of all representations and audit evidence (para. 16)
- If written representations are inconsistent with other audit evidence, the auditor **shall** perform procedures to resolve the matter (para. 17)
- If written representations are not reliable, the auditor **shall** take appropriate actions including considering the effect on the audit opinion (para. 18)
- If management does not provide requested representations, the auditor **shall** discuss the matter, reevaluate management's integrity, and take appropriate actions (para. 19)
- The auditor **shall** disclaim an opinion if there is sufficient doubt about management's integrity such that the required representations are not reliable, or if management does not provide the required representations about responsibilities and completeness (para. 20)

## Key Definitions

- **Written representation** — A written statement by management provided to the auditor to confirm certain matters or to support other audit evidence. Does not include financial statements, assertions therein, or supporting books and records.
