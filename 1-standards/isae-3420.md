# ISAE 3420 — Assurance Engagements to Report on the Compilation of Pro Forma Financial Information Included in a Prospectus

## Objective

The objectives of the practitioner are:

1. To obtain **reasonable assurance** about whether the pro forma financial information has been compiled, in all material respects, by the responsible party on the basis of the applicable criteria; and
2. To report in accordance with the practitioner's findings.

## Scope

This ISAE deals with **reasonable assurance attestation engagements** to report on the responsible party's compilation of pro forma financial information included in a prospectus.

Key scope points:
- Applies where such reporting is required by securities law or the regulation of the securities exchange, or is generally accepted practice in the jurisdiction
- The practitioner has **no responsibility to compile** the pro forma financial information — that responsibility rests with the responsible party
- The practitioner's sole responsibility is to report on whether the pro forma financial information has been compiled in accordance with applicable criteria
- Does **not** deal with non-assurance engagements to compile historical financial statements
- Pro forma financial information illustrates the impact of a significant event or transaction on unadjusted financial information **as if** the event had occurred at an earlier date — it does **not** represent actual financial position, performance, or cash flows
- The practitioner must comply with [[1-standards/isae-3000]] in addition to this ISAE

Effective for assurance reports dated on or after **March 31, 2013**.

## Key Requirements

1. **ISAE 3000 (Revised) compliance** — Apply alongside this ISAE
2. **Engagement acceptance** — Evaluate whether the engagement can be accepted, including understanding the purpose of the pro forma financial information
3. **Planning and performing** — Including:
   - Understanding the event or transaction and how management has reflected its impact
   - Identifying the source of unadjusted financial information and evaluating whether it has been properly extracted
   - Evaluating whether pro forma adjustments are appropriate, give effect to the applicable criteria, and are consistently applied
   - Evaluating whether the applicable criteria provide a reasonable basis for presenting the significant effects directly attributable to the event or transaction
   - Evaluating the overall presentation of the pro forma financial information
   - Reading other information in the prospectus for consistency
4. **Written representations** — Obtain from the responsible party
5. **Forming the opinion** — Consider whether pro forma financial information is compiled in all material respects on the basis of applicable criteria
6. **Form of opinion** — Express an unmodified opinion if satisfied; otherwise qualified, adverse, or disclaimer
7. **Reporting** — Prepare the assurance report with required elements

## Key Definitions

- **Applicable criteria** — Criteria used by the responsible party when compiling pro forma financial information (may be established by regulation or developed by the responsible party)
- **Pro forma adjustments** — Adjustments to unadjusted financial information, including adjustments to illustrate the impact of the event or transaction and adjustments to conform accounting policies
- **Pro forma column** — A column showing the resulting amounts after applying pro forma adjustments to the unadjusted financial information
- **Unadjusted financial information** — The historical financial information of the entity before application of pro forma adjustments

## Application to Probe Working Papers

This standard is not currently referenced by any Probe working papers. It is a specialized standard relevant to capital markets transactions and prospectus requirements, which is outside the typical scope of recurring audit engagements managed in Probe.
