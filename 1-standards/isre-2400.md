# ISRE 2400 (Revised) — Engagements to Review Historical Financial Statements

## Objective

The practitioner's objectives in a review of financial statements under this ISRE are to:

1. Obtain **limited assurance**, primarily by performing inquiry and analytical procedures, about whether the financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement, thereby enabling the practitioner to express a conclusion on whether anything has come to the practitioner's attention that causes the practitioner to believe the financial statements are not prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with an applicable financial reporting framework; and
2. Report on the financial statements as a whole and communicate, as required by this ISRE.

When limited assurance cannot be obtained and a qualified conclusion is insufficient, the practitioner must disclaim a conclusion or withdraw from the engagement.

## Scope

This ISRE deals with:
- The practitioner's responsibilities when engaged to perform a review of historical financial statements, **when the practitioner is not the auditor of the entity's financial statements**
- The form and content of the practitioner's report

It does **not** address reviews of financial statements or interim financial information performed by the independent auditor of the entity (see [[standards/isre-2410]] for that).

The ISRE may be applied, adapted as necessary, to reviews of other historical financial information. Limited assurance engagements other than reviews of historical financial information are performed under [[standards/isae-3000]].

Effective for reviews of financial statements for periods ending on or after **December 31, 2013**.

## Key Requirements

1. **Quality management** — ISQM 1 applies at the firm level; engagement-level quality management provisions apply
2. **Ethical requirements** — Compliance with relevant ethical requirements including independence
3. **Professional skepticism and judgment** — Required throughout the engagement
4. **Acceptance and continuance** — Evaluate preconditions, obtain agreement on terms of engagement
5. **Performing the engagement** — Primarily through:
   - **Inquiry** of management and others within the entity
   - **Analytical procedures** applied to financial data
   - **Additional procedures** if the practitioner becomes aware of matters suggesting material misstatement
6. **Subsequent events** — Consider events between the financial statement date and the report date
7. **Written representations** — Obtain from management
8. **Evaluating evidence** — Evaluate whether sufficient appropriate evidence has been obtained
9. **Forming conclusion** — Form a conclusion on the financial statements as a whole
10. **Reporting** — Issue the practitioner's report with conclusion
11. **Documentation** — Document the engagement

A review provides **limited assurance** (negative assurance), which is substantially less than reasonable assurance provided by an audit. The practitioner performs primarily inquiry and analytical procedures rather than the full range of audit procedures.

## Key Definitions

- **Analytical procedures** — Evaluations of financial information through analysis of plausible relationships among both financial and non-financial data
- **Engagement risk** — The risk that the practitioner expresses an inappropriate conclusion when the financial statements are materially misstated
- **Limited assurance** — A level of assurance that is less than reasonable assurance, obtained primarily through inquiry and analytical procedures

## Application to Probe Working Papers

This standard is not currently referenced by any Probe working papers, which are focused on audit engagements under the ISAs. However, ISRE 2400 would be relevant if CaseWare develops Probe templates for review engagements. The standard's requirement structure (inquiry and analytical procedures) would require a different set of working papers than a full audit.
