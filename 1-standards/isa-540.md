# ISA 540 (Revised) — Auditing Accounting Estimates and Related Disclosures

## Objective

The objective of the auditor is to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether accounting estimates and related disclosures in the financial statements are reasonable in the context of the applicable financial reporting framework.

## Scope

This ISA deals with the auditor's responsibilities relating to accounting estimates and related disclosures in an audit of financial statements. It includes requirements and guidance that expand on how ISA 315 (Revised 2019), ISA 330, ISA 450, ISA 500 and other ISAs are to be applied in relation to accounting estimates. It also addresses evaluation of misstatements and indicators of possible management bias.

The degree of estimation uncertainty varies substantially across accounting estimates, and the nature, timing and extent of required procedures scale accordingly.

## Key Requirements

### Risk Assessment Procedures and Related Activities
- The auditor **shall** obtain an understanding of the entity's accounting estimates, including: transactions giving rise to estimates, applicable framework requirements, regulatory factors, nature of expected estimates, internal controls, management's use of experts, risk assessment processes, information systems, and control activities (para. 13)
- The auditor **shall** review outcomes of previous accounting estimates or their subsequent re-estimation (para. 14)
- The auditor **shall** determine whether specialized skills or knowledge are needed (para. 15)

### Identifying and Assessing Risks
- The auditor **shall** take into account the degree of estimation uncertainty and the degree to which complexity, subjectivity and other inherent risk factors affect method selection, assumptions, data and point estimate selection (para. 16)
- The auditor **shall** determine whether any risks are significant risks, and if so, identify and evaluate relevant controls (para. 17)

### Responses to Assessed Risks
- Further audit procedures **shall** be responsive to assessed risks and include one or more of: obtaining evidence from events up to the auditor's report date, testing how management made the estimate, or developing an auditor's point estimate or range (para. 18)
- The auditor **shall** not bias procedures towards corroborative evidence or away from contradictory evidence (para. 19)
- For significant risks, further procedures **shall** include tests of controls (if relying on them) or tests of details (if substantive-only approach) (para. 20)

### Testing How Management Made the Estimate
- Procedures **shall** address methods (appropriateness, consistency, mathematical accuracy, models), significant assumptions (appropriateness, consistency, management bias indicators), and data (appropriateness, relevance, reliability) (paras. 22–26)
- The auditor **shall** address whether management has taken appropriate steps to understand and address estimation uncertainty (para. 27)

### Developing an Auditor's Point Estimate or Range
- If developing an auditor's estimate, procedures **shall** evaluate whether methods, assumptions or data are appropriate (para. 28)

### Disclosures
- The auditor **shall** obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about disclosures related to accounting estimates (para. 31)

### Management Bias
- The auditor **shall** evaluate whether judgments and decisions give rise to indicators of possible management bias (para. 32)

### Overall Evaluation
- The auditor **shall** evaluate whether the estimates and related disclosures are reasonable in the context of the framework, or are misstated (para. 33)
- The auditor **shall** obtain written representations from management regarding the reasonableness of significant assumptions (para. 37)
- The auditor **shall** communicate with those charged with governance about significant accounting estimates involving significant judgments (para. 38)

## Key Definitions

- **Accounting estimate** — A monetary amount whose measurement is subject to estimation uncertainty
- **Auditor's point estimate or auditor's range** — Amount or range developed by the auditor to evaluate management's point estimate
- **Estimation uncertainty** — Susceptibility to an inherent lack of precision in measurement
- **Management bias** — A lack of neutrality by management in the preparation of information
- **Management's point estimate** — Amount selected by management for recognition or disclosure
- **Outcome of an accounting estimate** — Actual monetary amount resulting from resolution of the underlying transaction(s), event(s) or condition(s)
