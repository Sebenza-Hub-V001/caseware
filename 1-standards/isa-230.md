# ISA 230 — Audit Documentation

## Objective

The objective of the auditor is to prepare documentation that provides:

- A sufficient and appropriate record of the basis for the auditor's report; and
- Evidence that the audit was planned and performed in accordance with ISAs and applicable legal and regulatory requirements.

## Scope

ISA 230 deals with the auditor's responsibility to prepare audit documentation for an audit of financial statements. It sets out the nature and purposes of audit documentation and the requirements for its form, content, extent, assembly, and retention. Other ISAs contain specific additional documentation requirements.

## Key Requirements

- **Timely preparation:** The auditor shall prepare audit documentation on a timely basis.
- **Sufficient documentation for an experienced auditor:** The auditor shall prepare audit documentation that is sufficient to enable an experienced auditor, having no previous connection with the audit, to understand: (a) the nature, timing and extent of procedures performed; (b) the results and audit evidence obtained; and (c) significant matters, conclusions and professional judgments.
- **Identifying characteristics:** The auditor shall record who performed the work and when, who reviewed it and the date and extent of review, and the identifying characteristics of specific items or matters tested.
- **Documenting discussions:** The auditor shall document discussions of significant matters with management, those charged with governance, and others.
- **Inconsistent information:** If the auditor identified information inconsistent with the final conclusion on a significant matter, the auditor shall document how the inconsistency was addressed.
- **Departure from requirements:** If the auditor departs from a relevant ISA requirement in exceptional circumstances, the auditor shall document the alternative procedures and reasons.
- **Assembly of the final audit file:** The auditor shall assemble the audit documentation in an audit file and complete the administrative process on a timely basis after the date of the auditor's report.
- **Retention:** After assembly, the auditor shall not delete or discard audit documentation before the end of its retention period.
- **Modifications after assembly:** Any modifications or additions after assembly of the final audit file must be documented with the specific reasons, when and by whom changes were made and reviewed.

## Key Definitions

- **Audit documentation** — The record of audit procedures performed, relevant audit evidence obtained, and conclusions the auditor reached (also known as "working papers" or "workpapers").
- **Audit file** — One or more folders or other storage media, in physical or electronic form, containing the records that comprise the audit documentation for a specific engagement.
- **Experienced auditor** — An individual who has practical audit experience and a reasonable understanding of audit processes, ISAs and applicable requirements, the business environment, and auditing and financial reporting issues relevant to the entity's industry.
