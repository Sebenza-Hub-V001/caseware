# ISA 810 — Engagements to Report on Summary Financial Statements

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor are:

- To determine whether it is appropriate to accept the engagement to report on summary financial statements; and
- If engaged to report on summary financial statements:
  - To form an opinion on the summary financial statements based on an evaluation of the conclusions drawn from the evidence obtained; and
  - To express clearly that opinion through a written report that also describes the basis for that opinion.

## Scope

ISA 810 (Revised) deals with the auditor's responsibilities relating to an engagement to report on summary financial statements derived from financial statements audited in accordance with ISAs by that same auditor. The auditor must have audited the underlying financial statements to accept a summary financial statements engagement.

## Key Requirements

### Engagement Acceptance (paras 5-7)

- **Precondition:** The auditor shall accept the engagement only when the auditor has been engaged to conduct an audit in accordance with ISAs of the financial statements from which the summary financial statements are derived.
- **Before accepting, the auditor shall:**
  - Determine whether the applied criteria are acceptable
  - Obtain management's agreement that it acknowledges responsibility for preparing the summary financial statements in accordance with the applied criteria, making the audited financial statements available to intended users without undue difficulty (or describing the law/regulation that provides otherwise), and including the auditor's report in any document containing the summary financial statements
  - Agree with management the form of opinion to be expressed
- **Unacceptable criteria or no agreement:** The auditor shall not accept the engagement unless required by law or regulation (in which case the report shall not indicate compliance with this ISA).

### Nature of Procedures (para 8)

- The auditor shall:
  - Evaluate whether the summary financial statements adequately disclose their summarised nature and identify the audited financial statements
  - Evaluate whether they clearly describe where the audited financial statements are available (or the law/regulation that provides they need not be available)
  - Evaluate whether the summary financial statements adequately disclose the applied criteria
  - Compare the summary financial statements with related information in the audited financial statements (agree or recalculate)
  - Evaluate whether the summary financial statements are prepared in accordance with the applied criteria
  - Evaluate whether the summary financial statements contain necessary information at an appropriate level of aggregation so as not to be misleading
  - Evaluate whether the audited financial statements are available to intended users without undue difficulty

### Form of Opinion (paras 9-11)

- **Unmodified opinion wording:** Either "consistent, in all material respects, with the audited financial statements, in accordance with [the applied criteria]" or "a fair summary of the audited financial statements, in accordance with [the applied criteria]."
- **Law-prescribed wording:** If prescribed wording differs, evaluate whether users might misunderstand the opinion. If additional explanation cannot mitigate misunderstanding, do not accept the engagement (unless required by law/regulation).

### Timing and Subsequent Events (paras 12-13)

- If the report on summary financial statements is dated later than the report on the audited financial statements, state that they do not reflect events subsequent to the date of the auditor's report on the audited financial statements.
- If the auditor becomes aware of facts that existed at the date of the auditor's report on the audited financial statements, do not issue the summary financial statements report until ISA 560 considerations are completed.

### Information in Documents Containing Summary Financial Statements (paras 14-15)

- Read other information in the document and consider whether there is a material inconsistency between that information and the summary financial statements.
- If the auditor identifies a material inconsistency or becomes aware of an apparent material misstatement of fact, discuss with management and take further action as necessary.

### Auditor's Report on Summary Financial Statements (paras 16-21)

- The report shall include: title, addressee, identification of the summary financial statements and audited financial statements, opinion, date, and auditor's address.
- **Modified opinion on audited financial statements:** If the auditor's opinion on the audited financial statements was modified, describe the modification and its effect on the summary financial statements. If the opinion was adverse or a disclaimer, state that it is inappropriate to express an opinion on the summary financial statements.
- **Other information in audited report:** If the auditor's report on the audited financial statements contained an EOM, OM, Other Information section, or KAM section, and the auditor considers it relevant, include a reference or description in the summary financial statements report.

### Restriction on Distribution/Use and Accounting Basis Alerts (para 22)

- If the auditor's report on the audited financial statements included an Emphasis of Matter paragraph about a special purpose framework, include a similar paragraph in the summary financial statements report.

### Comparatives (paras 23-24)

- If the audited financial statements contain comparatives, determine whether the summary financial statements contain corresponding comparatives. If prior period summary figures were reported on by another auditor, apply ISA 710 reporting principles adapted as necessary.

### Auditor Association (paras 26-27)

- If the auditor becomes aware that the entity plans to state the auditor reported on summary financial statements when no such engagement was undertaken, the auditor shall request that the entity not do so. If the entity proceeds despite the request, the auditor shall inform intended users and take further appropriate action (e.g., seeking legal advice).

## Key Definitions

- **Applied criteria:** The criteria applied by management in the preparation of the summary financial statements.
- **Audited financial statements:** Financial statements audited by the auditor in accordance with ISAs, from which the summary financial statements are derived.
- **Summary financial statements:** Historical financial information derived from financial statements but containing less detail, while still providing a structured representation consistent with that provided by the financial statements.
