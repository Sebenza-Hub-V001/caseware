# ISA 620 — Using the Work of an Auditor's Expert

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor are:
- To determine whether to use the work of an auditor's expert
- If using the work of an auditor's expert, to determine whether that work is adequate for the auditor's purposes

## Scope

This ISA deals with the auditor's responsibilities relating to the work of an individual or organisation in a field of expertise other than accounting or auditing, when that work is used to assist the auditor in obtaining sufficient appropriate audit evidence. It does not deal with engagement team members with specialised accounting or auditing expertise (ISA 220 (Revised)) or the use of management's experts (ISA 500).

The auditor has sole responsibility for the audit opinion; this responsibility is not reduced by using an auditor's expert. However, if the auditor concludes the expert's work is adequate, the auditor may accept the expert's findings or conclusions as appropriate audit evidence.

## Key Requirements

### Determining the Need
- If expertise in a field other than accounting or auditing is necessary to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence, the auditor **shall** determine whether to use an auditor's expert (para. 7)

### Nature, Timing and Extent of Procedures
- The auditor **shall** consider: the nature of the matter, risks of material misstatement, significance of the expert's work, the auditor's prior experience with that expert, and whether the expert is subject to the auditor's firm's quality management system (para. 8)

### Competence, Capabilities and Objectivity
- The auditor **shall** evaluate whether the expert has the necessary competence, capabilities and objectivity, including inquiry regarding interests and relationships that may threaten objectivity (for external experts) (para. 9)

### Understanding the Field of Expertise
- The auditor **shall** obtain sufficient understanding of the expert's field to determine the nature, scope and objectives of the work, and to evaluate its adequacy (para. 10)

### Agreement with the Expert
- The auditor **shall** agree (in writing when appropriate) on: nature, scope and objectives; respective roles and responsibilities; nature, timing and extent of communication; and confidentiality requirements (para. 11)

### Evaluating Adequacy of the Expert's Work
- The auditor **shall** evaluate adequacy including: relevance and reasonableness of findings/conclusions and consistency with other audit evidence; relevance and reasonableness of significant assumptions and methods; and relevance, completeness and accuracy of source data (para. 12)
- If the work is not adequate, the auditor **shall** agree on further work to be performed by the expert or perform additional audit procedures (para. 13)

### Reference in the Auditor's Report
- The auditor **shall not** refer to the expert's work in an unmodified opinion report unless required by law or regulation (para. 14)
- If reference is relevant to a modified opinion, the auditor **shall** indicate the reference does not reduce the auditor's responsibility (para. 15)

## Key Definitions

- **Auditor's expert** — An individual or organisation with expertise in a field other than accounting or auditing, whose work is used by the auditor. May be an auditor's internal expert (partner or staff of the auditor's firm or network firm) or an auditor's external expert
- **Expertise** — Skills, knowledge and experience in a particular field
- **Management's expert** — An individual or organisation with expertise in a field other than accounting or auditing, whose work is used by the entity to assist in preparing financial statements
