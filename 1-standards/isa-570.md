# ISA 570 (Revised) — Going Concern

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor are:
- To obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding, and conclude on, the appropriateness of management's use of the going concern basis of accounting in the preparation of the financial statements
- To conclude, based on the audit evidence obtained, whether a material uncertainty exists related to events or conditions that may cast significant doubt on the entity's ability to continue as a going concern
- To report in accordance with this ISA

## Scope

This ISA deals with the auditor's responsibilities in the audit of financial statements relating to going concern and the implications for the auditor's report. Under the going concern basis of accounting, financial statements are prepared on the assumption that the entity will continue its operations for the foreseeable future. The standard applies even where the financial reporting framework does not include an explicit requirement for management to assess going concern.

## Key Requirements

### Risk Assessment Procedures and Related Activities
- The auditor **shall** consider whether events or conditions exist that may cast significant doubt on going concern when performing risk assessment procedures, and determine whether management has performed a preliminary assessment (para. 10–11)
- The auditor **shall** remain alert throughout the audit for evidence of events or conditions casting significant doubt on going concern (para. 11)

### Evaluating Management's Assessment
- The auditor **shall** evaluate management's assessment of the entity's ability to continue as a going concern (para. 12)
- The auditor **shall** cover the same period as management (at least twelve months from the financial statement date) (para. 13)
- The auditor **shall** consider whether management's assessment includes all relevant information known to the auditor (para. 14)

### Period Beyond Management's Assessment
- The auditor **shall** inquire of management as to knowledge of events or conditions beyond the assessment period that may cast significant doubt (para. 15)

### Additional Procedures When Events or Conditions Are Identified
- If events or conditions are identified, the auditor **shall** obtain sufficient appropriate evidence to determine whether a material uncertainty exists, including (para. 16):
  - Requesting management to make its assessment if not yet done
  - Evaluating management's plans for future actions and their feasibility
  - Evaluating cash flow forecasts (reliability of data, support for assumptions)
  - Considering additional facts or information since the assessment date
  - Requesting written representations about plans and their feasibility

### Auditor Conclusions
- The auditor **shall** conclude on the appropriateness of management's use of the going concern basis (para. 17)
- The auditor **shall** conclude whether a material uncertainty exists (para. 18)
- If a material uncertainty exists with adequate disclosure, the auditor **shall** express an unmodified opinion with a "Material Uncertainty Related to Going Concern" section (para. 22)
- If adequate disclosure is not made, the auditor **shall** express a qualified or adverse opinion (para. 23)
- If going concern basis is inappropriate, the auditor **shall** express an adverse opinion (para. 21)

### Communication
- The auditor **shall** communicate with those charged with governance about identified events or conditions and whether they constitute a material uncertainty (para. 25)

## Key Definitions

No additional definitions specific to this ISA; the standard relies on definitions from ISA 560 (date of financial statements) and the general framework concepts of going concern basis of accounting and material uncertainty.
