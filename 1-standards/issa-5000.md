# ISSA 5000 — General Requirements for Sustainability Assurance Engagements

## Objective

In conducting a sustainability assurance engagement, the objectives of the practitioner are:

1. To obtain reasonable assurance or limited assurance, as applicable, about whether the sustainability information is free from material misstatement;
2. To express a conclusion on the sustainability information through a written report that conveys a reasonable assurance or a limited assurance conclusion, as applicable, and describes the basis for the conclusion; and
3. To communicate further as required by this ISSA and any other relevant ISSA.

When assurance cannot be obtained and a qualified conclusion is insufficient, the practitioner must disclaim a conclusion or withdraw from the engagement.

## Scope

ISSA 5000 applies to **all assurance engagements on sustainability information**, regardless of how that information is presented. It covers both reasonable and limited assurance engagements and deals only with attestation engagements (not direct engagements).

Effective for assurance engagements on sustainability information reported for periods beginning on or after **December 15, 2026**, or as at a specific date on or after December 15, 2026. Earlier application is permitted.

Key scope points:
- Applies to all types of sustainability information (climate, labor practices, biodiversity, etc.)
- Sustainability information is reported in accordance with criteria; framework criteria established by authorized bodies following transparent due process are presumed suitable
- The scope may cover all sustainability information reported by the entity or only part of it
- Does **not** address sustainability information required to be included in financial statements (that falls under ISAs)
- This is an **overarching standard** — practitioners are not required to also apply [[standards/isae-3000]] when performing the engagement
- Scalable for entities of all sizes and complexity

## Key Requirements

The standard follows the structure of an assurance engagement:

1. **Conduct of engagement** — Compliance with ISSAs, applying requirements proportionally
2. **Acceptance and continuance** — Evaluating preconditions, terms of engagement
3. **Quality management** — Firm-level (ISQM 1) and engagement-level quality management, including direction/supervision/review, engagement quality review considerations
4. **Fraud and non-compliance** — Consideration of fraud and non-compliance with laws and regulations
5. **Documentation** — Sufficient documentation of the engagement
6. **Evidence** — Obtaining sufficient appropriate evidence
7. **Planning** — Planning the engagement, including understanding the entity's process to identify sustainability information
8. **Risk assessment** — Performing risk assessment procedures, understanding the entity and its environment, identifying/assessing risks of material misstatement
9. **Responding to risks** — Designing and performing procedures responsive to assessed risks of material misstatement
10. **Evaluating misstatements** — Accumulation and consideration of identified misstatements
11. **Other procedures** — Evaluating criteria description, subsequent events, written representations, other information
12. **Forming conclusion** — Forming the assurance conclusion
13. **Reporting** — Preparing the assurance report

Requirements differentiated by "L" suffix apply only to limited assurance engagements, and "R" suffix for reasonable assurance only.

## Key Definitions

- **Sustainability information** — Information about sustainability matters (e.g., climate, labor practices, biodiversity, risks and opportunities, metrics and KPIs)
- **Applicable criteria** — The criteria used for the particular sustainability assurance engagement
- **Another practitioner** — A firm, other than the practitioner's firm, that performs work the practitioner intends to use for purposes of the engagement
- **Analytical procedures** — Evaluations of sustainability information through analysis of plausible relationships among both quantitative and qualitative data
- **Entity's process to identify sustainability information to be reported** — The process applied by the entity to determine sustainability matters to be reported and the reporting boundary

## Application to Probe Working Papers

This standard is not currently referenced by any Probe working papers, as sustainability assurance is a new area (effective December 2026). However, as sustainability reporting mandates expand globally, CaseWare may need to develop Probe working papers for sustainability assurance engagements. This standard would be the foundational reference for any such working papers.

Note: ISSA 5000 supersedes [[standards/isae-3410]] (GHG Statements) with effect from December 15, 2026.
