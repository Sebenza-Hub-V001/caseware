# ISA 710 — Comparative Information: Corresponding Figures and Comparative Financial Statements

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor are:

- To obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether the comparative information included in the financial statements has been presented, in all material respects, in accordance with the requirements for comparative information in the applicable financial reporting framework; and
- To report in accordance with the auditor's reporting responsibilities.

## Scope

ISA 710 deals with the auditor's responsibilities relating to comparative information in an audit of financial statements. It addresses two approaches to comparative information: corresponding figures and comparative financial statements. The approach adopted is often specified by law or regulation or in the terms of engagement. When prior period financial statements were audited by a predecessor auditor or were not audited, requirements in ISA 510 (Opening Balances) also apply.

The essential reporting difference: for corresponding figures, the auditor's opinion refers to the current period only; for comparative financial statements, the opinion refers to each period presented.

## Key Requirements

### Audit Procedures (paras 7-9)

- **Determine inclusion and classification:** The auditor shall determine whether the financial statements include the comparative information required by the framework and whether it is appropriately classified.
- **Evaluate consistency:** Whether comparative information agrees with prior period amounts (or has been restated) and whether accounting policies are consistent (or changes properly accounted for and disclosed).
- **Material misstatement in comparatives:** If the auditor becomes aware of a possible material misstatement in comparative information, perform additional procedures as necessary. If the auditor audited the prior period, also follow ISA 560 (Subsequent Events) requirements.
- **Written representations:** Obtain written representations for all periods referred to in the opinion, including specific representations regarding any restatement of prior period misstatements.

### Reporting — Corresponding Figures (paras 10-14)

- **General rule:** The auditor's opinion shall not refer to the corresponding figures except in specific circumstances.
- **Unresolved prior period modification:** If the prior period report included a modified opinion and the matter is unresolved, the auditor shall modify the current period opinion, referring to both current and corresponding figures (if effects are material on current period) or explaining the impact on comparability.
- **Material misstatement in prior period (previously unmodified):** If corresponding figures have not been properly restated or disclosed, express a qualified or adverse opinion modified with respect to the corresponding figures.
- **Predecessor auditor:** The auditor may state in an Other Matter paragraph that the prior period was audited by a predecessor auditor, the type of opinion expressed, reasons for any modification, and the date of that report.
- **Prior period not audited:** State in an Other Matter paragraph that the corresponding figures are unaudited (but this does not relieve the auditor of the requirement regarding opening balances).

### Reporting — Comparative Financial Statements (paras 15-19)

- **Opinion refers to each period:** The auditor's opinion shall refer to each period for which financial statements are presented and on which an audit opinion is expressed.
- **Different opinion from prior period:** If the opinion on prior period financial statements differs from the one previously expressed, disclose the substantive reasons in an Other Matter paragraph per [[1-standards/isa-706]].
- **Predecessor auditor:** State in an Other Matter paragraph that the prior period was audited by a predecessor auditor, the opinion type, reasons for modification, and date.
- **Material misstatement in predecessor-audited statements:** Communicate with management and those charged with governance, request that the predecessor auditor be informed. If amended and predecessor issues a new report, report only on the current period.
- **Prior period not audited:** State in an Other Matter paragraph that comparative financial statements are unaudited.

## Key Definitions

- **Comparative information:** The amounts and disclosures included in the financial statements in respect of one or more prior periods in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework.
- **Corresponding figures:** Comparative information where prior period amounts and disclosures are included as an integral part of the current period financial statements, intended to be read only in relation to current period figures.
- **Comparative financial statements:** Comparative information where prior period amounts and disclosures are included for comparison with current period financial statements and, if audited, are referred to in the auditor's opinion. The level of information is comparable with current period statements.
