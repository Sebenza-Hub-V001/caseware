# ISA 701 — Communicating Key Audit Matters in the Independent Auditor's Report

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor are to determine key audit matters and, having formed an opinion on the financial statements, communicate those matters by describing them in the auditor's report.

## Scope

ISA 701 deals with the auditor's responsibility to communicate key audit matters in the auditor's report. It addresses both the auditor's judgment as to what to communicate and the form and content of such communication. The purpose is to enhance the communicative value of the auditor's report by providing greater transparency about the audit performed.

The standard applies to audits of complete sets of general purpose financial statements of listed entities and circumstances when the auditor otherwise decides to communicate key audit matters, or is required to do so by law or regulation. ISA 705 (Revised) prohibits communicating key audit matters when the auditor disclaims an opinion, unless required by law or regulation.

Communicating key audit matters is not a substitute for: disclosures required by the financial reporting framework; expressing a modified opinion when required; reporting on going concern under ISA 570 (Revised); or a separate opinion on individual matters.

## Key Requirements

### Determining Key Audit Matters (paras 9-10)

- **From matters communicated with those charged with governance:** The auditor shall determine those matters that required significant auditor attention, taking into account:
  - Areas of higher assessed risk of material misstatement, or significant risks identified per ISA 315 (Revised 2019)
  - Significant auditor judgments relating to areas involving significant management judgment, including accounting estimates with high estimation uncertainty
  - The effect on the audit of significant events or transactions during the period
- **Most significant matters:** The auditor shall determine which of these matters were of most significance in the audit of the current period and therefore are the key audit matters.

### Communicating Key Audit Matters (paras 11-16)

- **Separate section with heading:** Each key audit matter shall be described using an appropriate subheading in a separate section headed "Key Audit Matters."
- **Introductory language:** Shall state that key audit matters are those of most significance in the audit and were addressed in the context of forming the opinion (no separate opinion on these matters).
- **Not a substitute for modified opinion:** The auditor shall not communicate a matter in the Key Audit Matters section when a modified opinion would be required as a result of that matter.
- **Description requirements:** Each key audit matter shall include a reference to related disclosure(s) in the financial statements and address:
  - Why the matter was considered one of most significance (and therefore a key audit matter)
  - How the matter was addressed in the audit
- **Exceptions to communication:** A key audit matter need not be described if law or regulation precludes public disclosure, or in extremely rare circumstances where adverse consequences would outweigh public interest benefits (unless the entity has publicly disclosed information about the matter).
- **Interaction with other report elements:** Matters giving rise to a modified opinion or a material uncertainty related to going concern are by their nature key audit matters but shall not be described in the Key Audit Matters section; instead, the auditor reports on them per the applicable ISAs and includes a cross-reference in the Key Audit Matters section.
- **No key audit matters:** If there are no key audit matters to communicate (or the only ones are those addressed by the interaction provision above), the auditor shall include a statement to this effect.

### Communication with Those Charged with Governance (para 17)

- The auditor shall communicate with those charged with governance the matters determined to be key audit matters, or the determination that there are no key audit matters.

### Documentation (para 18)

- The auditor shall include in audit documentation the matters that required significant auditor attention, the rationale for the determination, and where applicable, the rationale for not communicating a matter.

## Key Definitions

- **Key audit matters:** Those matters that, in the auditor's professional judgment, were of most significance in the audit of the financial statements of the current period. Key audit matters are selected from matters communicated with those charged with governance.
