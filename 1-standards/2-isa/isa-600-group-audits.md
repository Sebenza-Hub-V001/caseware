# ISA 600 (Revised) — Special Considerations—Audits of Group Financial Statements

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor are to:
- Determine whether sufficient appropriate audit evidence can reasonably be expected to be obtained to provide a basis for forming an opinion on the group financial statements (acceptance and continuance)
- Identify and assess the risks of material misstatement of the group financial statements and plan and perform further audit procedures to respond to those risks
- Be sufficiently and appropriately involved in the work of component auditors throughout the group audit, communicating clearly about scope and timing and evaluating results
- Evaluate whether sufficient appropriate audit evidence has been obtained as a basis for forming an opinion on the group financial statements

## Scope

This ISA deals with special considerations that apply to a group audit, including when component auditors are involved. Group financial statements include financial information of more than one entity or business unit through a consolidation process (consolidation, proportionate consolidation, equity method, combined financial statements, or aggregation of branches/divisions). The ISA applies to all group audits regardless of size or complexity, with requirements intended to be applied contextually.

Effective for periods beginning on or after December 15, 2023.

## Key Requirements

### Leadership and Quality
- The group engagement partner **shall** take overall responsibility for managing and achieving quality on the group audit (para. 16)

### Acceptance and Continuance
- The group engagement partner **shall** determine whether sufficient appropriate audit evidence can reasonably be expected to be obtained, considering the group auditor's access and ability to be involved in component auditors' work (paras. 17–21)

### Audit Strategy and Plan
- The group auditor **shall** establish an overall group audit strategy and group audit plan (paras. 22–29)
- The group auditor **shall** determine the nature, timing and extent of involvement of component auditors

### Understanding the Group
- The group auditor **shall** obtain understanding of the group and its environment, the applicable framework and the group's system of internal control (paras. 30–32)

### Risk Assessment
- The group auditor **shall** identify and assess risks of material misstatement of the group financial statements (paras. 33–34)

### Materiality
- The group auditor **shall** determine group materiality and group performance materiality, and component performance materiality where applicable (paras. 35–36)

### Responding to Risks
- The group auditor **shall** design and perform further audit procedures to respond to assessed risks, including determining the nature, timing and extent of work to be performed at components (paras. 37–44)

### Evaluating Component Auditor Work
- The group auditor **shall** evaluate communications from component auditors and the adequacy of their work (paras. 45–48)

### Subsequent Events
- The group auditor **shall** perform procedures regarding subsequent events for the group financial statements (paras. 49–50)

### Sufficiency of Evidence
- The group auditor **shall** evaluate whether sufficient appropriate audit evidence has been obtained (paras. 51–52)

### Communication
- The group auditor **shall** communicate with group management and those charged with governance about significant matters (paras. 54–58)

## Key Definitions

- **Aggregation risk** — Probability that aggregate of uncorrected and undetected misstatements exceeds materiality for the financial statements as a whole
- **Component** — An entity, business unit, function or business activity determined by the group auditor for audit purposes
- **Component auditor** — An auditor who performs audit work related to a component for the group audit (part of the engagement team)
- **Component management** — Management responsible for a component
- **Component performance materiality** — Amount set by the group auditor to reduce aggregation risk to an appropriately low level
- **Group** — A reporting entity for which group financial statements are prepared
- **Group auditor** — The group engagement partner and engagement team members other than component auditors
- **Group engagement partner** — The engagement partner responsible for the group audit
- **Group financial statements** — Financial statements including financial information of more than one entity or business unit through a consolidation process
- **Group management** — Management responsible for the preparation of group financial statements
- **Group performance materiality** — Performance materiality in relation to the group financial statements as a whole
