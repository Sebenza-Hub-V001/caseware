# ISA 560 — Subsequent Events

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor are:
- To obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether events occurring between the date of the financial statements and the date of the auditor's report that require adjustment of, or disclosure in, the financial statements are appropriately reflected in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework
- To respond appropriately to facts that become known after the date of the auditor's report that, had they been known at that date, may have caused the auditor to amend the report

## Scope

This ISA deals with the auditor's responsibilities relating to subsequent events in an audit of financial statements. It addresses three periods: (1) between the date of the financial statements and the date of the auditor's report; (2) after the auditor's report date but before financial statements are issued; (3) after financial statements have been issued. It does not deal with other information obtained after the auditor's report date (addressed in ISA 720 (Revised)).

## Key Requirements

### Events Between Financial Statement Date and Auditor's Report Date
- The auditor **shall** perform procedures to identify all events requiring adjustment or disclosure, covering the period from the financial statement date to the auditor's report date (or as near as practicable) (paras. 6–7)
- Procedures **shall** include: understanding management's procedures for identifying subsequent events, inquiring of management/those charged with governance, reading minutes of meetings held after the financial statement date, and reading latest interim financial statements (para. 7)
- If events are identified, the auditor **shall** determine whether each is appropriately reflected (para. 8)
- The auditor **shall** request a written representation that all subsequent events requiring adjustment or disclosure have been addressed (para. 9)

### Facts Known After Auditor's Report Date but Before Issuance
- If a fact becomes known that may have caused the auditor to amend the report, the auditor **shall** discuss with management, determine whether amendment is needed, and inquire how management intends to address it (para. 10)
- If management amends the financial statements, the auditor **shall** carry out necessary procedures on the amendment and provide a new auditor's report (para. 11)
- If management does not amend when the auditor believes amendment is needed, the auditor **shall** modify the opinion or take steps to prevent reliance on the report (para. 13)

### Facts Known After Financial Statements Have Been Issued
- The auditor has no obligation to perform procedures after issuance, but if a fact becomes known that would have caused amendment, the auditor **shall** discuss with management, determine whether amendment is needed, and if so, inquire how management intends to address it (para. 14)
- If management amends and reissues, the auditor **shall** perform necessary procedures and issue a new report (para. 15)
- If management does not take necessary action, the auditor **shall** take appropriate action to prevent reliance on the auditor's report (para. 17)

## Key Definitions

- **Date of the financial statements** — The date of the end of the latest period covered by the financial statements
- **Date of approval of the financial statements** — Date on which all statements have been prepared and those with authority have asserted responsibility
- **Date of the auditor's report** — The date the auditor dates the report on the financial statements
- **Date the financial statements are issued** — Date the auditor's report and audited financial statements are made available to third parties
- **Subsequent events** — Events occurring between the date of the financial statements and the date of the auditor's report, and facts that become known to the auditor after the date of the auditor's report
