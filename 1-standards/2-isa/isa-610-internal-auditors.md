# ISA 610 (Revised 2013) — Using the Work of Internal Auditors

## Objective

Where the entity has an internal audit function and the external auditor expects to use its work or use internal auditors to provide direct assistance, the objectives of the external auditor are:
- To determine whether the work of the internal audit function or direct assistance from internal auditors can be used, and if so, in which areas and to what extent
- If using the work of the internal audit function, to determine whether that work is adequate for purposes of the audit
- If using internal auditors to provide direct assistance, to appropriately direct, supervise and review their work

## Scope

This ISA deals with the external auditor's responsibilities if using the work of internal auditors, including: (a) using the work of the internal audit function in obtaining audit evidence and (b) using internal auditors to provide direct assistance under the external auditor's direction, supervision and review.

The ISA does not apply if the entity does not have an internal audit function, or if the function's activities are not relevant to the audit. The external auditor has sole responsibility for the audit opinion; this responsibility is not reduced by using internal auditors' work.

## Key Requirements

### Evaluating the Internal Audit Function
- The external auditor **shall** evaluate whether the function can be used by assessing: objectivity (organisational status and policies), competence level, and whether it applies a systematic and disciplined approach including quality control (para. 15)
- The external auditor **shall not** use the function's work if objectivity, competence, or systematic approach is inadequate (para. 16)

### Determining Nature and Extent of Use
- The external auditor **shall** consider the nature, scope and relevance of the internal audit function's work to the overall audit strategy and plan (para. 17)
- The external auditor **shall** plan to use less of the function's work and perform more directly when: more judgment is involved, higher assessed risk exists (especially significant risks), the function's objectivity is less supported, or competence is lower (para. 18)
- The external auditor **shall** evaluate whether aggregate planned use still results in sufficient involvement (para. 19)
- The external auditor **shall** communicate planned use to those charged with governance (para. 20)

### Using the Work
- The external auditor **shall** discuss planned use with the internal audit function to coordinate activities (para. 21)
- The external auditor **shall** read relevant reports to understand the nature and extent of procedures and findings (para. 22)
- The external auditor **shall** perform sufficient procedures on the body of work to determine its adequacy, evaluating whether it was properly planned/performed/supervised/reviewed/documented, whether sufficient evidence was obtained, and whether conclusions are appropriate (para. 23)

### Direct Assistance
- Before using internal auditors for direct assistance, the external auditor **shall** evaluate threats to objectivity and competence, and not use them if threats are too significant (paras. 29–30)
- The external auditor **shall** obtain written agreement from an authorised representative that internal auditors will be allowed to follow the external auditor's instructions and that the entity will not intervene (para. 31)
- The external auditor **shall** direct, supervise and review the work of internal auditors providing direct assistance (paras. 33–35)

## Key Definitions

- **Internal audit function** — A function of an entity that performs assurance and consulting activities designed to evaluate and improve the effectiveness of the entity's governance, risk management and internal control processes
- **Direct assistance** — The use of internal auditors to perform audit procedures under the direction, supervision and review of the external auditor
