# ISA 250 — Consideration of Laws and Regulations

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor are:

- To obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding compliance with the provisions of those laws and regulations generally recognized to have a direct effect on the determination of material amounts and disclosures in the financial statements;
- To perform specified audit procedures to help identify instances of non-compliance with other laws and regulations that may have a material effect on the financial statements; and
- To respond appropriately to identified or suspected non-compliance with laws and regulations identified during the audit.

## Scope

ISA 250 (Revised) deals with the auditor's responsibility to consider laws and regulations in an audit of financial statements. It does not apply to other assurance engagements where the auditor is specifically engaged to test and report on compliance with specific laws or regulations. The standard distinguishes between two categories of laws and regulations: those with a direct effect on financial statement amounts and disclosures, and those where non-compliance may have a material effect on the financial statements.

## Key Requirements

- **General understanding:** The auditor shall obtain a general understanding of the legal and regulatory framework applicable to the entity and the industry, and how the entity is complying with that framework.
- **Direct-effect laws and regulations (category a):** The auditor shall obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding compliance with provisions that directly affect material amounts and disclosures (e.g., tax, pension).
- **Other laws and regulations (category b):** The auditor shall perform specified procedures: inquire of management and those charged with governance about compliance, and inspect correspondence with relevant licensing or regulatory authorities.
- **Remaining alert:** During the audit, the auditor shall remain alert to the possibility that other audit procedures may bring instances of non-compliance to attention.
- **When non-compliance is identified or suspected:** The auditor shall obtain an understanding of the nature of the act and the circumstances, evaluate the effect on the financial statements, discuss with management and those charged with governance, and consider whether to obtain legal advice.
- **Communication:** The auditor shall communicate identified or suspected non-compliance to those charged with governance (unless trivial), and to management at an appropriate level. If the auditor suspects management or those charged with governance are involved, the auditor shall communicate to the next higher level of authority.
- **Reporting to authorities:** The auditor shall determine whether there is a responsibility to report identified or suspected non-compliance to an appropriate authority outside the entity.
- **Documentation:** The auditor shall document identified or suspected non-compliance and the results of discussions with management, those charged with governance and others.

## Key Definitions

- **Non-compliance** — Acts of omission or commission, intentional or unintentional, committed by the entity or by those charged with governance, management or other individuals working for or under the direction of the entity, which are contrary to the prevailing laws or regulations. Does not include personal misconduct unrelated to the business activities of the entity.
