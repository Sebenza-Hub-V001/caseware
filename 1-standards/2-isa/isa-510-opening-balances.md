# ISA 510 — Initial Audit Engagements—Opening Balances

## Objective

In conducting an initial audit engagement, the objective of the auditor with respect to opening balances is to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether:
- Opening balances contain misstatements that materially affect the current period's financial statements
- Appropriate accounting policies reflected in the opening balances have been consistently applied in the current period's financial statements, or changes thereto are appropriately accounted for and adequately presented and disclosed

## Scope

This ISA deals with the auditor's responsibilities relating to opening balances in an initial audit engagement. Opening balances include financial statement amounts and matters requiring disclosure that existed at the beginning of the period (such as contingencies and commitments). When financial statements include comparative information, ISA 710 also applies.

## Key Requirements

### Audit Procedures
- The auditor **shall** read the most recent financial statements and predecessor auditor's report for information relevant to opening balances (para. 5)
- The auditor **shall** obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether opening balances contain material misstatements by: determining correct carry-forward of closing balances, determining appropriate accounting policies, and performing specific procedures (reviewing predecessor's working papers, evaluating current period procedures, or performing specific procedures for opening balances) (para. 6)
- If misstatements are found in opening balances, the auditor **shall** perform additional procedures and communicate misstatements per ISA 450 (para. 7)
- The auditor **shall** obtain evidence about consistency of accounting policies (para. 8)
- If the predecessor auditor's report was modified, the auditor **shall** evaluate the effect of the matter on current period risk assessment (para. 9)

### Audit Conclusions and Reporting
- If unable to obtain sufficient evidence regarding opening balances, the auditor **shall** express a qualified opinion or disclaim (para. 10)
- If opening balances contain a material misstatement not appropriately addressed, the auditor **shall** express a qualified or adverse opinion (para. 11)

## Key Definitions

- **Initial audit engagement** — An engagement in which either the prior period financial statements were not audited, or were audited by a predecessor auditor
- **Opening balances** — Account balances at the beginning of the period, based on prior period closing balances, reflecting prior period transactions, events, and accounting policies (including contingencies and commitments)
- **Predecessor auditor** — The auditor from a different firm who audited the prior period financial statements
