# ISA 705 — Modifications to the Opinion in the Independent Auditor's Report

## Objective

The objective of the auditor is to express clearly an appropriately modified opinion on the financial statements that is necessary when:

- The auditor concludes, based on the audit evidence obtained, that the financial statements as a whole are not free from material misstatement; or
- The auditor is unable to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to conclude that the financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement.

## Scope

ISA 705 (Revised) deals with the auditor's responsibility to issue an appropriate report when a modification to the opinion is necessary. It establishes three types of modified opinions and addresses how the form and content of the auditor's report is affected. All reporting requirements in [[1-standards/2-isa/isa-700]] continue to apply alongside this standard.

The decision on which type of modified opinion is appropriate depends on: the nature of the matter (material misstatement vs. inability to obtain evidence) and the auditor's judgment about the pervasiveness of the effects on the financial statements.

## Key Requirements

### When Modification Is Required (para 6)

- The auditor shall modify the opinion when:
  - The financial statements as a whole are not free from material misstatement; or
  - The auditor is unable to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to conclude that they are free from material misstatement.

### Determining the Type of Modification (paras 7-10)

- **Qualified opinion:** When misstatements are material but not pervasive; or when inability to obtain evidence means possible effects could be material but not pervasive.
- **Adverse opinion:** When misstatements are both material and pervasive to the financial statements.
- **Disclaimer of opinion:** When inability to obtain evidence means possible effects could be both material and pervasive; or in extremely rare circumstances involving multiple uncertainties where it is not possible to form an opinion despite having evidence on each individual uncertainty.

### Management-Imposed Limitations (paras 11-14)

- The auditor shall request management to remove any limitation on audit scope that would likely result in a qualified opinion or disclaimer.
- If management refuses, the auditor shall communicate with those charged with governance and determine whether alternative procedures are possible.
- If unable to obtain sufficient evidence: qualify the opinion (if effects could be material but not pervasive) or withdraw from the engagement/disclaim an opinion (if effects could be both material and pervasive).
- Before withdrawing, the auditor shall communicate any misstatements identified that would have given rise to a modification.

### Other Considerations (para 15)

- When expressing an adverse opinion or disclaimer on the financial statements as a whole, the report shall not also include an unmodified opinion on a single financial statement or specific element under the same framework in the same report.

### Form and Content of Modified Report (paras 16-29)

- **Heading:** Use "Qualified Opinion," "Adverse Opinion," or "Disclaimer of Opinion" as appropriate.
- **Qualified opinion wording:** "except for the effects of the matter(s) described" (for misstatements) or "except for the possible effects of the matter(s)" (for inability to obtain evidence).
- **Adverse opinion wording:** "because of the significance of the matter(s) described... do not present fairly."
- **Disclaimer wording:** The auditor states they do not express an opinion, states inability to obtain sufficient evidence, and amends the report to state the auditor was "engaged to audit" rather than "audited."
- **Basis for [Modified] Opinion section:** Amend the heading appropriately, include a description of the matter giving rise to modification, and quantify financial effects where practicable.
- **Key Audit Matters:** When the auditor disclaims an opinion, KAM shall not be communicated unless required by law or regulation.
- **Auditor's responsibilities section in disclaimer:** Modified to state the auditor was engaged to audit and omit statements about reasonable assurance.
- **Communication:** The auditor shall communicate with those charged with governance the circumstances that led to the modification and the expected wording.

## Key Definitions

- **Pervasive:** Effects on the financial statements that (i) are not confined to specific elements, accounts or items; (ii) if confined, represent a substantial proportion of the financial statements; or (iii) in relation to disclosures, are fundamental to users' understanding.
- **Modified opinion:** A qualified opinion, an adverse opinion, or a disclaimer of opinion on the financial statements.
