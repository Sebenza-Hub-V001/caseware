# ISA 805 — Special Considerations: Audits of Single Financial Statements and Specific Elements, Accounts or Items of a Financial Statement

## Objective

The objective of the auditor, when applying ISAs in an audit of a single financial statement or of a specific element, account or item of a financial statement, is to address appropriately the special considerations that are relevant to:

- The acceptance of the engagement;
- The planning and performance of that engagement; and
- Forming an opinion and reporting on the single financial statement or on the specific element, account or item of a financial statement.

## Scope

ISA 805 (Revised) deals with special considerations in applying the ISAs in the 100-700 series to an audit of a single financial statement or of a specific element, account or item of a financial statement. The financial statement or element may be prepared in accordance with a general or special purpose framework. If prepared under a special purpose framework, [[1-standards/isa-800]] also applies.

This ISA does not apply to component auditor work on financial information for group audit purposes (see ISA 600 Revised). It does not override other ISA requirements.

## Key Requirements

### Accepting the Engagement (paras 7-9)

- **Application of ISAs:** All ISAs relevant to the audit apply regardless of whether the auditor also audits the entity's complete set of financial statements. If not also engaged on the complete set, the auditor shall determine whether the audit of the single statement or specific element in accordance with ISAs is practicable.
- **Acceptability of the framework:** Determine whether application of the financial reporting framework will result in a presentation providing adequate disclosures to enable intended users to understand the information conveyed and the effect of material transactions and events.
- **Form of opinion:** Consider whether the expected form of opinion is appropriate in the circumstances.

### Planning and Performing the Audit (para 10)

- The auditor shall adapt all ISAs relevant to the audit as necessary in the circumstances of the engagement. ISAs are written in the context of an audit of financial statements and must be adapted when applied to single financial statements or specific elements.

### Forming an Opinion and Reporting (paras 11-17)

- **Apply ISA 700 (Revised) and ISA 800 (Revised):** Adapted as necessary in the circumstances.
- **Separate opinion for each engagement:** If the auditor reports on both a single financial statement/element and the complete set, a separate opinion shall be expressed for each engagement.
- **Differentiation from complete set:** If the audited single statement or element is published with the complete set of audited financial statements, ensure sufficient differentiation. The auditor shall not issue the report until satisfied with the differentiation.
- **Consider implications of complete set report:** If the auditor's report on the complete set includes a modified opinion, EOM/OM paragraph, material uncertainty related to going concern, KAM, or uncorrected material misstatement of other information, consider the implications for the single statement/element audit and report.
- **Adverse opinion or disclaimer on complete set:**
  - ISA 705 does not permit an unmodified opinion on a single financial statement forming part of the complete set in the same report (would contradict the adverse/disclaimer opinion).
  - For a specific element, an unmodified opinion may be expressed only if: not prohibited by law/regulation; the report is not published together with the adverse/disclaimer report; and the element does not constitute a major portion of the complete set.
  - An unmodified opinion on a single financial statement is never permitted when the auditor has expressed an adverse opinion or disclaimed on the complete set, even in a separate report, because a single financial statement is deemed a major portion.

## Key Definitions

- **Element of a financial statement (or "element"):** An element, account or item of a financial statement. The related disclosures are included.
- Examples of specific elements include: accounts receivable, allowance for doubtful accounts, inventory, a pension obligation, a schedule of net tangible assets, a schedule of disbursements under a lease, or a schedule of profit participation or income taxes.

## Application to Probe Working Papers

No specific Probe working papers are currently mapped to ISA 805. The standard would be relevant when the auditor is engaged to report on a single financial statement or a specific element separately from the complete set of financial statements.
