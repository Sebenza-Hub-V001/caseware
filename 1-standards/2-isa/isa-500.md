# ISA 500 — Audit Evidence

## Objective

The objective of the auditor is to design and perform audit procedures in such a way as to enable the auditor to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to be able to draw reasonable conclusions on which to base the auditor's opinion.

## Scope

This ISA explains what constitutes audit evidence in an audit of financial statements, and deals with the auditor's responsibility to design and perform audit procedures to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence. It is applicable to all audit evidence obtained during the course of the audit. Other ISAs deal with specific aspects (e.g., ISA 315 (Revised 2019)), evidence for particular topics (e.g., ISA 570 (Revised)), specific procedures (e.g., ISA 520), and evaluation of sufficiency (ISA 200, ISA 330).

## Key Requirements

- The auditor **shall** design and perform audit procedures that are appropriate in the circumstances for obtaining sufficient appropriate audit evidence (para. 6)
- When designing and performing audit procedures, the auditor **shall** consider the relevance and reliability of information to be used as audit evidence, including from external information sources (para. 7)
- If information used as audit evidence has been prepared using a management's expert, the auditor **shall** evaluate the competence, capabilities and objectivity of that expert; obtain an understanding of the expert's work; and evaluate the appropriateness of that work as audit evidence (para. 8)
- When using information produced by the entity, the auditor **shall** evaluate whether the information is sufficiently reliable, including obtaining evidence about accuracy and completeness, and evaluating precision and detail (para. 9)
- The auditor **shall** determine means of selecting items for testing that are effective in meeting the purpose of the audit procedure (para. 10)
- If audit evidence from one source is inconsistent with another, or the auditor has doubts over reliability, the auditor **shall** determine what modifications or additions to audit procedures are necessary and consider the effect on other aspects of the audit (para. 11)

## Key Definitions

- **Accounting records** — Records of initial accounting entries and supporting records (checks, invoices, contracts, ledgers, journal entries, worksheets, spreadsheets)
- **Appropriateness (of audit evidence)** — The measure of the quality of audit evidence; its relevance and reliability in supporting the auditor's conclusions
- **Audit evidence** — Information used by the auditor in arriving at the conclusions on which the auditor's opinion is based, including information from accounting records and other sources
- **External information source** — An external individual or organization providing information used by the entity in preparing financial statements, or obtained by the auditor as audit evidence, suitable for a broad range of users
- **Management's expert** — An individual or organization with expertise in a field other than accounting or auditing, whose work is used by the entity to assist in preparing financial statements
- **Sufficiency (of audit evidence)** — The measure of the quantity of audit evidence, affected by risk assessment and quality of evidence
