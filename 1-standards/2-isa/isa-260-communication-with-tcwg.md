# ISA 260 — Communication with Those Charged with Governance

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor are:

- To communicate clearly with those charged with governance the responsibilities of the auditor in relation to the financial statement audit, and an overview of the planned scope and timing of the audit;
- To obtain from those charged with governance information relevant to the audit;
- To provide those charged with governance with timely observations arising from the audit that are significant and relevant to their responsibility to oversee the financial reporting process; and
- To promote effective two-way communication between the auditor and those charged with governance.

## Scope

ISA 260 (Revised) deals with the auditor's responsibility to communicate with those charged with governance in an audit of financial statements. It applies irrespective of an entity's governance structure or size, with particular considerations for entities where all those charged with governance are involved in management, and for listed entities. The standard provides an overarching framework for communication and identifies specific matters to be communicated. Additional communication matters are specified in other ISAs.

## Key Requirements

- **Identifying appropriate persons:** The auditor shall determine the appropriate person(s) within the entity's governance structure with whom to communicate. If communicating with a subgroup (e.g., audit committee), the auditor shall consider whether communication with the full governing body is also needed.
- **Auditor's responsibilities:** The auditor shall communicate the auditor's responsibilities in relation to the financial statement audit, including that the audit does not relieve management or those charged with governance of their responsibilities.
- **Planned scope and timing:** The auditor shall communicate an overview of the planned scope and timing of the audit, including significant risks identified.
- **Significant findings:** The auditor shall communicate: views about significant qualitative aspects of accounting practices; significant difficulties encountered during the audit; significant matters discussed with management; written representations requested; circumstances affecting the auditor's report; and any other significant matters relevant to oversight of financial reporting.
- **Auditor independence:** The auditor shall communicate relevant ethical requirements including independence. For listed entities, the auditor shall communicate a statement of compliance with independence requirements, all relationships that may bear on independence, and actions taken to address threats.
- **Communication process:** The auditor shall establish the form, timing and expected general content of communications. Significant findings must be communicated in writing if oral communication would be inadequate.
- **Timing:** Communications shall be on a timely basis.
- **Adequacy of communication:** The auditor shall evaluate whether two-way communication has been adequate for the purpose of the audit.
- **Documentation:** Where matters are communicated orally, the auditor shall include them in audit documentation. Written communications shall be retained.

## Key Definitions

- **Those charged with governance** — The person(s) or organization(s) with responsibility for overseeing the strategic direction of the entity and obligations related to the accountability of the entity, including overseeing the financial reporting process.
- **Management** — The person(s) with executive responsibility for the conduct of the entity's operations.
