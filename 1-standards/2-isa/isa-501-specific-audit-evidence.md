# ISA 501 — Audit Evidence—Specific Considerations for Selected Items

## Objective

The objective of the auditor is to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding the:
- Existence and condition of inventory
- Completeness of litigation and claims involving the entity
- Presentation and disclosure of segment information in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework

## Scope

This ISA deals with specific considerations by the auditor in obtaining sufficient appropriate audit evidence in accordance with ISA 330, ISA 500 and other relevant ISAs, with respect to certain aspects of inventory, litigation and claims involving the entity, and segment information in an audit of financial statements.

## Key Requirements

### Inventory
- If inventory is material, the auditor **shall** obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding existence and condition by attending physical inventory counting (unless impracticable) and performing audit procedures over final inventory records (para. 4)
- At physical inventory counting, the auditor **shall** evaluate management's instructions and procedures, observe count procedures, inspect inventory, and perform test counts (para. 4)
- If counting is at a date other than the financial statement date, the auditor **shall** perform procedures on changes in inventory between the dates (para. 5)
- If unable to attend due to unforeseen circumstances, the auditor **shall** make or observe counts on an alternative date (para. 6)
- If attendance is impracticable, the auditor **shall** perform alternative procedures or modify the opinion (para. 7)
- For material inventory held by third parties, the auditor **shall** request confirmation or perform inspection/other procedures (para. 8)

### Litigation and Claims
- The auditor **shall** design and perform procedures to identify litigation and claims that may give rise to risk of material misstatement, including inquiry of management, reviewing minutes, and reviewing legal expense accounts (para. 9)
- Where risk of material misstatement is assessed regarding litigation or claims, the auditor **shall** seek direct communication with external legal counsel through a letter of inquiry (para. 10)
- The auditor **shall** request written representations that all known litigation and claims have been disclosed and properly accounted for (para. 12)

### Segment Information
- The auditor **shall** obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding presentation and disclosure of segment information, including understanding and testing management's methods (para. 13)
