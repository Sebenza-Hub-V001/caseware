# ISA 530 — Audit Sampling

## Objective

The objective of the auditor, when using audit sampling, is to provide a reasonable basis for the auditor to draw conclusions about the population from which the sample is selected.

## Scope

This ISA applies when the auditor has decided to use audit sampling in performing audit procedures. It deals with the use of statistical and non-statistical sampling when designing and selecting the audit sample, performing tests of controls and tests of details, and evaluating results from the sample. It complements ISA 500, which provides guidance on means available for selecting items for testing.

## Key Requirements

### Sample Design, Size and Selection
- The auditor **shall** consider the purpose of the audit procedure and the characteristics of the population when designing an audit sample (para. 6)
- The auditor **shall** determine a sample size sufficient to reduce sampling risk to an acceptably low level (para. 7)
- The auditor **shall** select items so that each sampling unit has a chance of selection (para. 8)

### Performing Audit Procedures
- The auditor **shall** perform appropriate audit procedures on each item selected (para. 9)
- If a procedure is not applicable to a selected item, the auditor **shall** perform it on a replacement item (para. 10)
- If unable to apply designed or alternative procedures to a selected item, the auditor **shall** treat that item as a deviation (tests of controls) or misstatement (tests of details) (para. 11)

### Nature and Cause of Deviations and Misstatements
- The auditor **shall** investigate the nature and cause of any deviations or misstatements identified, and evaluate their possible effect on the audit procedure purpose and other audit areas (para. 12)
- In extremely rare circumstances where a misstatement or deviation is considered an anomaly, the auditor **shall** obtain a high degree of certainty that it is not representative of the population (para. 13)

### Projecting Misstatements
- For tests of details, the auditor **shall** project misstatements found in the sample to the population (para. 14)

### Evaluating Results
- The auditor **shall** evaluate the results of the sample and whether use of audit sampling has provided a reasonable basis for conclusions about the population (para. 15)

## Key Definitions

- **Audit sampling (sampling)** — Application of audit procedures to less than 100% of items within a population such that all sampling units have a chance of selection, to provide a reasonable basis for conclusions about the entire population
- **Population** — The entire set of data from which a sample is selected
- **Sampling risk** — Risk that the auditor's conclusion based on a sample may differ from the conclusion if the entire population were tested (leads to two types of erroneous conclusions)
- **Non-sampling risk** — Risk of erroneous conclusion for reasons not related to sampling risk
- **Anomaly** — A misstatement or deviation demonstrably not representative of the population
- **Sampling unit** — Individual items constituting a population
- **Statistical sampling** — Random selection of items and use of probability theory to evaluate results
- **Stratification** — Dividing a population into sub-populations with similar characteristics
- **Tolerable misstatement** — Monetary amount set by the auditor to obtain assurance that actual misstatement does not exceed it
- **Tolerable rate of deviation** — Rate of deviation set by the auditor to obtain assurance that the actual rate does not exceed it
