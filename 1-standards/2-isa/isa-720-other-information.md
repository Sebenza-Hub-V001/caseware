# ISA 720 — The Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Other Information

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor, having read the other information, are:

- To consider whether there is a material inconsistency between the other information and the financial statements;
- To consider whether there is a material inconsistency between the other information and the auditor's knowledge obtained in the audit;
- To respond appropriately when such material inconsistencies appear to exist, or when the auditor otherwise becomes aware that other information appears to be materially misstated; and
- To report in accordance with this ISA.

## Scope

ISA 720 (Revised) deals with the auditor's responsibilities relating to other information, whether financial or non-financial (other than financial statements and the auditor's report thereon), included in an entity's annual report. The auditor's opinion does not cover the other information, nor does the ISA require obtaining audit evidence beyond that needed for the opinion on the financial statements.

The standard requires the auditor to read and consider other information because material inconsistencies may indicate a material misstatement of the financial statements or of the other information, undermining the credibility of both the financial statements and the auditor's report.

The ISA does not apply to preliminary announcements of financial information or securities offering documents/prospectuses. The auditor's responsibilities do not constitute an assurance engagement on other information.

## Key Requirements

### Obtaining the Other Information (para 13)

- The auditor shall determine which documents comprise the annual report, through discussion with management, and the planned timing of issuance.
- Make appropriate arrangements to obtain the final version of the annual report documents in a timely manner, if possible before the auditor's report date.
- When documents will not be available until after the auditor's report date, request a written representation that the final version will be provided when available and prior to issuance.

### Reading and Considering the Other Information (paras 14-15)

- **Read the other information** and:
  - Consider whether there is a material inconsistency between the other information and the financial statements (compare selected amounts or other items)
  - Consider whether there is a material inconsistency between the other information and the auditor's knowledge obtained in the audit
- **Remain alert** for indications that other information not related to the financial statements or the auditor's knowledge appears to be materially misstated.

### Responding to Apparent Material Inconsistency (para 16)

- If a material inconsistency appears to exist or other information appears to be materially misstated, discuss with management and perform other procedures as necessary to conclude whether: a material misstatement of the other information exists; a material misstatement of the financial statements exists; or the auditor's understanding needs to be updated.

### Responding to Material Misstatement of Other Information (paras 17-19)

- **Request correction:** If a material misstatement exists, request management to correct it. If management refuses, communicate with those charged with governance and request correction.
- **Before auditor's report date (uncorrected):** Consider implications for the auditor's report; or withdraw from the engagement where possible.
- **After auditor's report date:** If corrected, perform necessary procedures. If not corrected, take appropriate action to bring the misstatement to users' attention.

### Responding to Financial Statement Misstatement (para 20)

- If the procedures reveal a material misstatement in the financial statements or the need to update the auditor's understanding, respond in accordance with other ISAs.

### Reporting (paras 21-24)

- **Other Information section:** Required in the auditor's report when the auditor has obtained (or for listed entities, expects to obtain) other information at the report date.
- **Required content:** Statement that management is responsible for the other information; identification of other information obtained (and for listed entities, expected to be obtained); statement that the auditor's opinion does not cover the other information; description of the auditor's responsibility to read and consider the other information.
- **Material misstatement reporting:** If the auditor concludes that a material misstatement of the other information exists (obtained before report date), describe it in the Other Information section.
- **Inability to conclude:** If the auditor is unable to conclude whether a material misstatement exists, explain this in the Other Information section.

### Documentation (para 25)

- Document the procedures performed relating to the other information and the final version of the other information on which those procedures were performed.

## Key Definitions

- **Annual report:** A document (or combination of documents) prepared typically on an annual basis by management or those charged with governance, providing owners with information on the entity's operations and financial results/position. Contains or accompanies the financial statements and auditor's report.
- **Misstatement of the other information:** Exists when the other information is incorrectly stated or otherwise misleading (including because it omits or obscures information necessary for proper understanding).
- **Other information:** Financial or non-financial information (other than financial statements and the auditor's report thereon) included in an entity's annual report.

## Application to Probe Working Papers

No specific Probe working papers are currently mapped to ISA 720. The standard's requirements would be applied as part of the finalisation process when the entity's annual report contains other information alongside the audited financial statements.
