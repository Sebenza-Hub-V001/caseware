# ISA 330 — The Auditor's Responses to Assessed Risks

## Objective

The objective of the auditor is to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding the assessed risks of material misstatement, through designing and implementing appropriate responses to those risks. (Para. 3)

## Scope

ISA 330 deals with the auditor's responsibility to design and implement responses to the risks of material misstatement identified and assessed by the auditor in accordance with [[1-standards/2-isa/isa-315-risk-identification]]. It covers overall responses at the financial statement level, further audit procedures at the assertion level (including tests of controls and substantive procedures), evaluating the adequacy of presentation of the financial statements, and evaluating the sufficiency and appropriateness of audit evidence.

## Key Requirements

### Overall Responses (Para. 5)
- The auditor shall design and implement overall responses to address the assessed risks of material misstatement at the financial statement level.

### Further Audit Procedures at the Assertion Level (Para. 6-7)
- The auditor shall design and perform further audit procedures whose nature, timing and extent are based on and responsive to the assessed risks of material misstatement at the assertion level.
- The auditor shall consider the reasons for the assessment given to the risk (inherent risk and control risk) and obtain more persuasive audit evidence the higher the assessment of risk.

### Tests of Controls (Para. 8-17)
- The auditor shall design and perform tests of controls if:
  - The risk assessment includes an expectation that controls are operating effectively; or
  - Substantive procedures alone cannot provide sufficient appropriate audit evidence
- More persuasive audit evidence is required the greater the reliance placed on a control's effectiveness.
- Tests of controls shall include inquiry combined with other procedures (observation, inspection, reperformance) to evaluate how controls were applied, their consistency, and by whom they were applied.
- **Timing** (Para. 11-14): Controls shall be tested for the time/period the auditor intends to rely on them. If evidence is from an interim period, obtain evidence about changes and determine additional evidence needed. Audit evidence from previous audits may be used subject to specific conditions, but controls must be tested at least once every third audit.
- **Controls over significant risks** (Para. 15): If relying on controls over a significant risk, those controls shall be tested in the current period.
- **Evaluating effectiveness** (Para. 16-17): If deviations are detected, determine whether tests provide an appropriate basis for reliance, whether additional tests are necessary, or whether substantive procedures must address the risks.

### Substantive Procedures (Para. 18-23)
- The auditor shall design and perform substantive procedures for each material class of transactions, account balance and disclosure, irrespective of the assessed risks of material misstatement.
- The auditor shall consider whether external confirmation procedures should be performed.
- **Closing process** (Para. 20): Substantive procedures shall include agreeing/reconciling financial statement information with underlying accounting records and examining material journal entries and adjustments.
- **Significant risks** (Para. 21): Substantive procedures specifically responsive to significant risks shall be performed; if the approach consists only of substantive procedures, these shall include tests of details.
- **Timing** (Para. 22-23): If substantive procedures are performed at an interim date, the remaining period shall be covered by further substantive procedures (combined with tests of controls or alone).

### Adequacy of Presentation (Para. 24)
- The auditor shall perform audit procedures to evaluate whether the overall presentation of the financial statements is in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework.

### Evaluating Sufficiency and Appropriateness of Audit Evidence (Para. 25-27)
- The auditor shall evaluate before the conclusion of the audit whether the risk assessments at the assertion level remain appropriate.
- The auditor shall conclude whether sufficient appropriate audit evidence has been obtained, considering all relevant audit evidence (both corroborative and contradictory).
- If insufficient evidence is obtained, the auditor shall attempt to obtain further evidence or express a qualified opinion or disclaim an opinion.

### Documentation (Para. 28-30)
- The overall responses, the nature/timing/extent of further audit procedures, and the linkage of those procedures with assessed risks at the assertion level
- Results of audit procedures, including conclusions where not otherwise clear
- Conclusions about relying on controls tested in previous audits
- Demonstration that information in the financial statements agrees or reconciles with underlying accounting records

## Key Definitions

- **Substantive procedure** — An audit procedure designed to detect material misstatements at the assertion level. Comprises: (i) tests of details (of classes of transactions, account balances and disclosures); and (ii) substantive analytical procedures.
- **Test of controls** — An audit procedure designed to evaluate the operating effectiveness of controls in preventing, or detecting and correcting, material misstatements at the assertion level.
