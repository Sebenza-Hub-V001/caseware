# ISRE 2410 — Review of Interim Financial Information Performed by the Independent Auditor of the Entity

## Objective

The objective of an engagement to review interim financial information is to enable the auditor to express a conclusion whether, on the basis of the review, anything has come to the auditor's attention that causes the auditor to believe that the interim financial information is not prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with an applicable financial reporting framework.

The auditor makes inquiries and performs analytical and other review procedures to reduce to a moderate level the risk of expressing an inappropriate conclusion when the interim financial information is materially misstated.

## Scope

This ISRE establishes standards and provides guidance on the auditor's professional responsibilities when the auditor undertakes an engagement to review **interim financial information of an audit client**, and on the form and content of the report.

Key scope points:
- Limited to reviews performed by the **independent auditor** of the entity (not a separate practitioner — that is covered by [[1-standards/isre-2400]])
- Interim financial information is financial information prepared for a period shorter than the entity's financial year (complete or condensed)
- The auditor's existing understanding of the entity from the annual audit assists in focusing inquiries and procedures
- May also be applied, adapted as necessary, to reviews of other historical financial information by the entity's auditor

Effective for reviews of interim financial information for periods beginning on or after **December 15, 2006**.

## Key Requirements

1. **General principles** — Comply with ethical requirements relevant to the annual audit (independence, integrity, objectivity, competence, confidentiality, professional behavior)
2. **Quality control** — Implement quality control procedures applicable to the individual engagement
3. **Professional skepticism** — Plan and perform with professional skepticism
4. **Terms of engagement** — Agree on terms with the client (engagement letter covering objective, scope, management responsibilities, assurance obtained, report form)
5. **Procedures for the review** — Include:
   - Inquiries of persons responsible for financial and accounting matters
   - Analytical procedures applied to interim financial data
   - Reading minutes of meetings
   - Considering the effect of matters giving rise to audit modifications or significant audit findings
   - Reading other interim financial information for consistency
6. **Evaluation of misstatements** — Evaluate uncorrected misstatements individually and in aggregate
7. **Management representations** — Obtain written representations
8. **Communication** — Communicate matters of governance interest to those charged with governance
9. **Reporting** — Express conclusion in review report (unmodified, qualified, adverse, or disclaimer)
10. **Documentation** — Document the review

The objective differs significantly from an audit — a review does **not** provide a basis for expressing an opinion on whether financial information gives a true and fair view. It is designed to provide **limited (negative) assurance**.

## Key Definitions

- **Interim financial information** — Financial information prepared and presented in accordance with an applicable financial reporting framework comprising either a complete or condensed set of financial statements for a period shorter than the entity's financial year
- **Moderate level of assurance** — Less than reasonable assurance but more than no assurance; negative form of conclusion

## Application to Probe Working Papers

This standard is not currently referenced by any Probe working papers. However, for firms that audit entities and also review their interim financial information, integration between Probe audit templates and interim review templates would be relevant, since the auditor leverages knowledge from the annual audit.
