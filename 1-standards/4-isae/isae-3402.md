# ISAE 3402 — Assurance Reports on Controls at a Service Organization

## Objective

The objectives of the service auditor are to obtain reasonable assurance about whether, in all material respects, based on suitable criteria:

1. The service organization's description of its system **fairly presents** the system as designed and implemented throughout the specified period (or as at a specified date for a Type 1 report);
2. The controls related to the control objectives stated in the description **were suitably designed** throughout the specified period (or as at a specified date for a Type 1 report);
3. Where included in scope (Type 2 only), the controls **operated effectively** to provide reasonable assurance that the control objectives were achieved throughout the specified period.

## Scope

This ISAE deals with assurance engagements undertaken by a service auditor to provide a report for use by **user entities and their auditors** on the controls at a service organization that provides a service likely to be relevant to user entities' internal control as it relates to financial reporting.

Key scope points:
- Complements [[1-standards/isa-315]] and ISA 402, as reports under this ISAE provide evidence usable under ISA 402
- Deals only with **reasonable assurance attestation engagements**
- Applies only when the service organization is responsible for, or able to make a statement about, the suitable design of controls
- Does **not** deal with:
  - Reports only on whether controls operated as described
  - Reports on controls not related to financial reporting (e.g., production or quality controls)
  - Reports on user entity transactions/balances maintained by a service organization
  - Agreed-upon procedures reports on controls

Effective for assurance reports covering periods ending on or after **June 15, 2011**.

## Key Requirements

1. **ISAE 3000 (Revised) compliance** — The service auditor must comply with both ISAE 3000 (Revised) and this ISAE
2. **Ethical requirements** — Compliance with IESBA Code including independence
3. **Acceptance and continuance** — Evaluate whether the engagement can be performed, including adequacy of criteria
4. **Determining suitability of criteria** — Assess whether criteria are suitable (description fairly presents, controls suitably designed, controls operating effectively)
5. **Materiality** — Determine materiality for the engagement
6. **Understanding the system** — Obtain understanding of the service organization's system, including controls
7. **Evidence regarding description** — Obtain evidence that the description fairly presents the system
8. **Evidence regarding design of controls** — Evaluate whether controls are suitably designed to achieve control objectives
9. **Evidence regarding operating effectiveness** (Type 2 only) — Test controls over the period, including nature, timing, and extent of tests
10. **Internal audit function** — May use the work of the service organization's internal audit function
11. **Written representations** — Obtain from the service organization
12. **Other information and subsequent events** — Consider other information and events after the period
13. **Documentation** — Document sufficient to enable understanding of the engagement
14. **Reporting** — Prepare the service auditor's assurance report (Type 1 or Type 2)

### Type 1 vs. Type 2 Reports

| Aspect | Type 1 | Type 2 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Description | As at a specified date | Throughout a specified period |
| Design of controls | Assessed | Assessed |
| Operating effectiveness | Not tested | Tested |

## Key Definitions

- **Service organization** — An organization that provides services to user entities that are part of those entities' information systems relevant to financial reporting
- **User entity** — An entity that uses a service organization and whose financial statements are being audited
- **Service auditor** — A practitioner who provides an assurance report on controls at a service organization
- **Control objective** — The aim of a control, as stated in the service organization's description of its system
- **Type 1 report** — Report on the description and design of controls at a service organization
- **Type 2 report** — Report on the description, design, and operating effectiveness of controls at a service organization

## Application to Probe Working Papers

This standard is not currently referenced by Probe working papers directly, but is closely related to audit work. When auditing entities that use service organizations, auditors rely on ISAE 3402 reports (SOC 1 reports in the US context) as part of their risk assessment and controls testing under ISA 402. Understanding this standard helps auditors evaluate whether such reports are sufficient and appropriate audit evidence.
