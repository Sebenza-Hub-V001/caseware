# ISAE 3000 (Revised) — Assurance Engagements Other than Audits or Reviews of Historical Financial Information

## Objective

In conducting an assurance engagement, the objectives of the practitioner are:

1. To obtain either **reasonable assurance or limited assurance**, as appropriate, about whether the subject matter information is free from material misstatement;
2. To express a conclusion regarding the outcome of the measurement or evaluation of the underlying subject matter through a written report that conveys either a reasonable assurance or a limited assurance conclusion and describes the basis for the conclusion; and
3. To communicate further as required by this ISAE and any other relevant ISAE.

When assurance cannot be obtained and a qualified conclusion is insufficient, the practitioner must disclaim a conclusion or withdraw from the engagement.

## Scope

ISAE 3000 (Revised) covers **assurance engagements other than audits or reviews of historical financial information**, as described in the International Framework for Assurance Engagements.

Key scope points:
- Covers both **attestation engagements** (third party measures/evaluates) and **direct engagements** (practitioner measures/evaluates), though specific requirements focus on attestation engagements
- Covers both **reasonable assurance** and **limited assurance** engagements
- Where a subject-matter specific ISAE exists (e.g., [[1-standards/isae-3402]], [[1-standards/isae-3410]], [[1-standards/isae-3420]]), that ISAE applies **in addition** to this ISAE
- Does **not** cover: agreed-upon procedures ([[1-standards/isrs-4400]]), compilation engagements ([[1-standards/isrs-4410]]), tax return preparation, consulting/advisory engagements, legal testimony

Effective for assurance reports dated on or after **December 15, 2015**.

Note: For sustainability assurance engagements, [[1-standards/issa-5000]] is a standalone standard and practitioners are not required to apply ISAE 3000 (Revised).

## Key Requirements

1. **Ethical requirements** — Comply with IESBA Code or equivalent requirements
2. **Acceptance and continuance** — Evaluate preconditions, rational purpose, subject matter appropriateness, criteria suitability, access to evidence, written agreement
3. **Quality management** — Firm-level (ISQM 1) and engagement-level quality management
4. **Professional skepticism, judgment, and assurance skills** — Apply throughout
5. **Planning and performing** — Develop overall strategy and engagement plan, consider materiality
6. **Obtaining evidence** — Design and perform procedures to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence, including:
   - Risk assessment procedures
   - Further procedures responsive to assessed risks
   - Analytical procedures
   - Inquiries, inspection, observation, recalculation, re-performance, confirmation
7. **Subsequent events** — Consider events up to the assurance report date
8. **Other information** — Consider other information included in documents containing the subject matter information
9. **Forming conclusion** — Evaluate evidence, form conclusion
10. **Reporting** — Prepare assurance report (unmodified or modified conclusions: qualified, adverse, disclaimer)
11. **Documentation** — Document the engagement sufficiently

## Key Definitions

- **Assurance engagement** — An engagement in which a practitioner aims to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence to express a conclusion designed to enhance the degree of confidence of intended users about the subject matter information
- **Attestation engagement** — A party other than the practitioner measures or evaluates the underlying subject matter against criteria
- **Direct engagement** — The practitioner measures or evaluates the underlying subject matter against criteria
- **Subject matter information** — The outcome of the measurement or evaluation of the underlying subject matter against criteria
- **Underlying subject matter** — The phenomenon that is measured or evaluated by applying criteria

## Application to Probe Working Papers

This standard is not currently referenced by any Probe working papers, which focus on audit engagements under the ISAs. However, ISAE 3000 is the foundational standard for non-audit assurance engagements. If CaseWare develops Probe templates for any assurance engagement that is not an audit, review, or sustainability assurance engagement, this standard would be the primary reference.
