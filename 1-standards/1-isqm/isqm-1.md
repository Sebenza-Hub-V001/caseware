# ISQM 1 — Quality Management for Firms

## Objective

The objective of the firm is to design, implement and operate a system of quality management for audits or reviews of financial statements, or other assurance or related services engagements performed by the firm, that provides the firm with reasonable assurance that:

- The firm and its personnel fulfill their responsibilities in accordance with professional standards and applicable legal and regulatory requirements, and conduct engagements in accordance with such standards and requirements; and
- Engagement reports issued by the firm or engagement partners are appropriate in the circumstances.

## Scope

ISQM 1 deals with a firm's responsibilities to design, implement and operate a system of quality management for audits or reviews of financial statements, or other assurance or related services engagements. It applies to all firms performing such engagements. The standard requires a risk-based approach and at least annual evaluation of the system.

## Key Requirements

- **Risk-based approach:** The firm shall apply a risk-based approach in designing, implementing and operating the components of the system of quality management.
- **Eight components of the system:** The firm's risk assessment process; governance and leadership; relevant ethical requirements; acceptance and continuance of client relationships; engagement performance; resources; information and communication; and the monitoring and remediation process.
- **Quality objectives:** The firm shall establish quality objectives — desired outcomes for each component of the system.
- **Quality risk identification:** The firm shall identify and assess quality risks that provide a basis for designing and implementing responses.
- **Responses to quality risks:** The firm shall design and implement responses whose nature, timing and extent are based on the quality risk assessments.
- **Annual evaluation:** At least annually, the individual(s) with ultimate responsibility shall evaluate the system and conclude whether it provides reasonable assurance.
- **Network requirements:** When the firm belongs to a network or uses service providers, the firm remains responsible for its own system of quality management.
- **Documentation:** The firm shall document its system of quality management, including quality objectives, quality risks, responses, monitoring activities, and evaluation conclusions.

## Key Definitions

- **Quality objectives** — The desired outcomes in relation to the components of the system of quality management to be achieved by the firm.
- **Quality risk** — A risk that has a reasonable possibility of occurring and, individually or in combination with other risks, adversely affecting the achievement of one or more quality objectives.
- **Deficiency** — Exists when a quality objective is not established, a quality risk is not identified or properly assessed, a response does not reduce quality risk to an acceptably low level, or another aspect of the system is absent or not properly designed/implemented/operating.
- **Response** — Policies or procedures designed and implemented by the firm to address one or more quality risks.
- **Engagement quality review** — An objective evaluation of the significant judgments made by the engagement team and the conclusions reached thereon.
- **Reasonable assurance** — A high, but not absolute, level of assurance.

## Application to Probe Working Papers

ISQM 1 is a firm-level standard rather than an engagement-level standard. It provides the foundation on which [[1-standards/2-isa/isa-220]] (quality management at the engagement level) operates. The Probe methodology implements firm-level quality management through the overall file structure and engagement templates.
