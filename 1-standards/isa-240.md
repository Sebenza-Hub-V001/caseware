# ISA 240 — Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Fraud

## Objective

The objectives of the auditor are:

- To identify and assess the risks of material misstatement of the financial statements due to fraud;
- To obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding the assessed risks of material misstatement due to fraud, through designing and implementing appropriate responses; and
- To respond appropriately to fraud or suspected fraud identified during the audit.

## Scope

ISA 240 deals with the auditor's responsibilities relating to fraud in an audit of financial statements. It expands on how ISA 315 (Revised 2019) and ISA 330 are to be applied in relation to risks of material misstatement due to fraud. The primary responsibility for prevention and detection of fraud rests with management and those charged with governance.

## Key Requirements

- **Professional skepticism:** The auditor shall maintain professional skepticism throughout the audit, recognizing the possibility that a material misstatement due to fraud could exist, notwithstanding past experience of management's honesty.
- **Engagement team discussion:** The discussion among engagement team members required by ISA 315 shall place particular emphasis on how and where the financial statements may be susceptible to material misstatement due to fraud, setting aside assumptions about management's integrity.
- **Inquiries of management:** The auditor shall inquire about management's assessment of fraud risk, processes for identifying and responding to fraud risks, communications to those charged with governance, and communications to employees about business practices and ethical behavior.
- **Inquiries of others:** The auditor shall inquire of others within the entity (and internal audit, if applicable) about knowledge of actual, suspected or alleged fraud.
- **Those charged with governance:** The auditor shall obtain an understanding of their oversight of management's fraud risk processes and make inquiries about knowledge of fraud.
- **Evaluation of fraud risk factors:** The auditor shall evaluate whether information obtained from risk assessment procedures and related activities indicates fraud risk factors are present.
- **Presumption of revenue recognition fraud risk:** The auditor shall treat the risk of material misstatement due to fraud related to revenue recognition as a significant risk (rebuttable presumption).
- **Responses to assessed fraud risks:** The auditor shall design overall responses and audit procedures responsive to the assessed risks of material misstatement due to fraud at the assertion level.
- **Management override of controls:** Regardless of the assessed risks, the auditor shall design and perform procedures to test the appropriateness of journal entries, review accounting estimates for bias, and evaluate the business rationale for significant unusual transactions.
- **Evaluation of audit evidence:** The auditor shall evaluate whether analytical procedures performed near the end of the audit indicate previously unrecognized fraud risks.
- **Written representations:** The auditor shall obtain written representations from management regarding fraud.
- **Communication:** The auditor shall communicate fraud matters to management, those charged with governance, and (where required) appropriate authorities outside the entity.
- **Documentation:** The auditor shall document communications about fraud, fraud risk factors identified, the assessment of fraud risks and responses, results of procedures addressing management override, and other conditions suggesting fraud.

## Key Definitions

- **Fraud** — An intentional act by one or more individuals among management, those charged with governance, employees, or third parties, involving the use of deception to obtain an unjust or illegal advantage.
- **Fraud risk factors** — Events or conditions that indicate an incentive or pressure to commit fraud or provide an opportunity to commit fraud.
