# ISA 220 — Quality Management for an Audit

## Objective

The objective of the auditor is to manage quality at the engagement level to obtain reasonable assurance that quality has been achieved such that:

- The auditor has fulfilled the auditor's responsibilities, and has conducted the audit, in accordance with professional standards and applicable legal and regulatory requirements; and
- The auditor's report issued is appropriate in the circumstances.

## Scope

ISA 220 (Revised) deals with the specific responsibilities of the auditor regarding quality management at the engagement level for an audit of financial statements, and the related responsibilities of the engagement partner. It operates within the context of the firm's system of quality management under [[standards/isqm-1]].

## Key Requirements

- **Leadership responsibilities:** The engagement partner shall take overall responsibility for managing and achieving quality on the audit engagement, including creating an environment that emphasises the firm's culture, professional ethics, open communication, and professional skepticism.
- **Delegation:** If the engagement partner assigns procedures to other engagement team members, the partner shall continue to take overall responsibility through direction, supervision and review.
- **Ethical requirements and independence:** The engagement partner shall have an understanding of relevant ethical requirements and take responsibility for other engagement team members being made aware of them. The partner shall identify, evaluate and address threats to independence.
- **Acceptance and continuance:** The engagement partner shall determine that acceptance and continuance procedures have been followed and that conclusions reached are appropriate.
- **Engagement resources:** The engagement partner shall determine that sufficient and appropriate resources are assigned or made available to the engagement.
- **Engagement performance:** The engagement partner shall take responsibility for direction, supervision and review; ensure consultation on difficult or contentious matters; and determine that engagement quality review (if required) has been completed.
- **Monitoring and remediation:** The engagement partner shall consider relevant information from the firm's monitoring and remediation process and its effect on the audit.
- **Documentation:** The auditor shall include in audit documentation matters identified and how they were resolved, including consultation and its conclusions.

## Key Definitions

- **Engagement partner** — The partner or other individual appointed by the firm who is responsible for the audit engagement and its performance, and for the auditor's report issued on behalf of the firm.
- **Engagement team** — All partners and staff performing the audit engagement, and any other individuals who perform audit procedures on the engagement (excluding auditor's external experts and internal auditors providing direct assistance).
- **Professional standards** — International Standards on Auditing and relevant ethical requirements.
- **Relevant ethical requirements** — Principles of professional ethics and ethical requirements applicable to professional accountants when undertaking the audit engagement.
