# ISRS 4410 (Revised) — Compilation Engagements

## Objective

The practitioner's objectives in a compilation engagement under this ISRS are to:

1. Apply **accounting and financial reporting expertise** to assist management in the preparation and presentation of financial information in accordance with an applicable financial reporting framework based on information provided by management; and
2. **Report** in accordance with the requirements of this ISRS.

## Scope

This ISRS deals with the practitioner's responsibilities when engaged to assist management with the preparation and presentation of historical financial information **without obtaining any assurance** on that information, and to report on the engagement.

Key scope points:
- Applies to compilation engagements for **historical financial information**
- May be applied, adapted as necessary, to compilation engagements for financial information other than historical, and to non-financial information
- A compilation engagement is **not an assurance engagement** — the practitioner does not verify accuracy or completeness, and does not express an opinion or conclusion
- Management retains responsibility for the financial information and its basis of preparation
- Factors indicating it may be appropriate to apply this ISRS include:
  - Financial information required under law/regulation or publicly filed
  - External parties may associate the practitioner with the information
  - Risk that the practitioner's involvement may be misunderstood
- ISQM 1 applies at the firm level

Effective for compilation engagement reports dated on or after **July 1, 2013**.

## Key Requirements

1. **Ethical requirements** — Comply with relevant ethical requirements (objectivity and competence always required; independence may not be required for compilation engagements depending on jurisdiction)
2. **Professional judgment** — Exercise professional judgment throughout
3. **Quality management** — Managing and achieving quality on the engagement
4. **Engagement acceptance and continuance** — Evaluate:
   - Whether the financial reporting framework is acceptable
   - Management's understanding and agreement regarding responsibilities
   - Competence of the practitioner
5. **Communication** — Communicate with management and those charged with governance on matters of significance
6. **Performing the engagement** — Including:
   - Obtaining an understanding of the entity's business and the applicable financial reporting framework
   - Compiling the financial information using records, documents, and information provided by management
   - Considering whether the financial information appears appropriate in form and free from obvious material misstatement
   - If the practitioner becomes aware the information is misleading, request management to make revisions; if management refuses, withdraw
7. **Documentation** — Document the engagement
8. **Reporting** — Issue the practitioner's compilation report, which must:
   - Identify the financial information compiled
   - Describe management's and the practitioner's responsibilities
   - State that the engagement is not an assurance engagement
   - State that the practitioner does not express an opinion or conclusion
   - If applicable, draw attention to departures from the financial reporting framework

## Key Definitions

- **Applicable financial reporting framework** — The financial reporting framework adopted by management for the preparation of the financial information, acceptable given the nature of the entity and the objective of the information, or required by law or regulation
- **Compilation engagement** — An engagement in which a practitioner applies accounting and financial reporting expertise to assist management in the preparation and presentation of financial information, and reports on the engagement
- **Misstatement** — A difference between the amount, classification, presentation, or disclosure of a reported item and what is required for that item under the applicable financial reporting framework; may be intentional or unintentional

## Application to Probe Working Papers

This standard is not currently referenced by any Probe working papers. Compilation engagements are common in smaller practices. If CaseWare develops Probe templates for compilation engagements, this standard would be the primary reference. The working paper requirements are significantly lighter than for audit or review engagements since no assurance is provided.
