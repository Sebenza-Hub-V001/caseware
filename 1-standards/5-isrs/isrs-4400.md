# ISRS 4400 (Revised) — Agreed-Upon Procedures Engagements

## Objective

The practitioner's objectives in an agreed-upon procedures engagement under this ISRS are to:

1. **Agree** with the engaging party the procedures to be performed;
2. **Perform** the agreed-upon procedures; and
3. **Communicate** the procedures performed and the related findings in accordance with the requirements of this ISRS.

## Scope

This ISRS deals with:
- The practitioner's responsibilities when engaged to perform an agreed-upon procedures engagement; and
- The form and content of the agreed-upon procedures report.

Key scope points:
- Applies to agreed-upon procedures engagements on **financial or non-financial** subject matters
- In an agreed-upon procedures engagement, the practitioner performs procedures that have been agreed upon by the practitioner and the engaging party
- The engaging party acknowledges that the procedures performed are appropriate for the purpose of the engagement
- The practitioner communicates findings in a report — the engaging party and intended users **draw their own conclusions** from the findings
- An agreed-upon procedures engagement is **not an assurance engagement** — the practitioner does not express a conclusion or opinion
- ISQM 1 applies at the firm level

Effective for engagements for which the terms of engagement are agreed on or after **January 1, 2022**.

## Key Requirements

1. **Relevant ethical requirements** — Comply with relevant ethical requirements (independence may or may not be required depending on the engagement)
2. **Professional judgment** — Exercise professional judgment throughout
3. **Quality management** — Engagement-level quality management, including direction, supervision, and review
4. **Engagement acceptance and continuance** — Evaluate whether:
   - The engagement has a rational purpose
   - The procedures can be described and performed as agreed
   - The practitioner has the necessary competence
5. **Agreeing terms** — Written agreement (engagement letter) covering:
   - Intended use and distribution of the report
   - The agreed-upon procedures to be performed
   - Practitioner's responsibilities
   - Engaging party's acknowledgement that procedures are appropriate
6. **Performing the procedures** — Perform the agreed-upon procedures as described in the terms of engagement
7. **Using a practitioner's expert** — May use the work of a practitioner's expert
8. **Reporting** — The report includes:
   - Description of each procedure performed
   - Findings for each procedure (factual results)
   - Statement that the engagement is not an assurance engagement
   - Restriction on use if applicable
9. **Combining with other engagements** — May undertake together with another engagement
10. **Documentation** — Document the engagement sufficiently

## Key Definitions

- **Agreed-upon procedures** — Procedures that have been agreed to by the practitioner and the engaging party (and if relevant, other parties)
- **Agreed-upon procedures engagement** — An engagement in which a practitioner carries out procedures agreed with the engaging party and communicates the findings
- **Engaging party** — The party that engages the practitioner to perform the agreed-upon procedures engagement
- **Findings** — Factual results of the agreed-upon procedures performed

## Application to Probe Working Papers

This standard is not currently referenced by any Probe working papers. However, agreed-upon procedures engagements are commonly performed by audit firms. If CaseWare develops Probe templates for AUP engagements, this standard would be the primary reference. The structure differs significantly from audit templates as there is no risk assessment or assurance conclusion.
