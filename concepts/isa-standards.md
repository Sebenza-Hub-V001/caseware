# International Standards on Auditing (ISA)

The International Standards on Auditing are the professional standards governing audit engagements. The [[entities/probe-audit-premium-plus]] methodology implements these standards within [[entities/caseware-working-papers]].

## Role in the Project

- Each working paper maps to specific ISA requirements.
- The How-To documents in the [[concepts/four-stage-pipeline]] make ISA alignment explicit.
- The "Key Questions — Additional Guidance" table links critical questions to the ISA standards they address.

## Most Referenced Standards

The following ISAs appear most frequently across the working papers:

1. **ISA 315** (11 working papers) — The backbone of the risk assessment process
2. **ISA 240** (10 working papers) — Fraud responsibilities appear throughout planning and execution
3. **ISA 330** (6 working papers) — Audit responses flow from risk assessment
4. **ISA 220** (6 working papers) — Quality management touches planning, execution, and finalisation
5. **ISA 320** (5 working papers) — Materiality underpins scoping and evaluation
