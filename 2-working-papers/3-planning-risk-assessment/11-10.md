# Risk Analysis Summary

## Purpose

This 3-page working paper provides a consolidated summary of the risk assessment across the entire audit engagement. It presents both financial-statement-level risks and assertion-level risks for every class of transactions, account balance, and disclosure (COTABD). It is the central risk matrix that aggregates inputs from multiple planning working papers and drives the audit strategy.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Risk at Financial Statement Level
Three high-level risk assessments:
- **Inherent risk at financial statement level** — Overall inherent risk of the entity
- **Control risk at financial statement level** — Overall control risk based on internal controls evaluation
- **Fraud risk at financial statement level** — Overall fraud risk assessment

### Performance Materiality
- Performance materiality amount (R) — derived from [[2-working-papers/2-pre-engagement-planning/10-60]]

### Risk at Assertion Level
A matrix for each COTABD with the following columns representing assertions:

**For balance sheet items (assets, liabilities, equity):**
- **C** — Completeness
- **EO** — Existence/Occurrence
- **RO** — Rights and Obligations
- **AVA** — Accuracy, Valuation and Allocation
- **CO** — N/A for balance sheet items
- **PD** — Presentation and Disclosure

**For income statement items (revenue, expenses):**
- **C** — Completeness
- **EO** — Existence/Occurrence
- **RO** — N/A for income statement items
- **AVA** — Accuracy, Valuation and Allocation
- **CO** — Cut-off
- **PD** — Presentation and Disclosure

**For disclosure-only items (related parties, commitments, cash flows):**
- Only **PD** (Presentation and Disclosure) applies

### Per COTABD, the following are shown:
- **Inherent risk at assertion level** — No IR (no inherent risk identified) or specific risk level
- **Risk of Material Misstatement** — Medium (1), High (2), or Low, per assertion
- **Specific materiality (R)** — If a specific materiality different from overall materiality is applied

### COTABDs Covered

**Balance Sheet — Assets:**
- Property, plant and equipment
- Investment property on the cost model
- Prepayments
- Inventories
- Cash and cash equivalents
- Trade and other receivables
- Loans to directors, managers and employees

**Balance Sheet — Liabilities:**
- Other financial liabilities
- Finance lease liabilities
- Bank overdraft
- Trade and other payables
- Current tax payable

**Income Statement:**
- Revenue
- Cost of sales
- Operating gains/losses
- Operating expenses
- Finance costs
- Taxation

**Equity:**
- Capital or contributions
- Retained income

**Disclosures:**
- Related parties
- Commitments and contingencies
- Statement of cash flows

## Completion Guidance

- This is a summary document that aggregates risk assessments from multiple sources — complete it after:
  - [[2-working-papers/2-pre-engagement-planning/10-20]] (desired audit risk)
  - [[2-working-papers/2-pre-engagement-planning/10-50]] and [[2-working-papers/2-pre-engagement-planning/10-51]] (understanding of transactions and balances)
  - [[2-working-papers/2-pre-engagement-planning/10-55]] (IT environment complexity)
  - [[2-working-papers/2-pre-engagement-planning/10-60]] (materiality and performance materiality)
  - [[2-working-papers/3-planning-risk-assessment/11-20]] (inherent risk assessment per COTABD)
  - [[2-working-papers/3-planning-risk-assessment/11-25]] (fraud risk assessment)
  - [[2-working-papers/3-planning-risk-assessment/11-30]] (internal control evaluation)
- The Risk of Material Misstatement = f(Inherent Risk, Control Risk) per the audit risk model
- Default ROMM is "Medium (1)" when no specific inherent risk is identified ("No IR")
- Review the assertion applicability: balance sheet items use C/EO/RO/AVA/PD; income statement items use C/EO/AVA/CO/PD; disclosure items use PD only
- Specific materiality should be set for COTABDs where overall materiality is not appropriate (e.g. related party transactions, director remuneration)
- Financial statement level risks must be assessed before assertion-level risks
- This document should be reviewed and potentially updated at the conclusion of the audit if new risks emerge
