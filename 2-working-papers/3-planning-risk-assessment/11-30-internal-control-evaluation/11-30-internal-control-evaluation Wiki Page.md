# Evaluation of System of Internal Control

## Purpose
This working paper documents the auditor's understanding and evaluation of the entity's system of internal control across three key components: the control environment, the risk assessment process, and the monitoring of controls. It determines the control risk assessment at the financial statement level and identifies any control deficiencies that may affect the audit approach.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Risk Assessment Procedures (Item 1)
- Selection of procedure types: Inquiries, Observations, Inspections
- Guidance notes on less complex entities where communication may be informal or oral rather than documented in policy manuals

### Control Environment (Items 2-16)
Each item has columns for: Description of processes, Explanation of how understanding was verified, and Risk.

**Management Oversight and Ethics (Items 2-3)**
- How management carries out oversight responsibility, creates/maintains culture, demonstrates commitment to integrity and ethical values — considers reinforcement of ethical values, actions to eliminate incentives for dishonesty, philosophy and operating style, codes of conduct
- How management communicates views on business practices and ethical behaviour to employees

**Financial Reporting Oversight (Items 4-5)**
- How management oversees financial reporting, including accounting estimates — considers skills/knowledge for methods/models, understanding of FRF compliance, timeliness of information
- How management oversees the entity's use of IT — considers IT steering committee, IT strategic plans, IT organisational chart

**Governance and Independence (Items 6-9)**
- How directors operate independently from management — considers independent individuals, objectivity, legal/regulatory requirements
- How directors oversee the entity's system of internal control — considers identification of oversight responsibilities, retention of oversight, evaluation of management actions, whistleblower procedures
- How directors oversee the financial reporting process, including accounting estimates
- How directors oversee the entity's use of IT

**Organisational Structure and Human Resources (Items 10-12)**
- How entity assigns authority and responsibility — considers knowledge/experience of key personnel, areas of authority, reporting lines, business practice policies, resource allocation
- How entity attracts, develops, and retains competent individuals (financial, accounting, IT) — considers recruiting, training, performance appraisals
- How entity holds individuals accountable for internal control responsibilities

**Control Environment Conclusions (Items 13-16)**
- Whether management has created a culture of honesty and ethical behaviour
- Whether IT oversight, organisational structure, and resources are appropriate given entity complexity
- Evaluation of whether control environment provides appropriate foundation for other internal control components
- Whether control deficiencies were identified that may undermine other components

### Risk Assessment Process (Items 17-25)
**Management's Risk Assessment (Items 17-21)**
- How management identifies business risks relating to financial statement preparation under IFRS for SMEs
- How management assesses significance and likelihood of those risks
- How management decides upon actions to manage risks and results thereof
- How management identifies and addresses risks relating to accounting estimates — considers FRF requirements, data sources, IT environment, key personnel
- How management identifies and addresses susceptibility to misstatement from bias or fraud — considers monitoring for change in methods/assumptions, performance indicators, incentives for bias, model oversight, documentation requirements

**Governance Risk Assessment (Items 22-25)**
- How directors identify business risks relating to financial statement preparation
- How management identifies and responds to risks of fraud
- How management communicates its fraud risk response process to directors
- How directors exercise oversight over management's fraud risk identification and response processes

**Risk Assessment Process Conclusion (Item 26)**
- Evaluation of appropriateness of risk assessment process, noting that in less complex entities the process is often less formally documented

### Monitoring (Items 27-31)
**Monitoring Activities (Items 27-30)**
- How entity monitors effectiveness of internal control through ongoing and separate evaluations — considers design, performance/frequency, evaluation of results, management review, internal audit, compliance oversight, external communications
- How entity monitors information processing controls involving IT — considers permissions, segregation of duties, error identification/resolution
- How entity identifies control deficiencies and takes remedial action — considers timely communication to responsible parties
- Sources of information used in monitoring and basis for reliability

**Monitoring Conclusion (Item 31)**
- Evaluation of whether monitoring process is appropriate

### Overall Conclusion (Items 32-33)
- Whether results indicated risks management should have identified but failed to
- Control risk at financial statement level assessment

## ISA References
- **ISA 315 (Revised)** — Understanding the entity's system of internal control (control environment, risk assessment process, monitoring components)
- **ISA 240** — Fraud risk assessment by management and governance oversight (implicit in items 23-25)
- References to IFRS for SMEs Accounting Standard throughout

## Completion Guidance
- This is the most extensive working paper in the 11.xx section at **8 pages** — allow adequate time for completion.
- Each numbered item requires three responses: a description of the process, how the auditor verified their understanding, and any identified risks.
- For owner-managed or less complex entities, note that formal documentation may not exist — the auditor should describe how they obtained their understanding through inquiry and observation.
- The control environment assessment at items 13-16 provides critical conclusions that affect the overall audit approach.
- The risk assessment process section (items 17-26) specifically addresses how management deals with accounting estimates and fraud risk.
- The monitoring section (items 27-31) covers both manual and IT-based controls.
- The overall conclusion at item 33 provides the control risk at financial statement level, which flows to [[2-working-papers/3-planning-risk-assessment/11-10-risk-analysis-summary/11-10-risk-analysis-summary Wiki Page]].

## How-To Document Summary

The How-To V5 draft for 11.30 Evaluation of System of Internal Control explains the functionality and features of this working paper within Probe Audit Premium+. It is a questionnaire-type document guiding the engagement team through understanding and evaluating the entity's system of internal control.

### Key Topics Addressed
- **Document placement** — located in the Planning and risk assessment procedures folder in CaseWare document manager
- **Three components of internal control** evaluated per ISA 315 (Revised 2019):
  - **Control environment** — integrity/ethical values, oversight responsibilities, organisational structure, commitment to competence, accountability
  - **Risk assessment process** — how management identifies business risks, assesses significance, and manages those risks
  - **Monitoring** — how the entity monitors effectiveness of internal control, including IT controls and remediation of deficiencies
- **Risk assessment procedures** — selecting procedure types (inquiries, observations, inspections), describing information sources
- **Section layout** — each component section uses columns for Procedures, Description of processes, Explanation of how understanding was verified, and Risk
- **Component conclusions** — each of the three component sections has its own conclusion evaluating whether the component is appropriate given entity nature and complexity; control environment and monitoring conclusions also address whether deficiencies may undermine other components
- **Control risk at financial statement level** — the overall conclusion documents the control risk assessment
- **Document outcomes** — mandatory evaluation of design and implementation of controls, separate conclusions per component, consideration of deficiency impact on other components
- **Group audits** — additional questions for Group Audit Single/Multiple engagement types
- **Available features** — insert/delete comments, record risks/events, Core vs Extended, Guidance, Freeze

### ISA References in the How-To
- **ISA 315 (Revised 2019)** — understanding the entity's system of internal control (control environment, risk assessment process, monitoring components)
