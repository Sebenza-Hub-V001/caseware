# Identification of Components

## Purpose
This working paper is used in **group audit engagements** to identify and document all entities and business units within the group structure, assign them to components, and establish the basis for the group audit strategy. It is the "G" (Group) variant of the inherent risk assessment section and is only relevant when auditing consolidated or group financial statements.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Group Information
- Year-end date
- Reviewed by field

### Detail of All Entities and Business Units Within Group
A table capturing each entity/business unit with the following columns:
- **Name of entity or business unit** — e.g., Client HoldCo (Pty) Ltd, B (Pty) Ltd, C (Pty) Ltd, A (Pty) Ltd
- **Type of relationship** — Parent, Subsidiary, etc.
- **New, existing or derecognised?** — Status of the entity in the group
- **Country registered or main location of business unit?** — e.g., RSA
- **Operations or activities** — Nature of operations and locations (e.g., "Production in Gauteng, sales to B and C")
- **Group information** — Year-end date for each entity
- **Financial reporting framework** — e.g., IFRS for SMEs Accounting Standard
- **Type of engagement** — Audit, Review, etc.
- **Consider together with other entities or business units?** — Yes/No, with reference to 10.50G
- **Assign to component** — Component assignment (e.g., Component 1, Component 2)
- **Comment** — Additional notes, e.g., "Separate activities not linked to Client HoldCo or B or C"

### Detail of All Components
A summary table listing:
- **Name of components** — e.g., Consolidation, Component 1, Component 2
- **Description** — What entities/activities are included (e.g., "Parent (Client HoldCo), Sub B, Sub C" or "Sub A only")

## ISA References
- **ISA 600 (Revised)** — Special Considerations — Audits of Group Financial Statements (Including the Work of Component Auditors)
- The component identification process supports the group audit team's scoping decisions under ISA 600.

## Completion Guidance
- This working paper is **only used for group/multiple entity engagements** — it appears in the "Probe Audit (Pty) Ltd (Group Multiple)" template variant.
- Each entity in the group must be listed, including the parent and all subsidiaries.
- The "Consider together" column determines whether entities with similar characteristics can be grouped into a single component for audit purposes (cross-reference 10.50G).
- The consolidation line itself is listed but typically marked as "Not relevant" across most columns since it represents consolidation adjustments rather than an operating entity.
- Components should be assigned based on financial significance, risk profile, and operational relatedness.
- The summary table at the bottom provides a clear overview of the component structure for the engagement team.
