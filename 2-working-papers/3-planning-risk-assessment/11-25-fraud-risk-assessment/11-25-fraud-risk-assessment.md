# Fraud Risk Assessment

## Purpose
This working paper documents the auditor's assessment of fraud risk factors structured around the fraud triangle framework: Incentive/Pressure, Opportunity, and Rationalisation. It identifies conditions and circumstances that may indicate risks of material misstatement due to fraud, including the mandatory presumed risk of management override of controls per ISA 240.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Risk Assessment Procedures (Item 1)
- Selection of procedure types: Inquiries, Observations, Inspections
- Description of sources from which information was obtained

### Incentive/Pressure (Items 2-7)
Each item has Yes/No, Description, and Risk columns:
- **Item 2:** Financial stability or profitability threatened by economic/industry/entity conditions — considers market saturation, declining margins, negative cash flows, rapid growth, operating losses, changing industry
- **Item 3:** Excessive pressure on management from those charged with governance or third parties — considers profitability expectations, financing needs, debt covenants, performance targets
- **Item 4:** Management's personal net worth threatened by entity performance — considers financial interests, contingent compensation, personal guarantees
- **Item 5:** Personal financial obligations creating pressure on management/employees with access to cash or assets
- **Item 6:** Adverse relationships between entity and employees motivating misappropriation — considers layoffs, inconsistent rewards
- **Item 7:** Analytical procedures indicating risk of material misstatement due to fraud, including revenue accounts

### Opportunity (Items 8-12)
- **Item 8:** Nature of industry/operations providing opportunities for fraudulent financial reporting — considers omission/misstatement of disclosures, related-party transactions, subjective estimates, complex period-end transactions
- **Item 9:** Ineffective monitoring by management — considers domination by single person, ineffective oversight by those charged with governance
- **Item 10:** Complex or unstable organisational structure — considers difficulty determining controlling interest, unusual legal entities
- **Item 11:** Deficient internal control components — considers inadequate segregation of duties, inadequate physical safeguards, inadequate monitoring, high staff turnover, inadequate IT understanding
- **Item 12:** Susceptibility of assets to misappropriation — considers large cash amounts, small high-value items, easily convertible assets

### Rationalisation (Items 13-17)
- **Item 13:** Ineffective culture of ethical behaviour — considers communication/enforcement of ethical standards, inappropriate accounting justification, no personal/business transaction distinction, tolerance of petty theft
- **Item 14:** Known history of violations of securities/other laws
- **Item 15:** Low morale among senior management
- **Item 16:** Strained relationship between auditors and management — considers disputes on accounting matters, unreasonable demands, restricted access
- **Item 17:** Behaviour indicating displeasure or dissatisfaction with entity

### Identified Risks
A table for documenting identified fraud risks with columns:
- Source (ISA reference)
- Risk description
- ROMM considerations
- ROMM level
- Significant risk (Yes/No)
- Overall response

**Pre-populated risk:** Management override of controls (ISA 240.31/32) — mandatory significant risk requiring:
- Understanding controls surrounding journal entries, including non-standard entries (refer to 11.50)
- Testing appropriateness of journal entries and adjustments (refer to 16.10)
- Consideration of additional journal testing based on fraud risk assessment

### Conclusion (Items 18-20)
- Whether results indicated risks management should have identified but failed to
- Whether any risks affect assessment at assertion level
- Fraud risk at financial statement level assessment

## ISA References
- **ISA 240** — The Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Fraud in an Audit of Financial Statements
  - ISA 240.26 — Presumed significant risk of fraud in revenue recognition
  - ISA 240.31 — Management override of controls as a mandatory significant risk
  - ISA 240.32 — Further audit procedures responsive to management override
- **ISA 315 (Revised)** — Risk assessment procedures framework
- Cross-references to working papers 11.50 (journal entry controls) and 16.10 (journal testing)

## Completion Guidance
- Each fraud risk factor (items 2-17) must be assessed with a Yes/No selection and a description explaining the auditor's evaluation.
- The fraud triangle structure (Incentive, Opportunity, Rationalisation) ensures all dimensions of fraud risk are considered.
- Management override of controls is **always** a significant risk — it is pre-populated and cannot be removed.
- Additional fraud risks identified should be added to the Identified Risks table with ROMM assessments.
- The conclusion at item 20 provides the fraud risk assessment at the financial statement level, which feeds into [[2-working-papers/3-planning-risk-assessment/11-10-risk-analysis-summary/11-10-risk-analysis-summary]].
- This is a 5-page document requiring careful consideration of each factor.

## How-To Document Summary

The How-To V5 draft for 11.25 Fraud Risk Assessment explains the functionality and features of this working paper within Probe Audit Premium+. It is a questionnaire-type document guiding the engagement team through the identification and assessment of risks of material misstatement due to fraud.

### Key Topics Addressed
- **Document placement** — located in the Planning and risk assessment procedures folder in CaseWare document manager
- **Fraud triangle framework** — structured around the three conditions of fraud per ISA 240: Incentive (pressure/motive), Opportunity (circumstances), and Rationalisation (attitude/ethical values)
- **Risk assessment procedures** — selecting procedure types (inquiries, observations, inspections), describing information sources, recording interviewee details
- **Incentive / Opportunity / Rationalisation sections** — Yes/No questions with description and risk columns to document consideration of fraud risk factors
- **Identified risks table** — pre-populated with the presumed risk of management override of controls (ISA 240.31 and ISA 240.32) as a mandatory significant risk that cannot be removed; additional fraud risks recorded via the risk dialogue populate this table and 12.20 Audit Plan and Strategy
- **Risk dialogue fields** — Source, Risk description, ROMM considerations, Significant risk indicator, ROMM level (low/medium/high), Overall response selections
- **Overall response** — when a fraud risk is raised, an additional overall response section is included with automated selections; significant risks also update 02.00 Partner sign-off
- **Conclusion** — confirms whether results indicate risks management failed to identify, whether risks affect assertion-level assessment, and documents the fraud risk at financial statement level
- **Document outcomes** — mandatory conclusion on fraud risk at financial statement level; overall responses required for all raised fraud risks
- **Group audits** — additional questions for Group Audit Single/Multiple engagement types
- **Available features** — insert/delete comments, insert/delete rows, record risks/events, Core vs Extended, Guidance, Freeze

### ISA References in the How-To
- **ISA 240** — The Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Fraud (fraud risk factors framework, incentive/opportunity/rationalisation)
- **ISA 240.31** — Presumed significant risk of management override of controls
- **ISA 240.32** — Further audit procedures responsive to management override
