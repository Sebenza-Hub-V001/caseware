# Risk Assessment at Assertion Level

## Purpose
This working paper documents the detailed risk assessment at the assertion level for every material financial statement line item. For each line item, it presents comparative financial data, inherent risk assessments per assertion, qualitative materiality considerations, and specific materiality levels. For revenue, it also documents identified fraud risks per ISA 240.26, including the option to rebut the presumed fraud risk in revenue recognition.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Structure Per Line Item
Each financial statement line item follows a consistent format:

**Lead Schedules:**
- Current year amount (2023)
- Percentage change from prior year
- Prior year amount (2022)
- Two-years-prior amount (2021)

**Risk Assessment at Assertion Level:**
- **C** — Completeness
- **EO** — Existence/Occurrence
- **RO** — Rights & Obligations
- **AVA** — Accuracy, Valuation & Allocation
- **CO** — Classification & Occurrence
- **PD** — Presentation & Disclosure

**Inherent Risk Assessment at Assertion Level:** Rating per assertion (No IR, Low, Medium, High, or N/A)

**Qualitative Materiality:** "Is [line item] qualitatively material?"

**Specific Materiality:** Amount and reason for any section-specific materiality threshold

### Balance Sheet Line Items

**Assets:**
- Property, plant and equipment (R9,125,744 — 422.1% increase)
- Investment property on the cost model (R10,000,000 — 33.3% decrease)
- Prepayments — NCA and CA (R221,400 — 87.3% increase)
- Inventories (R17,066,453 — 30.4% increase)
- Cash and cash equivalents (R1,779,600 — 6.9% decrease)
- Trade and other receivables — NCA and CA (R18,118,300 — 65.1% increase)
- Loans to directors, managers and employees — NCA and CA (R40,000 — 46.7% decrease)

**Liabilities:**
- Other financial liabilities — NCL and CL (R12,751,600 — 18.6% increase)
- Finance lease liabilities — NCL and CL (R7,000,000 NCL + R1,000,000 CL)
- Bank overdraft (R400,000 — 2,566.7% increase)
- Trade and other payables (R10,214,100 — 4.7% decrease)
- Current tax payable (R3,161,800 — 125.2% increase)

### Income Statement Line Items

**Revenue Section — Special Treatment:**
Before the standard lead schedule format, revenue includes a detailed fraud risk identification table with columns:
- Source (ISA reference)
- Description of risk
- Inherent risk factors
- Implication
- Assertions affected (C, EO, RO, AVA, CO, PD)
- Likelihood to occur
- Monetary impact
- Inherent risk level
- Significant risk (Yes/No)

Pre-populated for three revenue streams (per ISA 240.26), each with the option to rebut the presumed fraud risk and document reasons.

Additional revenue-specific question: "Do you conclude that the presumed fraud risk related to revenue recognition is not applicable in the circumstances of this engagement?"

**Other Income Statement Items:**
- Revenue (R130,781,700 — 38.5% increase)
- Cost of sales (R79,110,600 — 26.6% increase)
- Operating gains / losses (R1,185,500 — significant swing from prior year loss of R86,900)
- Operating expenses (R35,692,302 — 39.3% increase)
- Finance costs (R1,947,001 — 1.0% increase)
- Taxation (R3,161,900 — 125.2% increase)

### Equity Items
- Capital or contributions (R1,864,300 — no change)
- Retained income (R19,959,697 — 117.9% increase)

### Disclosure-Only Items (PD assertion only)
- Related parties (no balances)
- Commitments and contingencies
- Statement of cash flows

## ISA References
- **ISA 315 (Revised)** — Identifying and assessing risks of material misstatement at the assertion level
- **ISA 240.26** — Presumed significant risk of fraud in revenue recognition (documented in the Revenue section with rebuttal option)
- **ISA 320** — Materiality in planning and performing an audit (specific materiality per section)

## Completion Guidance
- This is the **largest working paper** in the 11.xx section at **13 pages** — it covers every financial statement line item.
- The comparative financial data (3-year trend) and percentage changes are auto-populated from the lead schedules in CaseWare.
- For balance sheet items, assertions C, EO, RO, AVA, CO apply; PD is separate. RO is N/A for income statement items.
- For disclosure-only items (related parties, commitments, cash flows), only the PD assertion applies.
- The inherent risk assessment per assertion should be consistent with the overall assessment on [[2-working-papers/3-planning-risk-assessment/11-10-risk-analysis-summary/11-10-risk-analysis-summary]].
- Large percentage changes (e.g., bank overdraft at 2,566.7%, PPE at 422.1%) should be investigated and may indicate elevated inherent risk for specific assertions.
- Revenue receives special treatment with the fraud risk identification table — the auditor must address each revenue stream's fraud risk or formally rebut the presumption.
- Specific materiality levels should be documented with reasons where they differ from overall performance materiality.
