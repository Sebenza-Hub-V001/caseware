# Engagement Letter

## Purpose

This working paper is the standard engagement letter template used to formalise the terms, objectives, and scope of the audit engagement between the firm and the client. It sets out the auditor's responsibilities, the client's responsibilities, and the commercial and legal terms governing the relationship. It is addressed to the client and must be signed by an authorised representative of the entity.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Opening / Scope
- Identification of entity (Probe Audit (Pty) Ltd (Single))
- Financial statements covered (statement of financial position, comprehensive income, changes in equity, cash flows, and notes)
- Objective: reasonable assurance and issuance of auditor's report
- Audit conducted in accordance with ISAs

### Auditor's Responsibilities (paragraphs a-f)
- Identify and assess risks of material misstatement (fraud or error)
- Tests of transactions, existence, ownership and valuation
- Understanding of internal control (not for opinion on effectiveness)
- Evaluate accounting policies and estimates
- Going concern conclusion
- Overall presentation, structure and content evaluation

### 1. Detection of Fraud, Error and Non-Compliance
- Primary responsibility rests with directors and management
- Audit provides reasonable but not absolute assurance
- Inherent limitations of audit (test nature, sampling)
- Communication of illegal acts, errors, fraud to appropriate management level

### 2. Responsibilities of Management
- Management, conduct and operation of business
- Representations to third parties
- Maintenance of accounting records
- Internal control structure
- Preparation of annual financial statements (IFRS for SMEs / Companies Act, 2008)
- Going concern basis
- Adjustment for material misstatements
- Selection and application of accounting policies
- Safeguarding of assets
- Access to information, meetings, and documents

### 3. Responsibilities of Auditors
- Statutory responsibility to express opinion
- Compliance with IFRS for SMEs
- Professional responsibility for non-compliance reporting

### 4. Staff
- Staff continuity and training
- Non-solicitation clause (12-month restriction)
- Placement fee (10% of annual cost to company)

### 5. Reporting
- Audit opinion on financial statements
- Key audit matters communication (for PIEs)

### 6. Financial Intelligence Centre Act, 2001
- Section 29 reporting obligations for suspicious/unusual transactions
- Override of confidentiality rules

### 7. Regulatory and Professional Obligations
- Obligation to act in public interest
- Non-compliance with laws and regulations (NOCLAR) procedures
- Disclosure to appropriate authority when warranted

### 8. Reportable Irregularities
- Definition per Auditing Professions Act, 2005
- IRBA reporting process (initial report, 30-day follow-up)
- Staff obligation to report to engagement partner immediately

### 9. Reporting to Management
- Written communication of internal control weaknesses
- No third-party distribution without consent

### 10. Communication with Directors
- Audit matters of governance interest

### 11. Information
- Reliance on authorised persons
- No responsibility for accuracy of information received
- Client obligation to supply information for FICA/POCA compliance

### 12-13. Meetings and Documents
- Pre-audit and results meetings
- Right to attend general meetings
- Sight of all documents issued with financial statements

### 14-17. Use, Third Party Rights, and Distribution
- Future use of audit opinion (separate engagement for offerings)
- Additional deliverables limitations
- Third party rights exclusion
- Electronic distribution responsibilities

### 18. Electronic Communications
- Acceptance of inherent risks of electronic communication

### 19. Use of Software
- Software tools provided "as is"

### 20. Ownership of Audit Files
- Working papers are sole property of firm
- Copyright and intellectual property retention

### 21-22. Force Majeure, Waiver, Assignment
- Circumstances beyond control
- Sub-contractor appointment rights

### 23. Exclusions and Limitations on Liability
- Maximum liability: two times fees charged and paid
- Exclusion of indirect/consequential loss
- Claims must be brought within two years

### 24-25. Timetable and Fees
- Fee basis: time, skill, responsibility, complexity
- Hourly rates at applicable charge-out rates
- Disbursements recovery
- Payment on presentation; interest at 3% above prime after 30 days
- Right to suspend services for overdue fees

### 26-27. Quality of Service and Future Years
- IRBA quality requirements and internal file review
- Letter remains in place until replaced

### Sign-off
- Acceptance signature block for client representative (position, name, signature, date)

## ISA References

- ISA 210 — Agreeing the Terms of Audit Engagements (basis for the entire letter)
- ISA 240 — The Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Fraud in an Audit of Financial Statements (fraud detection limitations)
- ISA 260 — Communication with Those Charged with Governance (governance interest matters)
- ISA 700 — Forming an Opinion and Reporting on Financial Statements (key audit matters for PIEs)
- Auditing Professions Act, 2005 — Reportable irregularities (Section 8)
- Financial Intelligence Centre Act, 2001 — Section 29 reporting (Section 6)
- Companies Act, 2008 — Statutory audit requirements
- IRBA Code of Professional Conduct — Independence and ethical requirements

## Completion Guidance

- The letter is largely a template with merge fields populated from the Info Store (entity name, framework, etc.)
- Customise the addressee, entity name, and financial reporting framework references
- Ensure alignment with decisions made in [[2-working-papers/10-20]] (engagement type, PIE status, services scope)
- Key audit matters paragraph only applies if Q26 in 10.20 is "Yes" (PIE entities)
- Replace all "Name of practice / firm" placeholders with the actual firm name
- Obtain signed copy from client before commencing substantive work
- Review annually; update only if circumstances change (per Q32 in 10.20)
