# Engagement Evaluation

## Purpose

This working paper documents the evaluation of whether to accept or continue an audit engagement. It covers the type of engagement, terms, continuance considerations, ethical requirements, going concern assessment, and users of the financial statements. The overall output is a conclusion on whether the engagement should proceed and the setting of the desired audit risk level.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Type of Engagement (Q1-9)
- Engagement type (audit, review, etc.)
- Whether audit is required by statute and which statute
- Consolidation considerations (parent, subsidiaries, joint ventures)
- Whether the entity has multiple business units (branches/divisions)
- Country of incorporation and category of entity
- Public interest score
- MOI audit requirement
- Independent compilation and reporting status
- Confirmation of correct engagement type selection

### Terms of the Engagement (Q10-30)
- Engagement partner rotation requirements
- Smaller entity treatment decision (IFAC Glossary definition)
- Extended procedures election
- First audit of entity and prior year audit report type
- Management vs Those Charged with Governance distinction
- Financial reporting framework (e.g. IFRS for SMEs)
- Supplementary local regulation requirements
- Financial statement preparation, secretarial/bookkeeping, and tax responsibilities
- Section 90 Companies Act appointment eligibility
- Public Interest Entity (PIE) classification per IRBA Code (R400.23 SA)
- Key audit matters requirement
- Advisory duties/services
- Debt/equity trading status
- Fiduciary capacity
- SAICA micro entity guidance applicability

### Engagement Continuance (Q31-42)
- Preconditions for audit (management acceptance of responsibilities)
- Engagement letter currency and update necessity
- Resource availability (human, technological, intellectual)
- Publicly available information on management integrity and industry
- Reputational risk assessment
- Engagement quality review (EQR) requirement
- Firm's System of Quality Management (SoQM) considerations
- Prior audit results and concerns
- Management factors increasing engagement risk
- Special attention circumstances (high-risk industries, complex transactions, litigation)
- Accountable institution status (Financial Intelligence Centre Act)
- Money laundering and terrorist financing risk assessment

### Ethical Requirements (Q43-48)
- Firm-level independence information
- Self-interest threats (financial interests, business relationships, fees)
- Self-review threats (preparation of accounting records, non-audit services)
- Advocacy threats (promoting shares, legal disputes)
- Familiarity threats (long association, rotation, gifts)
- Intimidation threats (insufficient fees, litigation, replacement threats)

### Going Concern (Q49-55)
- Management's assessment of going concern
- Appropriateness of management's assessment (period, completeness)
- Events or conditions casting doubt on going concern
- Financial indicators (net liability, borrowings maturity, negative cash flows, adverse ratios)
- Operating conditions (intention to liquidate, loss of key management/market/supplier)
- Other indicators (non-compliance, legal proceedings, regulatory changes, catastrophes)
- Court orders or decisions to wind up

### Users of Financial Statements (Q56-57)
- External users (subsidiary of listed entity, planned sale, significant creditors/lenders)
- Changes, sales, or transfers in ownership

### Conclusions
- Engagement continuance decision
- Ethical requirements compliance
- Going concern risk assessment at planning stage (Low/Medium/High)
- Users analysis risk indication
- Desired audit risk setting (Very low/Low/Medium/High)

### Engagement Partner Declaration
- Confirmation that SoQM procedures were followed
- Conclusion appropriateness
- Whether information from continuance process affects risk assessment or audit plan
- Sign-off fields for engagement partner and engagement quality reviewer

## Completion Guidance

- Work through all questions sequentially; answers to earlier questions (e.g. PIE status at Q25) drive later requirements (e.g. key audit matters at Q26)
- The going concern assessment here is preliminary (planning stage); it feeds into the more detailed assessment at [[2-working-papers/02-20]]
- The desired audit risk set in the conclusion flows through to [[2-working-papers/10-60]] (materiality) and [[2-working-papers/11-10]] (risk analysis)
- Ethical requirements section should be completed in conjunction with the firm's independence register
- EQR decision triggers additional sign-off roles throughout the file
