# Gathering Information

## Purpose

This working paper captures the results of risk assessment procedures performed to understand the entity and its environment during the planning phase. It is a 4-page questionnaire covering audit considerations, transaction-level information, and accounting policy matters. It serves as the central planning document that drives the scope of work across related working papers.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Risk Assessment Procedures Header
- Type of procedures performed (Inquiries, Observations, Inspections)
- Source(s) from which information was obtained
- Risk indicator column

### Audit Considerations (Q2-9)
- Comparative figures agreement with prior year (links to 17.10 — Opening balances)
- Need for additional auditing standards beyond ISAs
- Firm's monitoring and remediation process findings relevant to the engagement
- Plans to use Information Produced by the Entity (IPE), including system-generated source information
- Plans to use automated tools and techniques (data analytics, RPA, AI/machine learning)
- Documentation approach for risk assessment procedures on types and volumes of transactions (per COTABD individually or once if similar)
- Use of 10.52 for analytical procedures vs simple comparisons on 10.51
- Walk-through procedures for significant classes of transactions, account balances and disclosures

### Planning Decisions (Q10-18)
- Reliance on interim audit work
- Prior year unadjusted differences
- Difficult-to-audit transactions, events, account balances and disclosures from prior year
- Significant disclosures not related to specific financial statement items
- Significant other areas not dealt with within a specific financial statement item
- Key audit matters in the audit report (for PIEs)
- Prior period key audit matters (significant risks, higher risk areas, matters reported to directors, significant judgements, estimates with high uncertainty, significant events/transactions)
- Other information to be presented in financial statements
- Supplementary information to be presented

### Entity-Level Information (Q19-43)
- Changes in directors
- Significant contracts entered into
- VAT registration status
- Budgets and forecasts as risk assessment source
- Assets held by third parties
- Foreign exchange transactions and balances
- Foreign operations
- Government grants received
- Service organisations used
- Restructuring (current or planned)
- Business combinations (IFRS for SMEs)
- Deferred payment or below-market interest rate transactions
- Related party transactions
- Significant transactions outside normal course of business
- Loans/financial assistance to directors or related persons (Companies Act, 2008, section 71)
- Lessee and lessor arrangements
- Disposal of all or greater part of assets (Companies Act, section 112)
- Amalgamation or merger (Companies Act, section 113)
- Scheme or arrangement (Companies Act, section 114)
- Changes to Memorandum of Incorporation
- Shareholder and director meetings held
- Minutes kept of important meetings
- Restrictive clauses in MOI
- Committees and governance structure (social and ethics, audit, other board committees, company secretary, internal auditors)

### Accounting (Q44-52)
- Changes in accounting policy application
- Borrowing costs incurred
- Amounts based on accounting estimates or fair value measurements
- Financial statements in currency other than functional currency
- Functional currency of hyperinflationary economy
- Election to present earnings per share
- Segment information presentation
- Cash-generating units classification
- Election to apply IAS 39 instead of IFRS for SMEs sections 11 and 12
