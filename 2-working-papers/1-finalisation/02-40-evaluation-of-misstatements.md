# Evaluation of Misstatements

## Purpose

The Evaluation of Misstatements (02.40) documents the reassessment of materiality at the finalisation stage and the evaluation of all misstatements (corrected and uncorrected) identified during the audit. It ensures that the materiality levels set during planning remain appropriate given the actual financial results, and that any misstatements are properly assessed for their impact on the audit opinion.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Materiality Reassessment
A table with Min/Max percentage ranges and the current year amounts for each materiality base:
- (a) **Revenue** -- 0.5% to 1.0%
- (b) **Net profit before tax** -- 5.0% to 10%
- (c) **Shareholders' equity** -- 2.0% to 5.0%
- (d) **Total assets** -- 1.0% to 2.0%
- (e) **Total liabilities** -- 1.0% to 2.0%

Calculated range: "Based on the parameters selected above, overall materiality may be set between R[min] and R[max]"

### Planning Stage Materiality
- Overall materiality set during planning stage
- Performance materiality set during planning stage

### Conclusion Regarding Materiality Reassessment
- Overall final materiality amount
- Reason(s) for any change from planning materiality

### Conclusion Regarding Misstatements
- Standard conclusion: "No misstatements, neither corrected nor uncorrected, were found"
- Alternative: listing of misstatements with individual and aggregate assessment

## Completion Guidance

- The materiality bases table is auto-populated from the financial statements; the auditor selects the appropriate base and percentage
- Compare the final materiality to the planning materiality -- if there is a material difference, consider whether additional audit procedures are needed
- Performance materiality should be reassessed in light of the final overall materiality
- All identified misstatements (from all audit sections) must be aggregated here
- Uncorrected misstatements must be individually assessed and communicated to those charged with governance
- The materiality base selection requires a documented reason (e.g. "Revenue selected as the most stable base for this entity")
- This is a single-page document

## How-To Document Summary

The How-To V5 document for 02.40 Evaluation of Misstatements explains the functionality and features of this CaseView evaluation document within Probe Audit Premium+. It describes how the engagement team reassesses materiality levels and evaluates misstatements identified during the audit.

### Key Topics Addressed

- **Document placement** -- located in the Finalisation folder in CaseWare document manager
- **Materiality reassessment** -- five pre-populated materiality bases with min/max percentage parameters and current year values: (a) Revenue, (b) Net profit before tax, (c) Shareholders' equity, (d) Total assets, (e) Total liabilities; engagement team selects one base via tick box
- **Selection outcome** -- overall materiality range is auto-calculated based on the selected base and percentage parameters
- **Planning stage materiality** -- auto-populated overall materiality and performance materiality from the planning stage for comparison
- **Overall final materiality** -- auto-calculated from the selected base; engagement team confirms appropriateness
- **Reason(s) field** -- input field to document the rationale for the final materiality amount selected
- **Conclusion regarding misstatements** -- pre-populated conclusion automatically determined based on misstatements recorded in the engagement file; engagement team reviews and confirms
- **Group audits** -- evaluation applies at both group and component level when engagement type is Group Audit Single or Group Audit Multiple
