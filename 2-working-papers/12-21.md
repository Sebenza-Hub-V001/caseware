# Using Internal Audit

## Purpose

The Using Internal Audit working paper (12.21) documents the external auditor's evaluation of whether and how the internal audit function can be used during the audit engagement. It covers both the use of the internal audit function's work and the direct assistance of internal auditors, in accordance with ISA 610. The document is 3 pages and is structured in three sections: Audit Plan, Audit Strategy, and Planning Memorandum.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Audit Plan (Page 1)

Questions 1-4 address the foundational decisions, each with Yes/No, Description, Risk, and Ref. columns:

1. **Can the work of the internal audit function be used?** — requires evaluation of:
   - Objectivity (organisational status, policies and procedures)
   - Level of competence
   - Quality control and systematic/disciplined approach
2. **Can direct assistance of internal auditors be used?** — requires evaluation of:
   - Whether law or regulation prohibits direct assistance
   - Objectivity and competence of internal auditors
3. **Safeguards** — document safeguards to address objectivity or other threats identified
4. **Nature, timing and scope** — document what work has been or is planned to be performed by internal audit and its relevance to the overall audit strategy and plan. Consideration of whether internal auditors should provide direct assistance for procedures that (ISA 610.30):
   - Involve making significant judgements
   - Relate to higher assessed risks of material misstatement
   - Relate to work the internal auditors have been involved in and reported on
   - Relate to decisions about the internal audit function

### Audit Strategy (Page 2)

A detailed table mapping each financial statement line item to internal audit involvement:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Relevant balances and transactions | The financial statement line item |
| Use work of the internal auditor | Yes/No |
| Reperform work done by internal auditor | Yes/No |
| Direct assistance | Yes/No |
| Reason | Justification |
| Risk | Risk level |
| Ref. | Cross-reference |

Covers 23 line items: Property/plant/equipment, Investment property, Prepayments, Inventories, Cash, Trade receivables, Loans to directors, Other financial liabilities, Finance lease liabilities, Bank overdraft, Trade payables, Current tax payable, Revenue, Cost of sales, Operating gains/losses, Operating expenses, Finance costs, Taxation, Capital/contributions, Retained income, Related parties, Commitments/contingencies, Statement of cash flows.

**Conclusion (Q24):** Confirmation that the audit plan considered:
- Amount of judgment in planning/performing procedures and evaluating evidence
- Assessed risk of material misstatement
- Internal audit function's organisational status and objectivity policies
- Competence level of internal auditors performing the work
- Competence level of internal auditors providing direct assistance
- Whether external auditors remain sufficiently involved

### Planning Memorandum (Page 3)

- **Q25:** Document discussions with internal audit regarding planned use
- **Q26:** Written agreement from entity representative that internal auditors will follow external auditor's instructions without entity intervention
- **Q27:** Internal audit staff declarations — table for name, role, qualification, industry experience, signature, and date. Confirms:
  - Attendance at planning meeting
  - Commitment to inform external auditor of objectivity threats and comply with ethics code
  - Undertaking to maintain confidentiality

## ISA References

- ISA 610 — Using the Work of Internal Auditors
- ISA 610.30 — Considerations for direct assistance (significant judgements, higher risk areas)

## Completion Guidance

- Only complete this working paper if the entity has an internal audit function and the external auditor plans to use its work or obtain direct assistance.
- The evaluation of objectivity, competence, and systematic approach (Q1-2) must be documented before any reliance decisions are made.
- The audit strategy table on page 2 should align with the line items and risk assessments in [[2-working-papers/12-20]].
- Obtain the written agreement (Q26) before internal auditors begin any direct assistance work.
- All internal audit staff providing assistance must sign the declaration on page 3.
- Consider whether reliance on internal audit for higher-risk areas is appropriate — ISA 610.30 cautions against this.

## How-To Document Summary

The How-To V5 draft for 12.21 Using Internal Audit explains the functionality and features of this working paper within Probe Audit Premium+. It is a questionnaire and checklist-type document for documenting the planned use of the internal audit function.

### Key Topics Addressed
- **Document placement** — located in the Audit planning folder in CaseWare document manager
- **Audit plan section** — Yes/No questions with Description, Risk, and Ref columns evaluating whether the work and direct assistance of the internal audit function can be used; requires evaluation of objectivity, competence, and systematic approach
- **Audit strategy section** — table mapping relevant balances and transactions to internal audit involvement with columns for: Use work of internal auditor, Reperform work, Direct assistance, Reason, Risk, and Ref
- **Conclusion** — confirms considerations addressed when determining the audit plan (judgment, assessed risk, objectivity, competence, sufficient involvement)
- **Planning memorandum section** — documents discussions with internal audit regarding planned use and written agreements obtained
- **Declarations** — internal audit staff declarations and signature table (name, role, qualification, industry experience, signature, date) confirming attendance at planning meeting, objectivity threats reporting, and confidentiality undertaking
- **Document outcomes** — mandatory completion of audit plan, audit strategy for each balance/transaction, conclusion, planning memorandum discussions, and internal audit staff declarations
- **Group audits** — additional questions for Group Audit Single/Multiple engagement types
- **Available features** — insert/delete comments, insert/delete rows, record risks/events, Guidance, Freeze

- **ISA 610.30** — Considerations for direct assistance (significant judgments, higher assessed risks)
