# Engagement Quality Review - Planning (Audit)

## Purpose

The Engagement Quality Review - Planning working paper (12.40) documents the engagement quality review performed over the planning phase of the audit. It provides a structured framework for the EQ Reviewer to evaluate the significant judgements made by the engagement team during planning, verify independence, and conclude on whether the review provides a sufficient basis for an objective evaluation. The document is 3 pages.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Engagement Quality Review Team (Page 1)

- **Q1:** Does the EQ Reviewer plan to use assistants to perform and complete the EQR?

### EQ Reviewer Declaration (Page 1)

A formal declaration confirming no circumstances that may impair eligibility, independence, or objectivity. Considerations include:
- Previous involvement with significant judgments made by the engagement team
- Close or immediate family member of the engagement partner or team member
- Close personal relationships with team members
- Engagement partner as an aggressive or dominant individual (actual or perceived pressure)
- Direct reporting line to the engagement partner
- Service as EQ Reviewer for a partner who acts as EQ Reviewer on the reviewer's own engagement

**Table columns:** Name, Qualification, Role, Experience as EQ Reviewer (years), Industry experience (years), Engagement experience (years), Engagement experience (role), Comment, Signature, Date

### Firm Policies or Procedures (Page 1)

- **Q2:** Did you inspect the firm's SoQM policies and procedures related to:
  - (a) Ethical requirements for EQ Reviewers (objectivity, independence)
  - (b) Overall responsibility of the EQ Reviewer for EQR performance
  - (c) Responsibilities to perform procedures at appropriate points in time
  - (d) Circumstances where engagement team discussions threaten EQ Reviewer objectivity, and appropriate actions
  - (e) Appropriate action when circumstances impair EQ Reviewer eligibility

### Eligibility as EQ Reviewer (Page 2)

- **Q3:** Are there no laws/regulations affecting eligibility of appointment?
- **Q4:** Are there no circumstances impairing eligibility to perform the EQR?

### Gathering Information (Page 2)

- **Q5:** Did you read and understand information communicated by the engagement team regarding the nature and circumstances of the engagement and entity?
- **Q6:** Based on understanding of engagement and entity, did you identify significant judgments and significant matters to discuss with the engagement partner?
- **Q7:** Did you read and understand information from the firm's monitoring and remediation process?
- **Q8:** Based on monitoring/remediation understanding, did you identify any deficiencies relating to significant judgments?
- **Q9:** Did you discuss with the engagement partner:
  - (a) Significant judgments made in planning
  - (b) Significant matters identified in planning
  - (c) Basis for determination that ethical requirements relating to independence have been fulfilled
- **Q10:** Did you discuss significant judgments and matters with other engagement team members where appropriate?

### Significant Judgments and Matters (Pages 2-3)

- **Q11:** Did you review selected documentation relating to significant judgments in planning?
- **Q12:** Based on evaluation of audit evidence:
  - (a) Is the basis for significant judgments appropriate?
  - (b) Is an appropriate level of professional skepticism applied?
  - (c) Does documentation support conclusions reached?
  - (d) Are conclusions reached appropriate?
- **Q13:** Is the basis for the engagement partner's determination on ethical requirements/independence appropriate?

### Conclusion (Page 3)

- **Q14:** Will it likely be perceived that the EQ Reviewer is not making decisions on behalf of the engagement team (based on frequency of discussions)?
- **Q15:** Do you anticipate that planned procedures will provide a sufficient basis for an objective evaluation? Considerations:
  - Identified deficiencies and remedial actions from monitoring
  - Complexity of the engagement
  - Nature and size of entity
  - Reasons for quality risk assessments
  - Results of external authority inspections
  - Concerns about engagement team work quality
  - Acceptance and continuance information
  - Co-operation of engagement team with EQ Reviewer

Ends with: EQ Reviewer comment, name, signature, and date.

## ISA References

- ISQM 2 — Engagement Quality Reviews
- ISA 220 — Quality Management for an Audit of Financial Statements (EQ review requirements)

## Completion Guidance

- This working paper is completed by the EQ Reviewer, not the engagement team. The engagement team should provide all necessary information and documentation access.
- The EQ Reviewer must be independent of the engagement team — verify eligibility (Q3-4) and complete the declaration before commencing the review.
- The gathering information section (Q5-10) should be completed through a combination of document review and discussions with the engagement partner and team.
- Q12 is the core evaluation — each sub-question requires careful consideration of the planning documentation.
- Q14 addresses the perception of objectivity — if discussions have been too frequent or directive, the EQ Reviewer may appear to be making decisions rather than reviewing them.
- A separate EQR working paper exists for the completion/reporting phase of the audit; this one covers planning only.
- Cross-reference to [[2-working-papers/4-audit-planning/12-22]] for EQ Reviewer appointment and eligibility documentation.

## How-To Document Summary

The How-To V5 draft for 12.40 Engagement Quality Review - Planning (Audit) explains the functionality and features of this working paper within Probe Audit Premium+. It is a questionnaire and checklist-type document for documenting the engagement quality review performed during the planning phase.

### Key Topics Addressed
- **Document placement** — located in the Audit planning folder in CaseWare document manager
- **Engagement quality review team** — documents whether the EQ Reviewer plans to use assistants
- **EQ Reviewer declaration** — formal declaration confirming no circumstances impairing eligibility, independence, or objectivity; signature table with name, qualification, role, EQ Reviewer experience, industry experience, engagement experience, comment, signature, and date
- **Firm policies or procedures** — inspection of the firm's SoQM policies related to EQ reviews (ethical requirements, overall responsibility, timing, objectivity threats, eligibility impairment)
- **Eligibility as EQ Reviewer** — confirmation that no laws, regulations, or circumstances impair eligibility
- **Gathering information** — documents information obtained about the engagement and entity, identification of significant judgments and matters, monitoring/remediation process review, and discussions held with the engagement partner and team
- **Significant judgments and matters** — review of selected documentation, evaluation of whether the basis for significant judgments is appropriate, professional skepticism is applied, documentation supports conclusions, and conclusions are appropriate; evaluation of the engagement partner's determination on ethical requirements and independence
- **Firm mandatory procedures** — based on events and conditions present
- **Conclusion** — confirms whether planned procedures provide a sufficient basis for objective evaluation, considering monitoring deficiencies, engagement complexity, entity nature/size, quality risk assessments, external inspection results, and engagement team co-operation
- **EQ Reviewer sign-off** — comment, name, signature, and date
- **Document outcomes** — mandatory completion of all sections by the EQ Reviewer (not the engagement team)
- **Group audits** — additional questions for Group Audit Single/Multiple engagement types
- **Available features** — insert/delete comments, insert/delete rows, Guidance, Freeze

### ISA References in the How-To
- **ISQM 2** — Engagement Quality Reviews (early independent oversight, planning-stage quality review)
- **ISA 220 (Revised)** — Quality Management for an Audit of Financial Statements (EQ review requirements)
