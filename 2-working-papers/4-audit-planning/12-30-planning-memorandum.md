# Planning Memorandum

## Purpose

The Planning Memorandum (12.30) is the narrative backbone of the audit planning phase. This 7-page document consolidates entity information, the audit strategy, a summary audit plan by line item, planning meeting notes, other significant discussions, and the engagement partner's declaration. It provides the context and rationale behind the audit approach documented in [[2-working-papers/4-audit-planning/12-20-audit-plan-and-strategy]].

## Key Fields & Sections

### Entity Information (Page 1)
Questions 1-8, each with Detail, Risk, and Ref. columns:

1. **Nature of the business**
2. **Brief history** — date of incorporation, changes in activities/locations, changes in ownership/management, significant acquisitions/disposals, other significant events
3. **Specific business risks** — main risks, insurance cover, succession planning
4. **Significant events in the current year**
5. **Known related party relationships** — pulls from 11.20 Inherent Risk Assessment
6. **Details of key contacts** — names and titles
7. **Laws and regulations with direct effect** on material amounts and disclosures
8. **Other laws and regulations** that may have a material effect

### Audit Strategy (Pages 1-2)
Questions 9-12:

9. **Characteristics and scope** — financial reporting framework, industry-specific requirements, components/locations, group relationships, specialised knowledge needs, internal auditors, service organisations, IT effects
10. **Reporting objectives** — client's reporting timetable, planned discussions (ISA 260.12), expected communications among team members, communications with third parties
11. **Results of preliminary engagement activities** — knowledge from other engagements
12. **Resources** — engagement team selection (including EQ reviewer), assignment of work, engagement budgeting

### Audit Plan Summary (Pages 2-3)

A summary table for all 23 financial statement line items showing:
- Assessed risk of material misstatement by assertion (C, EO, RO, AVA, CO, PD)
- Sign. Risk (significant risk indicator)
- Resp. (response)
- Test of control required
- Required procedures
- Extended analytical procedures
- Extended test of detail
- Substantive sampling

Additional plan items (Q36-39):
- **Q36:** Use of Computer Assisted Audit Techniques (CAATs)
- **Q37:** Accounting work necessary to produce auditable records
- **Q38:** Analytical procedures, inspection, observation, and enquiries as risk assessment procedures
- **Q39:** How, when, and to what extent engagement team work will be supervised and reviewed

### Planning Meeting Notes (Pages 3-5)
Questions 40-55:

- **Q40:** Engagement team members who attended
- **Q41:** Expected behaviour — responsibilities for quality, professional ethics, open communication, co-operation with EQ Reviewers
- **Q42:** Problems that may arise during the audit
- **Q43:** Conditions impeding professional skepticism and unconscious/conscious auditor biases
- **Q44:** Specific areas requiring particular professional skepticism (contradictory information, document reliability, management responses, fraud/error indicators, audit evidence assessment)
- **Q45:** Developments and changes since last audit
- **Q46:** Assigning work to team members considering complexity, skepticism, judgment, and SoQM policies; indicators of fraud risk
- **Q47:** Susceptibility of financial statements to material misstatement
- **Q48:** Susceptibility to fraud — omission/obscuring of disclosures, management obscuring understanding of disclosures
- **Q49:** Related party susceptibility to fraud and error
- **Q50:** External interests in the financial statements
- **Q51:** SoQM policies and ethical considerations
- **Q52:** Specific accounting standards, interpretations, practice statements — new/revised disclosures, changes in framework or entity
- **Q53:** Findings of prior year's review or quality reviews
- **Q54:** Materiality — overall materiality and performance materiality
- **Q55:** Matters to communicate to team members not in attendance

### Other Significant Discussions (Page 5)

- **Q56:** Communication to directors about planned scope, timing, significant risks. Consider: date, parties, planned use of internal audit, extent of direct assistance.

### Engagement Partner Declaration (Pages 5-7)
Questions 57-73:

- **Q57-59:** Continuance — nothing to decline, understanding of monitoring/remediation, relevance to audit plan
- **Q60-65:** Ethical requirements — understanding, threat identification, review of audit plan (ref 12.20), team awareness of ethical requirements, threats to compliance from team
- **Q66-67:** Resources — technological/intellectual resources sufficient (ref 12.22), team competence/capabilities/time
- **Q68:** Team informed of expected behaviour
- **Q69:** Direction, supervision, and review — nature/timing/extent considering SoQM, engagement nature, and resources (ref 12.30)
- **Q70-71:** Review of audit documentation for significant judgments
- **Q72:** Discussion of financial reporting framework and susceptibility to misstatement (ref 12.30)
- **Q73:** Determination of matters to communicate to absent team members (ref 12.30)

Ends with: Firm mandatory items, engagement partner name, signature, and date.

## Completion Guidance

- This document is best completed after the risk assessment (section 11) and the audit plan and strategy ([[2-working-papers/4-audit-planning/12-20-audit-plan-and-strategy]]) are substantially done, as it draws on those outputs.
- The entity information section (Q1-8) should be reviewed and updated each year — do not simply roll forward prior year information.
- The planning meeting notes (Q40-55) should be documented contemporaneously during or immediately after the planning meeting.
- The engagement partner declaration (Q57-73) is a critical sign-off — the partner must personally review and sign, not delegate this.
- Cross-references to other working papers should be filled in (e.g., 12.20 for audit plan review, 12.22 for resources, 12.30 self-references for supervision and team discussion).
- Materiality figures (Q54) should cross-reference to the materiality working paper.

## How-To Document Summary

The How-To V5 draft for 12.30 Planning Memorandum explains the functionality and features of this working paper within Probe Audit Premium+. It is a questionnaire and checklist-type document consolidating entity information, the audit strategy, the audit plan, planning meeting discussions, and the engagement partner declaration.

### Key Topics Addressed
- **Document placement** — located in the Audit planning folder in CaseWare document manager
- **Entity information** — Detail, Risk, and Ref columns documenting the nature of business, history, specific business risks, significant events, related parties, key contacts, and applicable laws and regulations
- **Audit strategy** — documents characteristics and scope of the engagement, reporting objectives, results of preliminary engagement activities, and resources necessary to perform the engagement
- **Audit plan** — summary table showing SCOTABD auto-populated from 11.20, assessed risk of material misstatement by assertion (C, EO, RO, AVA, CO, PD) auto-populated from risk assessment, and audit strategy selections (significant risk response, test of controls, required procedures, extended analytical procedures, extended tests of detail, substantive sampling) that tailor the engagement file
- **Planning meeting notes** — documents engagement team discussions including expected behaviour, problems, professional skepticism, susceptibility to misstatement and fraud, materiality, and matters for absent team members
- **Other significant discussions** — communication to directors about planned scope, timing, and significant risks
- **Engagement partner declaration** — Yes/No questions with cross-references and detail columns covering continuance, ethical requirements, independence, resources, team competence, direction/supervision/review, and financial reporting framework compliance
- **Engagement partner sign-off** — comment, name, signature, and date
- **Document outcomes** — mandatory completion of all sections; audit plan SCOTABD and risk levels auto-populate from 11.20; audit strategy selections tailor the engagement file
- **Group audits** — additional questions for Group Audit Single/Multiple engagement types
- **Available features** — insert/delete comments, insert/delete rows, record risks/events, Guidance, Freeze

### ISA References in the How-To
- **ISA 300** — Planning an Audit of Financial Statements (overall audit strategy, scope, timing, direction)
- **ISA 220 (Revised)** — Quality Management for an Audit of Financial Statements (engagement partner responsibilities)
