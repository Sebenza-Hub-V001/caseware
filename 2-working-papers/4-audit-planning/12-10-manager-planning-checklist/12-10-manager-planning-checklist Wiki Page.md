# Manager Planning Checklist

## Purpose

The Manager Planning Checklist (12.10) is a single-page quality control document used by audit managers to verify that all critical planning requirements have been addressed before fieldwork begins. It provides a structured Yes/No checklist covering the key ISA requirements applicable to the planning phase of the audit, ensuring nothing is overlooked.

## Key Fields & Sections

The document is structured as a table with columns for:
- **Audit Procedures** — the question to be addressed
- **Yes / No** — manager's confirmation
- **Performed by** — who carried out the procedure
- **Ref.** — cross-reference to supporting working papers

The checklist contains 19 questions organised by ISA standard, covering:

1. **ISA 210 (Q1-3):** Terms of engagement — preconditions for audit, written engagement letter, communication of responsibilities, scope, and timing
2. **ISA 220 (Q4-6):** Quality management — ethical requirements awareness, resource sufficiency and appropriateness, team competence/capabilities/time
3. **ISA 240 (Q7-8):** Fraud — assessment of fraud risk, design of responses to assessed fraud risks
4. **ISA 250 (Q9):** Laws and regulations — review of audit programs for compliance evidence
5. **ISA 300 (Q10):** Planning — audit planned effectively
6. **ISA 315 (Q11-14):** Understanding the client — knowledge of entity and environment, risk identification and assessment at financial statement and assertion levels, determination of material classes of transactions/account balances/disclosures (COTABD) not determined as significant (SCOTABD), IT environment complexity assessment
7. **ISA 320 (Q15):** Materiality — appropriate application of materiality in planning
8. **ISA 330 (Q16-17):** Responding to assessed risks — appropriateness of assertions to be tested, design of responses to assessed risks
9. **ISA 550 (Q18):** Related parties — understanding of related party relationships for fraud risk recognition
10. **ISA 620 (Q19):** Auditor's experts — consideration of whether to use an auditor's expert

## Completion Guidance

- This checklist should be completed by the audit manager after all planning working papers have been prepared.
- Each question requires a Yes/No response; any "No" answers should be investigated and resolved before proceeding.
- The "Ref." column should cross-reference to the specific working paper that provides evidence for each answer (e.g., 12.20 for the audit plan and strategy, 11.20 for risk assessment).
- The document includes fields for "Planning by", "Reviewed by", "Performed by", and "Final review" sign-offs.
- Pay particular attention to Q13 regarding the distinction between material COTABD and SCOTABD, as this drives the audit approach for each area.
- Q14 regarding IT complexity is important as it determines whether specialised IT skills are needed.
