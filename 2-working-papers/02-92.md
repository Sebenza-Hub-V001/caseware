# Letter of Representation

## Purpose

The Letter of Representation (02.92) is a formal written representation from management (typically the financial director) to the auditor, confirming key assertions about the financial statements, the information provided during the audit, and management's responsibilities. It is required by ISA 580 as audit evidence and addresses matters that are fundamental to the audit but cannot be independently verified through other procedures alone.

## Key Fields & Sections

The letter is addressed to the engagement partner and covers 20 sections of representations:

### 1. Financial Statements
- Management has fulfilled its responsibilities for preparing financial statements per IFRS for SMEs
- Financial statements fairly present the state of affairs, results and cash flows
- Compliance with the Companies Act, 2008

### 2. Information Provided
- (a) Access to all relevant information (records, documentation, other matters)
- (b) Additional information requested for the audit provided
- (c) Unrestricted access to persons within the entity
- All transactions recorded and reflected in financial statements

### 3. Laws and Regulations
- All known instances of non-compliance disclosed (both financial statement effect and operational)

### 4. Litigations and Claims
- Estimated effects of pending/threatened litigation properly recorded or disclosed
- No additional claims known beyond those disclosed

### 5. Related Parties
- Identity of all related parties disclosed
- All related party relationships and transactions disclosed
- Appropriately accounted for and disclosed per IFRS for SMEs

### 6. Going Concern
- All information relevant to going concern assessment disclosed

### 7. Subsequent Events
- All post-balance-sheet events requiring adjustment or disclosure have been appropriately treated per IFRS for SMEs

### 8. Fraud
- Definition of fraud acknowledged (fraudulent financial reporting and misappropriation of assets)
- Responsibility for internal controls to prevent and detect fraud acknowledged
- Results of management's fraud risk assessment disclosed
- All information on fraud or suspected fraud disclosed involving:
  - (a) Management
  - (b) Employees with significant roles in internal control
  - (c) Others where fraud could have material effect
- All allegations of fraud communicated by employees, former employees, analysts, regulators, or others disclosed

### 9. Uncorrected Misstatements
- Effects are immaterial individually and in aggregate
- List of uncorrected misstatements attached

### 10. Comparatives
- No significant matters requiring restatement of comparatives

### 11. Current Assets
- Current assets have realisable value at least equal to carrying amounts
- Receivables represent valid claims with appropriate impairment provisions

### 12. Property, Plant and Equipment
- Carrying values arrived at after considering:
  - (a) Capital expenditure additions
  - (b) Write-off of sold/scrapped items
  - (c) Depreciation/amortisation to estimated residual values

### 13. Impairment
- Carrying amounts reviewed for indicators of impairment; adjustments recorded where necessary

### 14. Financial Instruments
- All financial assets and liabilities correctly classified per IFRS for SMEs
- No inappropriate offsetting of financial assets and liabilities

### 15. Financial Institutions
- All financial institution accounts dealt with in financial statements
- All necessary disclosures from banking relationships made

### 16. Income Tax
- Deferred tax valuation determined per IFRS for SMEs including estimation of future taxable income
- Deferred tax recognised for all deductible temporary differences as required

### 17. Contractual Agreements
- Compliance with all material contractual agreements
- All contractual arrangements properly reflected and disclosed where material

### 18. Encumbrances
- Satisfactory title to all owned assets
- No undisclosed liens or encumbrances

### 19. Commitments
- No material undisclosed commitments (capital expenditure, forward contracts, authorised but not contracted)

### 20. Financial Statement Disclosure
- Proper recording and disclosure of:
  - (a) Losses from sale/purchase commitments
  - (b) Buy-back agreements
  - (c) Assets pledged as collateral
  - (d) Off-balance-sheet activities
  - (e) Common ownership/management control relationships
  - (f) Changes in accounting principles
  - (g) Share repurchase options and agreements
- No undisclosed:
  - (h) Other contingencies or liabilities (including environmental, illegal acts, human rights)
  - (i) Other environmental matters with material impact

## Completion Guidance

- This is a 3-page letter signed by the financial director on behalf of management
- The date of the letter must be as near as practicable to, but not after, the date of the auditor's report
- The engagement date in section 1 must be completed (date of the engagement letter)
- The letter must be on the entity's letterhead or in formal letter format addressed to the auditor
- All 20 sections are standard; additional entity-specific representations may be added
- If management refuses to provide the representation letter, this has serious implications for the audit opinion (ISA 580.19-20)
- The list of uncorrected misstatements referenced in section 9 should be attached as an appendix

### Key Topics Addressed

- **Document placement** -- located in the Client communication folder in CaseWare document manager (note: same as 02.90, in Client communication rather than Finalisation)

  4. Litigations and claims (estimated effects, no additional claims)
  5. Related parties (identity, relationships, transactions, disclosure)
  6. Going concern (disclosure of all relevant information)
  7. Subsequent events (adjustment or disclosure per IFRS for SMEs)
  8. Fraud (internal controls, risk assessment, all fraud information disclosed)
  9. Uncorrected misstatements (immateriality confirmation, list attached)
  10. Comparatives (no restatement required)
  11. Current assets (realisable value, valid receivables)
  12. Property, plant and equipment (carrying values, additions, write-offs, depreciation)
  13. Impairment (carrying amount review, adjustments)
  14. Financial instruments (classification, no inappropriate offsetting)
  15. Financial institutions (all accounts dealt with, disclosures made)
  16. Income tax (deferred tax valuation per IFRS for SMEs)
  17. Contractual agreements (compliance, proper reflection)
