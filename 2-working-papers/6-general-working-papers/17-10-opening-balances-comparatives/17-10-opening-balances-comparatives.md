# Opening Balances and Comparatives Work Program

## Purpose

The Opening Balances and Comparatives Work Program (17.10) provides audit procedures for verifying that opening balances are correctly brought forward from the prior period, that comparative figures in the current year's financial statements are accurate, and that any prior period errors or reclassifications are properly accounted for under the IFRS for SMEs Accounting Standard. The document is 2 pages.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Risk Assessment (Page 1)

- Financial statement level risk indicators:
  - Inherent risk
  - Control risk
  - Fraud risk
  - Overall materiality
  - Performance materiality

### Audit Procedures (Page 1)

- **Procedure 1:** Obtain a schedule of opening balances and test them (ISA 510.6):
  - (a) Agree opening retained income and other reserves in the general ledger to closing retained income and reserves per prior period's financial statements
  - (b) Agree to prior year financial statements (ISA 510.6(a) and ISA 710.7(a))
  - (c) Agree to comparative figures in the current year's financial statements (SME3.14)
- **Procedure 2:** If comparatives may be misstated:
  - (a) Perform additional audit procedures to confirm whether a material misstatement exists
  - (b) Discuss the matter with management
  - (c) Determine whether previous financial statements need amendment
  - (d) Enquire how management intends to address the matter in the financial statements
  - (e) If prior financial statements are amended, carry out appropriate procedures to ensure the amendment is correct, review steps taken to prevent reliance on the financial statements, and amend the audit report if necessary
- **Procedure 3:** Where presentation or classification has changed from the previous period, ensure comparatives are reclassified unless impracticable (SME3.11-13)
- **Procedure 4:** Confirm that prior period errors, if any, were appropriately accounted for per IFRS for SMEs (SME10.19-23)
- **Procedure 5:** If restatement of prior period financial statements due to error was necessary, review draft financial statements to ensure disclosure per IFRS for SMEs (SME10.23)

### Conclusion (Page 2)

Standard conclusion section with ten questions covering:
1. Material change in accounting policy due to new standard/interpretation (SME10.8)
2. Material change in accounting policy due to change providing more relevant/reliable information (SME10.8)
3. Change in accounting estimates (SME10.17)
4. Prior period errors
5. Subsequent events
6. Going concern indicators
7. Key audit matters for communication to directors and audit report
8. Suspected non-compliance with laws and regulations (NOCLAR)
9. Suspected irregularities reportable under Section 45 of the Auditing Profession Act, 2005
10. Whether remaining balance of populations after tests of detail is material

## Completion Guidance

- Procedure 1 is the core procedure — it establishes the three-way agreement between the general ledger opening balances, prior year financial statements, and current year comparative figures. All three must reconcile.
- This working paper is particularly important for initial audit engagements where the auditor has no prior year file to rely on. ISA 510 specifically governs this scenario.
- Procedure 2 provides a structured escalation path when misstatements in comparatives are suspected — from additional procedures through to management discussion and potential amendment of prior year financials.
- Procedures 4-5 address prior period error restatements. If errors are identified, the auditor must confirm that the entity follows the retrospective restatement requirements of IFRS for SMEs Section 10.19-23 and that adequate disclosure is made.
- The conclusion section is standardised across all execution working papers. Question 4 (prior period errors) is particularly relevant to this working paper — a "Yes" answer here should cross-reference directly to the procedures above.
