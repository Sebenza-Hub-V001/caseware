# Financial Statement Preparation Work Program

## Purpose

The Financial Statement Preparation Work Program (18.10) is the most comprehensive execution-phase working paper, providing audit procedures to verify that the entity's financial statements are prepared correctly and in full compliance with the IFRS for SMEs Accounting Standard and the Companies Act, 2008. It covers the entire financial statement package: statement of financial position, comprehensive income, changes in equity, cash flows, notes, accounting policies, director's report, and overall presentation. The document is 6 pages.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Risk Assessment (Page 1)

- Financial statement level risk indicators:
  - Inherent risk
  - Control risk
  - Fraud risk
  - Overall materiality
  - Performance materiality

### Audit Procedures — General (Pages 1-2)

- **Procedure 1:** Agree or reconcile financial statements to the trial balance and document in the audit file (ISA 330.20 and .30)
- **Procedure 2:** Inspect the following for indications of unidentified related party relationships or transactions (ISA 550.16):
  - (a) Bank and legal confirmations
  - (b) Minutes of meetings
  - (c) Income tax returns
  - (d) Information supplied to regulatory authorities
  - (e) Shareholders' registers
  - (f) Statements of conflicts of interest from management and directors
  - (g) Records of the client's investments and those of its pension plans
  - (h) Contracts and agreements with key management and directors
  - (i) Significant contracts and agreements not in the ordinary course of business
  - (j) Specific invoices and correspondence from the client's professional advisors
  - (k) Life insurance policies acquired
  - (l) Significant contracts renegotiated
  - (m) Such other records or documents as considered necessary
- **Procedure 3:** When previously unidentified related parties or transactions are found (ISA 550.22):
  - (a) Request management to identify all transactions with the related parties and enquire why controls failed
  - (b) Reconsider risk that other related parties or transactions may exist
  - (c) Consider implications if non-disclosure appears intentional (fraud risk)
- **Procedure 4:** Confirm financial statements were prepared within six months after year-end (Companies Act, 2008, section 30)

### Audit Procedures — Financial Statement Preparation (Pages 1-2)

- **Procedure 5:** Confirm financial statements are prepared:
  - (a) By an independent accounting professional per Regulation 26(1)(d) of the Companies Regulations, 2011
  - (b) On the basis of financial records provided by the company
  - (c) In accordance with the IFRS for SMEs Accounting Standard
- **Procedure 6:** Confirm by inspection that financial statements:
  - (a) Comply with IFRS for SMEs in form and content
  - (b) Present fairly the state of affairs and business
  - (c) Show assets, liabilities, equity, income, expenses, and prescribed information
  - (d) Set out publication date and applicable accounting period
  - (e) Disclose on first page whether audited, independently reviewed, or neither
  - (f) Disclose name and professional designation of preparer on first page
  - (g) Are not false or misleading in any material respect
  - (h) Are not incomplete in any material particular (Companies Act, 2008, section 29)
  - (i) Include an auditor's report
  - (j) Include a director's report covering state of affairs, business, and profit/loss
  - (k) Will be approved by the board and signed by an authorised director
- **Procedure 7:** Identify material, significant, and unusual journal entries relating to the financial statement closing process (ISA 240.32 and ISA 330.20):
  - (a) Confirm rationale for journal entries
  - (b) Examine supporting evidence
  - (c) Review accounting entries for compliance with IFRS for SMEs

### Audit Procedures — Statement Review (Pages 2-3)

- **Procedure 8:** Review draft financial statements and confirm all required statements are included (IFRS for SMEs):
  - (a) Statement of financial position (SME3.17(a))
  - (b) Single statement of comprehensive income, or separate income statement and statement of comprehensive income (SME3.17(b))
  - (c) Statement of changes in equity (SME3.17(c)) — or combined statement of income and retained earnings if only simple equity changes occurred (SME3.18)
  - (d) Statement of cash flows (SME3.17(d))
  - (e) Notes with summary of significant accounting policies and other explanatory information (SME3.17(e))
- **Procedure 9:** Confirm additional entity information is disclosed:
  - (a) Name of entity (SME3.23(a))
  - (b) Whether individual entity or group (SME3.23(b))
  - (c) Reporting period dates (SME3.23(c))
  - (d) Presentation currency (SME3.23(d))
  - (e) Level of rounding (SME3.23(e))
  - (f) Domicile, legal form, country of incorporation, and registered office address (SME3.24(a))
  - (g) Description of operations and principal activities (SME3.24(b))
  - (h) Date financial statements were authorised for issue and who gave authorisation (SME32.9)
  - (i) Details about non-adjusting events after reporting period (SME32.10)

### Audit Procedures — Director's Report (Page 3)

- **Procedure 10:** Cross-reference statements made by the director to appropriate working papers
- **Procedure 11:** Cross-reference all financial information in the report to appropriate leadsheets or working papers
- **Procedure 12:** Cross-reference details of the director to relevant working papers
- **Procedure 13:** Review report ensuring no statements, financial information, or disclosures contradict audit evidence

### Audit Procedures — Statement of Financial Position (Page 3)

- **Procedure 14:** Agree all material items to audit working papers
- **Procedure 15:** Agree all items to notes, if applicable
- **Procedure 16:** Confirm minimum IFRS for SMEs information is disclosed (SME4.2 and 4.3)
- **Procedure 17:** Confirm current and non-current assets are presented separately, except when liquidity-based presentation is more relevant (SME4.4, 4.5-4.6)
- **Procedure 18:** Confirm current and non-current liabilities are presented separately, except when liquidity-based presentation is more relevant (SME4.4, 4.7-4.8)
- **Procedure 19:** Confirm required IFRS for SMEs information is disclosed in the statement or notes (SME4.11-14)

### Audit Procedures — Statement of Comprehensive Income (Page 3)

- **Procedure 20:** Agree all material items to audit working papers
- **Procedure 21:** Agree all items to notes, if applicable
- **Procedure 22:** Confirm total comprehensive income is presented per IFRS for SMEs requirements (SME5.2-5.10)
- **Procedure 23:** Confirm expenses are analysed by nature or function per IFRS for SMEs (SME5.11)

### Audit Procedures — Statement of Changes in Equity / Income and Retained Earnings (Pages 3-4)

- **Procedure 24:** Agree all material items to audit working papers
- **Procedure 25:** Agree all items to notes, if applicable
- **Procedure 26:** Confirm minimum information for statement of changes in equity is disclosed (SME6.2 and 6.3)
- **Procedure 27:** Confirm minimum information for statement of income and retained earnings is disclosed (SME6.4 and 6.5)
- **Procedure 28:** Confirm compliance if only simple equity changes occurred (SME3.18, SME6.4-5)

### Audit Procedures — Accounting Policies (Page 4)

- **Procedure 29:** Agree all material items to audit working papers
- **Procedure 30:** Confirm measurement basis is disclosed in summary of accounting policies (SME8.5(a))
- **Procedure 31:** Confirm all relevant accounting policies are disclosed (SME8.5(b))
- **Procedure 32:** Confirm accounting policies are selected and applied consistently (SME10.7)
- **Procedure 33:** Confirm changes in accounting policies are only made if allowed by IFRS for SMEs and correctly accounted for (SME10.8, SME10.11-14)
- **Procedure 34:** Confirm financial statements adequately describe IFRS for SMEs as the reporting framework (ISA 700.15 and SME3.3-7)
- **Procedure 35:** Confirm financial statements are only described as complying with IFRS for SMEs if they comply with all requirements

### Audit Procedures — Notes (Page 4)

- **Procedure 36:** Review notes to confirm:
  - (a) All material items agreed to audit working papers
  - (b) Required IFRS for SMEs information is disclosed (SME8.2-4)
  - (c) Judgements, key assumptions, and other key sources of estimation uncertainty are disclosed (SME8.6 and 8.7)
- **Procedure 37:** Reperform casts and calculations for mathematical accuracy

### Audit Procedures — Financial Statement Presentation (Pages 4-5)

- **Procedure 38:** Confirm the following IFRS for SMEs considerations are observed:
  - (a) Fair presentation (SME3.2)
  - (b) Compliance with IFRS for SMEs (SME3.3-3.7)
  - (c) Going concern (SME3.8-3.9)
  - (d) Frequency of reporting (SME3.10)
  - (e) Consistency of presentation (SME3.11-13) — refer to section 74
  - (f) Comparative information (SME3.14) — refer to section 74
  - (g) Materiality and aggregation (SME3.15-16)
  - (h) Accrual basis of accounting (SME2.36)
  - (i) Offsetting (SME2.52)
  - (j) Notes and explanations are not used to rectify inappropriate accounting policies
  - (k) Departure from IFRS for SMEs only occurs as allowed by SME3.4 and is disclosed per SME3.5

### Conclusion (Page 6)

Standard conclusion section with ten questions covering:
1. Material change in accounting policy due to new standard/interpretation (SME10.8)
2. Material change in accounting policy due to change providing more relevant/reliable information (SME10.8)
3. Change in accounting estimates (SME10.17)
4. Prior period errors
5. Subsequent events
6. Going concern indicators
7. Key audit matters for communication to directors and audit report
8. Suspected non-compliance with laws and regulations (NOCLAR)
9. Suspected irregularities reportable under Section 45 of the Auditing Profession Act, 2005
10. Whether remaining balance of populations after tests of detail is material

## Completion Guidance

- This is the largest execution working paper and serves as the final check before the audit report is issued. It should be completed after all substantive testing working papers are finalised.
- Procedure 1 is the starting point — the financial statements must agree to the trial balance. This reconciliation underpins all subsequent procedures.
- Procedure 2 is a comprehensive related party sweep — many of the items listed (bank confirmations, minutes, tax returns) will already have been inspected elsewhere, so cross-referencing is key to avoid duplication.
- Procedures 5-6 are Companies Act compliance checks specific to South African requirements. Procedure 6(e) and 6(f) are first-page disclosures often missed.
- Procedures 8-9 and 14-37 systematically walk through each financial statement component. Work through these in order, agreeing each item to audit working papers and confirming IFRS for SMEs disclosure requirements.
- Procedure 38 addresses overarching presentation principles — this is where the auditor steps back and evaluates the financial statements as a whole.
- The director's report section (Procedures 10-13) requires cross-referencing every statement and figure in the report to audit evidence. Any contradiction must be resolved before sign-off.
