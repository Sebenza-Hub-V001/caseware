# Discussions with Those Charged with Governance

## Purpose

This working paper is a structured questionnaire used to document discussions with those charged with governance (directors) during the planning phase of the audit. It captures the interviewee's responses across six key areas, with columns for the response, auditor's response, and risk assessment. The document supports risk assessment procedures and understanding of the entity.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Header / Details of Interviewee
- Client name
- Period end
- Prepared by and date
- Interviewee position and name
- Completion method (electronic or other)
- Optional signature space for interviewee

### General Section
- Business risks identified in the current period affecting financial reporting
- New contracts entered into (including lease agreements)
- Changes to terms and conditions of contracts (finance/debt, lease, customer/supplier)
- Changes in specific laws and regulations affecting financial statements
- Related party transactions and relationships (identification, nature, transactions, balances)
- Controls over related parties (identification, authorisation, approval of significant transactions)
- Changes to business activities or operations (objectives, strategies, products/services, locations, customers, suppliers, industry, entity structure)
- Changes in key personnel and accounting/financial reporting team
- Assessment of risk of material misstatement due to non-compliance with laws and regulations
- Known or suspected non-compliance with laws and regulations
- Communications received from regulatory agencies
- Findings and recommendations on control deficiencies affecting financial reporting
- Management actions in response to control deficiency findings
- Risk assessment of entity being used for money laundering or terrorist financing
- Policies and procedures for identification and reporting of suspicious/unusual transactions
- Conditions rendering a director ineligible (Companies Act, 2008, section 69)
- Personal financial interests in transactions with the company

### Going Concern Section
- Ability to continue as a going concern (twelve-month period, events/conditions casting doubt, how addressed)

### Subsequent Events Section
- Significant events after the financial statement date affecting the financial statements

### Fraud Section
- Assessment of risk of material misstatement due to fraud (nature, extent, frequency)
- Actual, suspected or alleged fraud affecting the entity
- Incentives to commit fraud (pressure on results, performance-based compensation, financial targets, lifestyle changes)
- Opportunities to commit fraud (high-risk balances, control overrides, non-standard transactions)
- Rationalisation of fraudulent or unethical behaviour (staff turnover, strained relationships, management ethics)
- Responsibilities regarding preventing, detecting and resolving fraud
- Specific internal audit procedures to prevent, detect and resolve fraud

### IT Environment Section
- Risks arising from use of IT affecting financial reporting (data integrity, IT changes, software updates, IT strategy alignment)
- Nature and extent of changes to the IT environment during the period (system changes, new systems, data conversions, database changes)
- Configurations made to accounting software and integrated IT applications

### Accounting Section
- Changes in significant accounting policies (standards changes or operational changes)
- Changes to accounting estimates or need to revise estimates
- Significant, unusual or complex transactions, events or matters affecting financial statements and disclosures

### Response Columns (for each question)
- Response (from interviewee)
- Auditor's Response (auditor's assessment and follow-up)
- Risk (risk indicator)

## Completion Guidance

- This questionnaire should be completed during the planning phase, ideally before or alongside [[working-papers/10-50]]
- The interviewee should be a director or member of the board (Those Charged with Governance as defined in [[working-papers/10-20]])
- Can be completed electronically; the interviewee's responses are recorded in the Response column
- The auditor must document their own assessment in the Auditor's Response column
- Risk indicators should be flagged for items that feed into [[working-papers/11-10]] and [[working-papers/11-20]]
- Fraud section responses feed directly into the fraud risk assessment at [[working-papers/11-25]]
- IT environment responses inform [[working-papers/10-55]]
