# Partner Sign-Off

## Purpose

The Partner Sign-Off (02.00) is the engagement partner's comprehensive review and approval document for the entire audit engagement. It consolidates the status and conclusions of every audit section -- from financial statement level risks through to individual assertion-level work programs -- into a single sign-off document. The partner uses this to confirm they have reviewed all key areas, are satisfied with the work performed, and approve the audit opinion before the auditor's report is issued.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Summary at Financial Statement Level
- Inherent risk at financial statement level (assessment status)
- Control risk at financial statement level (assessment status)
- Fraud risk at financial statement level (assessment status)

### Fraud Risks Requiring Special Attention
- **Management override of controls** (ISA 240.31) -- includes response procedures: understanding journal entry controls (ref 11.50), testing journal entries (ref 16.10), and considering additional testing
- **Fraud in revenue recognition** (ISA 240.26) -- requires identification of revenue streams and tailored procedures

### Financial Statement Level Work Programs
Each section includes: work program progress (total, complete, exceptions), conclusion, sign-off fields (planning by, reviewed by, performed by, final review), and partner comment. Sections covered:
- Accounting records (16.10)
- Opening balances and comparatives (17.10)
- Financial statement preparation (18.10)
- Laws and regulations (20.10)

### Summary at Assertion Level
For each account balance / class of transactions, the document presents:
- Assertion-level columns: C (Completeness), EO (Existence/Occurrence), RO (Rights & Obligations), AVA (Accuracy/Valuation/Allocation), CO (Cut-off), PD (Presentation & Disclosure)
- Inherent risk assessment per assertion
- Risk of material misstatement per assertion
- Assertions selected for testing
- Work program progress and conclusion
- Estimated audit risk remaining vs desired audit risk

Account sections covered include:
- Property, plant and equipment (305.10)
- Investment property on the cost model (311.10)
- Prepayments (331.10)
- Inventories (405.10)
- Cash and cash equivalents (420.10)
- Trade and other receivables
- Loans to directors, managers and employees
- Trade and other payables
- Bank overdraft
- Finance lease liabilities
- Other financial liabilities
- Retained income
- Revenue streams (1, 2, 3)
- Operating expenses
- Operating gains / losses
- Finance costs
- Statement of cash flows
- Related parties

### Engagement Partner Declaration
- Confirmation of compliance with ethical requirements and independence
- Review of SoQM-related policies and procedures

### Engagement Quality Review (EQR) Questions
- Review of significant judgments and conclusions
- Review of draft auditor's report, including key audit matters
- Confirmation that EQR procedures were completed before dating the report
- Review of formal written communications to management/directors

## ISA References

- **ISA 240.31** -- Management override of controls as a fraud risk requiring special attention
- **ISA 240.26** -- Presumption of fraud risk in revenue recognition
- **ISA 220** -- Quality management for an audit of financial statements (engagement partner responsibilities)
- **ISA 701** -- Key audit matters in the independent auditor's report

## Completion Guidance

- This is the final document completed in the engagement -- only after all other working papers are finalised
- The partner must review every section's work program progress, ensuring all items show as complete with exceptions resolved
- Fraud risk responses must be documented with specific procedure references
- The engagement partner declaration at the end must be signed and dated
- If an EQR is required, the EQR-related questions in the latter pages must be completed by the engagement quality reviewer before the partner dates the auditor's report
- All sign-off fields (Planning by, Reviewed by, Performed by, Final review) should be populated throughout
