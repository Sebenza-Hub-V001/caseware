# Going Concern Conclusion

## Purpose

The Going Concern Conclusion (02.20) is a single-page document where the engagement team evaluates whether any events or conditions cast significant doubt on the entity's ability to continue as a going concern. It draws together evidence from the planning phase (management's initial assessment) and the finalisation phase (including the final analytical review) to reach a definitive conclusion on the going concern basis of accounting.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Considerations (with Event column)
The auditor must consider whether management's initial going concern assessment includes all relevant information gathered during the audit, specifically:
- (a) Fixed-term borrowings approaching maturity without realistic prospects of renewal or repayment
- (b) Indications of withdrawal of financial support by creditors
- (c) Inability to pay creditors on due dates or comply with loan agreement terms
- (d) Non-compliance with capital or other statutory requirements

### Final Analytical Review Cross-Check
- Consider whether results of the final analytical review (02.30) indicate any cash flow or liquidity concerns

### Going Concern Conclusion
- Conclude whether any event or condition was identified that may cast significant doubt on the entity's ability to continue as a going concern
- Standard conclusion: "No doubt exists relating to the appropriateness of the going concern concept"

### Audit Opinion Impact
- Statement on whether the opinion needs modification with respect to going concern
- Standard: "We do not need to modify our opinion with respect to the applicability of the going concern concept"

### Partner Sign-Off
- Partner confirms review of going concern procedures and evidence from both planning and finalisation
- Confirms sufficient and appropriate audit evidence supports the conclusion and the auditor's report

## ISA References

- **ISA 570** -- Going Concern
- **ISA 570.23** -- Communication with those charged with governance regarding going concern events/conditions

## Completion Guidance

- This document is completed after the final analytical review (02.30) has been performed
- The considerations listed are indicative, not exhaustive -- the auditor should apply professional judgment
- Must cross-reference to the planning-phase going concern assessment and any subsequent events work (02.10)
- If doubt exists, the conclusion section must describe the nature of the uncertainty and its effect on the audit opinion
- The partner must personally sign off on this working paper, confirming review of all going concern evidence
