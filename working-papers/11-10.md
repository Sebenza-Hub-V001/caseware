# Risk Analysis Summary

## Purpose
This working paper provides a consolidated summary view of all risk assessments across the audit engagement. It aggregates inherent risk, control risk, and fraud risk assessments at both the financial statement level and the assertion level for every material financial statement line item. It serves as the central reference point that ties together the outputs of working papers 11.20, 11.25, 11.30, and 11.60.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Risk at Financial Statement Level
- Inherent risk at financial statement level (from [[working-papers/11-20]])
- Control risk at financial statement level (from [[working-papers/11-30]])
- Fraud risk at financial statement level (from [[working-papers/11-25]])
- Performance materiality

### Risk at Assertion Level
For each financial statement line item, the document presents a matrix of:
- **Assertion columns:** C (Completeness), EO (Existence/Occurrence), RO (Rights & Obligations), AVA (Accuracy, Valuation & Allocation), CO (Classification & Occurrence), PD (Presentation & Disclosure)
- **Inherent risk at assertion level** per assertion
- **Risk of Material Misstatement (ROMM)** per assertion — rated as Low, Medium, or High with a numeric indicator
- **Specific materiality** amount for the section

### Financial Statement Line Items Covered
**Assets:**
- Property, plant and equipment
- Investment property on the cost model
- Prepayments
- Inventories
- Cash and cash equivalents
- Trade and other receivables
- Loans to directors, managers and employees

**Liabilities:**
- Other financial liabilities
- Finance lease liabilities
- Bank overdraft
- Trade and other payables
- Current tax payable

**Income Statement:**
- Revenue
- Cost of sales
- Operating gains / losses
- Operating expenses
- Finance costs

**Other:**
- Taxation
- Capital or contributions
- Retained income
- Related parties
- Commitments and contingencies
- Statement of cash flows

## ISA References
- The document implements the risk assessment requirements of ISA 315 (Revised) by summarising assessed risks at both the financial statement and assertion levels.
- The assertion categories (C, EO, RO, AVA, CO, PD) align with ISA 315's classification of assertions for classes of transactions, account balances, and presentation/disclosure.

## Completion Guidance
- This is a **summary document** — it is populated automatically from the assessments made in the underlying working papers (11.20, 11.25, 11.30, 11.60).
- The risk ratings should flow through from the detailed assessments; ensure they are consistent.
- Where assertions show "N/A", this indicates the assertion is not relevant for that line item (e.g., Rights & Obligations is N/A for income statement items; only PD applies to related parties, commitments, and cash flows).
- ROMM defaults to Medium (1) where no inherent risk factors have been identified — this reflects the baseline risk before considering controls.
- Specific materiality should be set per section where a lower threshold than overall materiality is warranted.
- The header includes sign-off fields: Planning by, Reviewed by, Performed by, and Final review.
