# Subordination Agreement Work Program

## Purpose

The Subordination Agreement Work Program (02.22) provides audit procedures for verifying the validity, enforceability, and adequacy of subordination agreements or letters of comfort/support that an entity has concluded with its creditors. These agreements are critical because they directly impact the entity's solvency position and the appropriateness of the going concern basis of accounting.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Audit Procedures (with Exceptions, Performed by, and Ref columns)

1. **Compile creditor list** -- List all creditors with subordination agreements or letters of comfort/support, showing outstanding balances at year-end
2. **Reasons for subordination** -- Obtain reasons by enquiry; consider influence on other audit areas and the audit report (e.g. going concern)
3. **Validity of agreements** -- Confirm by inspection that agreements/letters:
   - (a) Are in writing in appropriate format
   - (b) Are signed by the creditor or authorised representative
   - (c) Are accepted by the entity
4. **Creditor's legal entitlement** -- Determine by written enquiry whether the creditor is legally entitled to subordinate the debt (e.g. loan may have been offered as security)
5. **Creditor's ability and intention** -- Ensure creditors are able and intend to honour and comply with the agreement terms
6. **Sufficiency of subordinated amount** -- Consider whether the subordinated amount is sufficient to restore the entity to commercial solvency
7. **Creditor's factual solvency** -- Assess whether the creditor's assets exceed liabilities after subordination; consider whether subordination could lead to disposition without value
8. **Clause adherence** -- Confirm compliance with clauses regarding:
   - (a) Interest raised on the subordinated claim
   - (b) Amounts paid to the particular creditor
   - (c) Amendments to the agreement
9. **Third-party acceptance** -- Determine whether any creditors have accepted the benefit of the agreement (strengthens enforceability as it cannot be simply reversed)
10. **Agreement still in effect** -- Ensure the agreement/letter is still in effect at the audit report date; review asset/liability position clauses
11. **Non-resident creditor checks** -- If creditor is not South African, ensure:
    - (a) Creditor is validly existing under its domicile laws with authority to enter the agreement
    - (b) Agreement is duly authorised and enforceable under domicile laws
    - (c) All necessary governmental consents and approvals have been obtained
12. **Copy on file** -- Ensure a copy of the agreement/letter is on the audit file
13. **Disclosure** -- Confirm adequate disclosure in notes to the annual financial statements

### Conclusion
Three key conclusion questions (Yes/No):
- Did results indicate non-compliance with a subordination agreement?
- Did results indicate the company is trading without reasonable expectation of meeting liabilities (potentially causing financial loss)?
- Did results indicate the agreement did not justify adoption of a going concern basis of accounting?

## ISA References

- **ISA 570** -- Going Concern (subordination agreements as evidence supporting going concern)
- **Companies Act 71 of 2008** -- Trading while insolvent provisions

## Completion Guidance

- This working paper is only relevant when the entity has subordination agreements in place -- it may be marked as not applicable if none exist
- The going concern implications (procedure 2 and the conclusion questions) are the most critical aspect
- For non-South African creditors, additional legal verification steps are required (procedure 11)
- The conclusion questions are framed negatively -- a "Yes" answer to any triggers further investigation and potential audit opinion implications
- Ensure the agreement copy is retained on file (procedure 12) for documentation purposes
