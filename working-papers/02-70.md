# Manager's Final Checklist

## Purpose

The Manager's Final Checklist (02.70) is a 42-question checklist organised by ISA standard that the audit manager completes to confirm that the audit has been conducted in compliance with all relevant ISAs. Unlike the Senior's Final Checklist (which focuses on procedural completeness and documentation quality), the Manager's Checklist takes a higher-level, standards-based view of audit quality and sufficiency.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Considerations (Yes/No, Performed by, Ref)

Organised by ISA standard:

**ISA 200: Overall Objectives (1-3)**
1. Reasonable assurance obtained that financial statements are free from material misstatement
2. Report and communications reflect audit findings
3. No need to disclaim opinion or withdraw from engagement

**ISA 220: Quality Management (4-7)**
4. No potential breaches of ethical requirements by engagement team
5. Relevant ethical requirements and independence fulfilled
6. No difficult or contentious matters requiring consultation
7. No differences of opinion within the engagement team

**ISA 230: Audit Documentation (8-9)**
8. Audit documentation provides sufficient record of basis for report
9. Documentation evidences compliance with ISAs and legal requirements

**ISA 240: Fraud (10)**
10. Appropriate response to fraud or suspected fraud

**ISA 250: Laws and Regulations (11-13)**
11. Sufficient evidence regarding compliance with laws having direct financial statement effect
12. Reviews of correspondence and discussions to identify non-compliance
13. Appropriate response to non-compliance or suspected non-compliance

**ISA 260: Communication with Those Charged with Governance (14-16)**
14. All required information obtained from directors
15. Timely observations provided to directors regarding financial reporting oversight
16. Effective two-way communication between auditor and directors

**ISA 265: Communicating Control Deficiencies (17)**
17. Internal control deficiencies communicated to directors and management

**ISA 450: Evaluation of Misstatements (18-19)**
18. Effect of all identified misstatements evaluated (corrected and uncorrected)
19. Effect of uncorrected misstatements on financial statements evaluated

**ISA 500 and 501: Audit Evidence (20-22)**
20. Audit procedures designed to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence
21. Sufficient evidence regarding inventory existence and condition
22. Sufficient evidence regarding completeness of litigation and claims

**ISA 505: External Confirmations (23)**
23. External confirmation procedures appropriately designed and executed

**ISA 520: Analytical Procedures (24-25)**
24. Substantive analytical procedures provided relevant and reliable evidence
25. End-of-audit analytical procedures performed for overall consistency

**ISA 530: Audit Sampling (26)**
26. Audit sampling provided reasonable basis for conclusions

**ISA 540: Estimates (27)**
27. Accounting estimates and fair value estimates are reasonable

**ISA 550: Related Parties (28-29)**
28. Financial statements fairly presented regarding related party relationships
29. Sufficient evidence on identification, accounting, and disclosure of related parties

**ISA 560: Subsequent Events (30-31)**
30. Sufficient evidence that post-balance-sheet events are appropriately reflected
31. Appropriate response to facts becoming known after report date

**ISA 570: Going Concern (32-33)**
32. Sufficient evidence regarding appropriateness of going concern basis
33. Conclusion on material uncertainty and implications for report

**ISA 580: Written Representations (34-35)**
34. Written representations obtained from management and directors
35. Written representations obtained as required by ISAs

**ISA 700: Forming an Opinion (36)**
36. Opinion clearly reflects conclusions from audit evidence obtained

**ISA 701: Key Audit Matters (37)**
37. Key audit matters determined and communicated in auditor's report

**ISA 705: Modifications to the Opinion (38)**
38. Modified opinion clearly expressed if required

**ISA 706: Emphasis of Matter / Other Matter (39)**
39. Emphasis of Matter or Other Matter paragraph included if relevant

**ISA 710: Comparative Information (40)**
40. Comparative information presented in accordance with IFRS for SMEs

**ISA 720: Other Information (41-42)**
41. Material inconsistencies between other information and financial statements considered
42. Appropriate response to material inconsistencies if identified

## ISA References

Every major ISA applicable to a financial statement audit is referenced:
- ISA 200, 220, 230, 240, 250, 260, 265, 450, 500, 501, 505, 520, 530, 540, 550, 560, 570, 580, 700, 701, 705, 706, 710, 720

## Completion Guidance

- This checklist is completed after the Senior's Final Checklist (02.60) and before the Partner Sign-Off (02.00)
- Each question maps directly to a specific ISA -- use the ISA reference to guide the expected standard of evidence
- The questions are designed to be answerable with Yes/No but the Ref column should cross-reference to the relevant working papers that support each answer
- A "No" answer to any question requires explanation and may trigger additional procedures or affect the audit opinion
- This is a 3-page document covering 42 questions across 22 ISA standards
