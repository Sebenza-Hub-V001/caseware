# Use of Resources

## Purpose

The Use of Resources working paper (12.22) documents the engagement team composition and the resources assigned to the audit engagement. It addresses ISA 220 requirements regarding human resources, technological resources, intellectual resources, and the appointment and eligibility of the Engagement Quality (EQ) Reviewer. The document is 3 pages.

## Key Fields & Sections

### Engagement Team Members — Firm Personnel (Page 1)

A declaration table where each team member confirms:
1. Responsibility for contributing to quality management and achievement on the engagement, including:
   - Adhering to professional ethics, values, and attitudes
   - Engaging in open and robust communication with team members
2. Exercise of appropriate professional skepticism throughout the engagement
3. Understanding of relevant ethical requirements, including:
   - Identifying, evaluating, and addressing threats to compliance (including independence)
   - Circumstances that may cause a breach
   - Responsibilities when becoming aware of possible breaches
   - Responsibilities regarding non-compliance with laws and regulations by the entity
4. Independence from the client, taking into account the firm's SoQM policies and procedures

**Table columns:** Name, Role, Qualification, Industry experience (years), Engagement experience (years), Comment, Signature, Date

### Technological Resources (Page 1)

- **Q5:** Are you planning to use technological tools, including an IT application, to obtain audit evidence?

### Intellectual Resources (Page 1)

- **Q6:** Based on the nature and circumstances of the engagement, are intellectual resources other than those provided by the firm's audit methodology and tools required? (Consider: industry-specific methodology)

### EQ Reviewers (Pages 1-3)

Detailed questions regarding the Engagement Quality Reviewer:

- **Q7:** Was the EQ Reviewer appointed by the individual(s) assigned responsibility per firm policies? Consider whether engagement team members are allowed to appoint the EQ Reviewer.
- **Q8:** Eligibility of EQ Reviewer and assistants:
  - (a) Not members of the engagement team
  - (b) Compliance with ethical requirements (objectivity, independence)
  - (c) Compliance with laws/regulations prescribing additional eligibility requirements
  - (d) Appropriate authority to fulfill EQR responsibilities
  - (e) Appropriate competence, capabilities, and time

  Considerations include: conditions requiring EQR, knowledge of responsibilities, understanding of firm policies, professional standards knowledge, entity/industry knowledge, experience with similar engagements, engagement team composition, specialised expertise, and monitoring results.

- **Q9:** Does the EQ Reviewer meet all eligibility criteria per firm's SoQM policies?
- **Q10:** For external EQ Reviewers — are service provider requirements adhered to?
- **Q11:** Was the cooling-off period adhered to, particularly when the EQ Reviewer was previously the engagement partner?

## ISA References

- ISA 220 — Quality Management for an Audit of Financial Statements (team competence, resources, EQ review)
- ISQM 1 — Quality Management for Firms (SoQM policies and procedures)
- ISQM 2 — Engagement Quality Reviews

## Completion Guidance

- Every engagement team member must sign the declaration on page 1 before commencing work.
- The team composition should provide sufficient competence and capacity for the engagement's complexity.
- Technological resources (Q5) should be considered in light of the IT environment assessment — if CAATs or data analytics are planned, document them here.
- Intellectual resources (Q6) address whether additional methodology or guidance is needed beyond the firm's standard Probe methodology.
- The EQ Reviewer section (Q7-11) is only required where an Engagement Quality Review is mandated (e.g., listed entities, public interest entities, or per firm policy). If not required, document why.
- Pay particular attention to Q11 on cooling-off periods — this is a common independence issue when partners rotate.
