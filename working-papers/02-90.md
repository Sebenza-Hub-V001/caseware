# Report to Management and Those Charged with Governance

## Purpose

The Report to Management and Those Charged with Governance (02.90) is a formal written communication from the auditor to the entity's management and directors. It addresses all matters that the auditor is required or considers appropriate to communicate as a result of the audit, including findings on laws and regulations, significant audit matters, independence, related parties, going concern, internal control deficiencies, and key audit matters.

## Key Fields & Sections

### 1. Laws and Regulations
- Description of any identified or suspected instances of non-compliance, particularly those believed to be intentional and/or material

### 2. Significant Findings from Our Engagement
Covers multiple ISA 260.16 requirements:
- Views about significant qualitative aspects of accounting practices (policies, estimates, disclosures)
- Significant difficulties encountered during the audit (e.g. unavailability of information, inability to obtain evidence, management restrictions)
- Significant matters discussed or subject to correspondence with management
- Written representations requested from management
- Other significant matters relevant to oversight of the financial reporting process
- Uncorrected misstatements: effect individually and in aggregate on the opinion; identification of material uncorrected misstatements with request to correct (ISA 450.12)
- Effect of uncorrected misstatements related to prior periods (ISA 450.13)
- Circumstances affecting the form and content of the auditor's report (modifications, emphasis of matter, going concern uncertainty, KAMs) (ISA 260.16)

### 3. Auditor's Independence
- Communication of relevant ethical requirements, including independence requirements specific to certain entity types (ISA 260.16A)

### 4. Related Parties
- Significant matters arising during the audit in connection with the entity's related parties

### 5. Going Concern
- Events or conditions identified that may cast significant doubt on the entity's ability to continue as a going concern (ISA 570.23)
- Whether the events/conditions constitute a material uncertainty
- Whether the going concern basis of accounting is appropriate
- Adequacy of related disclosures
- If opinion modification expected: circumstances and proposed wording

### 6. Deficiencies in Internal Control
- Reported on accompanying pages
- Discussed with management (name to be inserted) with comments noted
- Includes a disclaimer that the audit was not designed to express an opinion on internal control effectiveness
- Notes that more extensive procedures might have identified additional deficiencies

### 7. Potential Key Audit Matters
- Identification of potential KAMs to be reported in the auditor's report
- Standard: "No potential key audit matters were identified"

### Closing
- Statement that the report is provided for the outlined purposes only and may not be suitable for other purposes
- Offer of assistance in implementing recommendations

## ISA References

- **ISA 260.16** -- Matters to communicate with those charged with governance (multiple sub-requirements)
- **ISA 260.16A** -- Communication about independence

- **ISA 450.13** -- Effect of prior period uncorrected misstatements
- **ISA 550** -- Related Parties
- **ISA 570.23** -- Going concern communication
- **ISA 701** -- Key Audit Matters

## Completion Guidance

- This is a letter-format document (3 pages) addressed to the appropriate person at the entity
- The addressee name must be inserted at the top of the letter
- Section 6 (internal control deficiencies) references "accompanying pages" -- additional detail pages may be attached
- The management discussion on internal control deficiencies must reference the specific person by name
- All sections should be completed even if the finding is "nil" -- this evidences that the matter was considered
- The letter should be issued before or at the same time as the auditor's report
- This document represents the formal ISA 260 communication
